While I only worked with SF for a short while, I think the relevant point is this from FV: "If at any stage you find that a task is no longer relevant, then delete it."

The forcing function of always starting your chain with the first unactioned task causes you to engage with every item on the list and make a decision about it. if one is truly overcommitted, you will be made aware of that fact and you can decide what to do about it. After iterating over the list for several cycles, my experience was that even 6 pages wasn't a burden for me and I got a lot of things done and off of the list and the things I added helped me do my work better.

Could you swamp it, probably, but then you know are in the process of or have overcommitted and you need to make the appropriate adjustments in life, not just to a list.
