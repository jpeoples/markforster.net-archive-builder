When I start a new preselection, sometimes it happens that I read the top task and feel like I want to do it straitght away. In AF terms, it "stands out"/"feels ready to be done".

In that case, is the FV best practice still to scan the list to see if anything may come up that I want to do before this task (even though I'm convinced there won't be), or can I trust my intuition and use this drive to just get the task out of the way? Are there any downsides to not scanning the full list here?

Lots of times I want to do the top task straightaway. Occasionally, I have done it mindlessly. No harm was done, but it is not the way I like to operate.

It takes me less than a minute to quickly scan the entire list. I feel that it is always worth that small investment in time to make sure I am not missing something.

Of course, by taking less than a minute, I am not carefully looking at each open item. I am just running my eyes quickly through the list.

I think that the more times I scan the list the better. It keeps me more conscious of the important and/or urgent items that are still open.
