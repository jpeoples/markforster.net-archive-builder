Hi All

Just thought I would state what notepad I am using and would be interested to hear what size/type/no of lines etc others are using! Also - advantages / disadvantages too

I had an A5 pad lying around and decided to try that - approximately 15cm by 21cm - it is actually ring-bound in the middle.

Advantages
Can see more that one page at a time - also can see pages together (spread out) or front and back
Ring binders help to flick through the pages easily

'top-flip' pads would be harder to use (as well as the pages getting snagged) - although you could write top-down on each page so that all the pages read the same way

Dave,

your idea is similar to my GTD-DIT-mixture system:

A4 notebook.
Left page dated carrying the entries for appointments and tasks to do on that day. Horizontal line under this, under this line the emergencies and same day tasks that come up during the day.
Right page: GTD-type next actions list: 'at work' top down, 'at home' bottom up, divided by a horizontal line

This system works fine as long I am disciplined enough to always leave at least one empty line at the bottom of the left page (for emergencies and same day tasks). You need to always have post-its or a small notepad at hand to take notes with this system.

3"x5" vertical format spiral wire binding. It opens flat so I can hold it in my hand if necessary. I get them from Sam's Club (U.S.) at 10 for about $5.00 so the price can't be beat. I got a fine point ball point pen that breaks in half so it just fits into the rings and stays with the pad. I have three plastic paperclips (I don't like metal because they tear paper) one for first active page, one for current page, one for last page. I get 15 lines per page and I found that doubling it by putting two items on a space (one near the top line and another near the bottom) I get 30 per page which does work better for me. I do write small and that fine point pen helps.

I flip the pages and book over to write on the back so I only work in one direction. I use the back of the book (last page toward first) to hold my Tickler items and Shopping lists, etc. I tear those out as they fill.

I also carry a 4"x6" vertical format wire spiral bound book for journaling. I put notes of phone calls, meetings, thoughts about this and that, etc. in that book. No special anything about it except that it is just a tad larger so I can write lots of text in it without feeling like I'm writing a list.

The whole thing fits right in my pocket.

I went shopping for a bound reporters book but found that I did not like them because they would not lie flat when opened as the spiral ones do and I don't like that cover flapping around.

I find that I'm still tweaking the system but backing out just as many tweaks as I'm putting in. It seems to work best when kept simple.
