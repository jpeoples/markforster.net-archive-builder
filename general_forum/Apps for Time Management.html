Hi Folks!

Excited to find a forum dedicated to the topic of time management.

As a physician entrepreneur, I face the challenge of managing my time day-to-day.

Especially as I've made a commitment to myself not to sacrifice my health on the way to becoming successful, my daily challenge consists of finding time to eat right, sleep well, and exercise, among other wellness priorities (i.e. meditation, yoga, spending time with family and friends).

In the process, I found it best to have a Visual and Fun way to plan my day, in a way designed to emphasize wellness goals alongside work and play.

The result is Owaves (http://www.owaves.com ). It's helping me tremendously, and it may help y'all as well.

Meanwhile, would love to learn what apps people find most useful. And if anyone has tried Owaves or heard of it before, any level of feedback or suggestions would be appreciated!

2014-09-22 3:27
There was something immediately appealing to me about the circular colorful design. The circle makes a lot of sense for the time period of the day (it allows you to show proportions betters, including sleep, and it lines up with the turning of the earth, so you can show where sunrise and sunset are, for example), and I have been thinking more about what times of day one does things that they want to do daily.

For example, I started tracking whether or not I exercised (walking ~40 minutes) each day (as seen on The X Effect subreddit here: http://www.reddit.com/r/theXeffect/ ). At first I would fail to do it if I worked too long on that day. But then I started walking right after work because I wanted to get outside before it got too dark, and enjoy the cooler weather and views of the sunset, even if I had worked late. And now it has become a habit to go on a walk every day after work, and even on the weekend I go outside when it is starting to get dark. (I wonder what will happen in the winter and Daylight Saving Time kicks in and sunset is at 5pm instead of 7pm).

Even though the tracking technique doesn't require you to use routines, I fell into one. Also some books I've read recently suggest that you create routines, such as a morning routine to get you energized and then work on something specific for an hour every morning (such as writing books, if you are an author).

I was going to try this program on my iPod touch or iPhone but it is iPad only. The UI looks like it could easily fit on a phone, so maybe that could be done without too high of a level of effort (compared to, say, the Android version).
