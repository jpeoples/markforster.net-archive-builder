Sometimes these lists lack vitality, aliveness and start to feel flat and uninspiring. All the love and inspiration has drained away. They need an infusion of love, attention and imagination.

I'm playing with a system by @Jupiter on another thread, with a tweak...

a new (dated) page each day
a "what's on my mind" list
a few things that stand out

and

a space for some doodling to float and drift in imagination, loosen up those 2 hemispheres by messing up those clean stark white pages
