Seraphim:

I think I would put it as high as MUST be actioned or accept forever after that you are a spineless wimp who has no right to be taking up valuable earth resources!

But seriously, if you start letting yourself out of completing Column 2, especially the unfinished tasks, you are in danger of destroying the whole motive power of SuperFocus.

Admittedly on a few occasions I have decided that an urgent task isn't quite as urgent as I first thought, and I have decided it would be more appropriate on a later page. But even that I would keep to the absolute minimum.

The only exception I would permit for unfinished tasks is if it's not physically possible to do something, e.g. because it's out of office hours. In that case you can cross it out and move it to the next page.

Reply by Mark Forster

Christian:

What I meant was that all tasks in Column 2 must be ACTIONED - not dismissed or deleted.

There are two exceptions:

1) When it's not physically possible to do a task (e.g. because of the wrong location or time of day). In that case you can cross the task out and re-enter it on the following page in Column 2. (Note that this exception does not apply to Column 1 tasks).

2) When you decide a urgent task is not as urgent as you first thought. In which case you can cross it out and enter it at the end of the list in Column 1.
