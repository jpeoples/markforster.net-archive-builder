No I didn't see that program - not sure it was ever screened here? They only screened one series of the US apprentice but the original here, with Alan Sugar, has just started it's latest series. I love that program! I can never quite believe some of the ridiculous things they do though - like not thinking to shut the doors on cars they were washing with a pressure hose.... !!!!

I've always had a lot of admiration for RB's approach. His motivation in setting up the VA airline, was the poor service he felt he had received as a passenger when flying - his motivation was to make his passengers feel valued and respected. I flew on one of his early flights when the plane was delayed through fog. We all received a letter advising of the problem, were kept regularly updated, and were given the option of providing details of someone we would like contacted. The friend who was meeting me was therefore telephoned, two or three times, and kept informed of the status of the flight. We were diverted into another airport and, rather than just arranging transport to the scheduled airport, (London Gatwick) were offered the option of transport to London Heathrow and Central London as well. Now THAT was customer service! I have to say that they don't go to those lengths now but they still win hands down over many of their competitors. Such an ethos has to come from the top.

I saw an interview with him a couple of weeks ago and success genuinely doesn't seem to have changed him.
