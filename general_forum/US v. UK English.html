I'd just like a bit of guidance on the use of words like "calendar" and "diary" in US English.

In UK English a "calendar" is something which you hang on your wall with the days of the month represented in a box shape. It often has a pretty picture for each month. There may be a small space provided to write events in, but it is the representation of the month in diagrammatic form that is the focus. Another use of the word is for things like the "church calendar" which shows when the major feasts of the year are.

In UK English the word "diary" has two main meanings:

1) A book in which you write about your life day by day. The most famous example is Samuel Pepys's diary.

2) A book in which you put all your appointments etc for the coming year, e.g. "unsold 2010 diaries are now selling at half-price". This is the sense I have been using "diary" in the instructions for DIT2/AF5.

Other words with similar meanings would include "journal" for diary sense 1 and "schedule" which is a detailed breakdown of a short period or particular subject, e.g. "my schedule for today" or "the conference schedule" or "my schedule of seminars".

How does US English use of these words compare? I'm asking so that the final version of the instructions is comprehensible to both sides of the pond (not to mention the rest of the world)!
