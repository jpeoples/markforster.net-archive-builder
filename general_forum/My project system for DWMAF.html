The rules below are how I ran my project system before I got into SuperFocus. It's less integrated, but works well with any AF system, including DWM.

1. Keep your AF list as you always have, and have a separate collection of project pages.
2. You may take any AF task and put it into a project page.
3. At any time you may insert "Work on Project X" into AF.
4. To work on Project X:
Get out the project, Define new tasks, work some tasks, or add project tasks to AF, by writing as "Task --> X". Put the page away when done.

5. Later when your work on Task --> X, in your AF list:
Work task as normal. When you finish Task, you may wish to grab another task from Project X, or add "Work on project X" to AF. Enter these as new tasks.

Hi all. Dear Alan, this system already exist and it's GTD. ;-)) :-))...

The question is only about the treatment of the task and in the philosophy of the 2 systems

1. about the treatment. Once you have your project you have tasks to do.
With GTD you choose the next actionable task and decides the options.
And you do things by contexts. ie &Call and so on...
With AF there are no context and no next action. All is grouped together so you can to do the by contexts or not as the tasks "stand at you".

2. The philosophy is different too. GTD is a precise methodology with a precise way of doing things and an exhaustive collect of everything. the master word of gtd could be CONTROL. AF is more an artisanal systems which try to let your brain manage your stuff on a focus area which doesn't mean an exhaustive collect. His master word could be FEEL...

Anyway, both system are good and efficient. For my part i now use GTD but when i am fed up of working by contexts and controlling things I use AF. I just created a new context call @Autofocus which is a kind of guideline of my today's tasks. And when things becomes really unmanageable i come back to gtd with a good midweek review. But i alway keep my weekly review and follow GTD rules.

Finally things are quiet simple. It depends on the amount of tasks and projects.
If you are like me with a mountain of things to do which oblige you to choose the best valuable task to succed then GTD is better. IF sometime things becomes quiet so you can use AF.

But the principle are the same. If you need projects then you need to separate projects from a list of things to do and make frequent come back from one to the other to do things and create next things to do.

Jupiter said about "project sytem" ...
<< this system already exist and it's GTD >>

GTD *separates* Projects from its NextActionTasks.
AF/DWM *mix* Projects, NextActionTasks and OtherTasks in one main list.

GTD *separates* Capture, Process, Do, Review into distinct steps.
AF/DWM *mix* Capture/Process/Do/Review into little&often steps in one main list.

GTS is *modal/rigid*
AF/DWM is "autofocus" ;-)

Before AF/DWM I was on GTD for about a year, and I tend to get lost in Process/Do steps, and missed doing Review step. In AF/DWM, the Review step gets done for each task as it's needed.
