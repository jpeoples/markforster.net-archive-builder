Gary,

A word of advice: Do NOT NOT NOT enter your entire backlog into your new system. Add a few tasks at a time. Best to start fresh, if you can. If not, just add maybe 5% - 10% of your backlog each day on the page one month from now.

Follow the system ... enter all tasks you enter on the page one month in the future.

No, it does not matter that when a page becomes near (less than one week) it has both one month old tasks and tasks that were just re-entered. It does not matter at all. You don't make any distinctions based on that ... you simply DO the task no matter what.

I think it's a mistake (i.e., not reading the mechanics of DWM closely enough) to look only at today, today+7, and today+1M, and ignoring everything else. DWM has you looking at EVERYTHING, from today all the way to today+1M, cycling through each group of tasks, taking action on the ones that "stand out". Each different expiration date puts a different level of pressure on you to get the task done -- each day they creep a little closer. Some people on this forum have called this the "about to go over the waterfall" effect or the "Space Invaders" effect.

This is much different than tasks surfacing at today+1M, and then, if you took no action, disappearing again for three weeks till they finally appear on today+7, and then disappearing again for 6 days till they appear on Today.

Of course you can modify and "break" the rules however you want, if you find it useful. I just wanted to make sure you were doing that deliberately, rather than simply misunderstanding Mark's instructions.
