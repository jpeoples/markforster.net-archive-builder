Is it DWM or is it me?

Whereas in AF1, I always felt comfortable listing anything that came to mind, whether a project, a 'next action' type task or just a vague idea to follow up later, I find that in DWM I am only writing down the more 'major' items - serious projects and ongoing responsibilities. It's as if I feel it isn't worth adding anything that will be finished in one go or not even done at all. But, as a result, I have started keeping other lists, for the day-to-day stuff and for odds and ends.

On the one hand, it may work quite well to separate ongoing projects from trivia, but, on the other, it was always something I liked about AF1 that I didn't have to separate stuff at the outset - everything went on one list and was dealt with, as appropriate, when it stood out.

I'm not sure why this has happened - whether I have created some kind of mental barrier to using DWM as simply as I did AF1 - or whether it's the forward-looking emphasis of DWM, that makes me feel it's only appropriate for clearly defined work tasks.

Has anyone else noticed this? I haven't been using DWM for long, so maybe I'm still adjusting to the new perspective, but at the moment I am feeling quite nostalgic for AF1! Should I go back to it? Or, if I stick with DWM, should I accept that it doesn't cover everything I need to remember and keep other lists as well or make more of an effort to write down everything, as I used to with AF1?

It's YOU! (LOL!)

With DIT, I would always write something down before I did it, even if it wasn't previously on my list for today. With AF1-4, I couldn't do that. They were too restrictive. With DWM, I am doing it again and it feels great. I am so much more self-aware. One of the beautiful things about DWM is that there is essentially random access. I can go to any item at any time. So, I will write down the smallest thing.

All I can say is that it's worth trying. But you need to make sure that you have a lot of space available on your pages.
