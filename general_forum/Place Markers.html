I had been dog-earing the first and last pages so I could find them quickly, and using the dot (and later the circled dot) to indicate the task I was working on.

But I have these plastic Post-it flags, and I got the idea of using them for these purposes.

The first active page is still dog-eared, because I only have to find that page about once a day, and I cut off the corners of completed pages, so it's no problem.

I got out my electric label-maker, and put "You are here" on one of the Post-it flags, to show the page that I am on. I put a label on both sides of the flag.

Another flag has "Write new tasks here," for the last page.

I also have some narrower, colorful flags that are like an "arrow" ~ I put the words "Task in progress" on some of those, and I can use them when I'm working on a task. This works better for me than a dot or a circled dot, because the dots look too much like symbols I already use, and they don't help the page in terms of visual clarity.

I've only used these for a day, but they seem to help keep me focused on the page & task I am on. It is helping more than I expected it would!
