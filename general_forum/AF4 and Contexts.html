I would like to revisit something that has been discussed before and that is coming to tasks on your list that you cannot do as you are in the wrong context. One of the powerful methods in GTD is the use of context lists. So, if I am at home, and I start working through my backlog component of my AF4 list, and I encounter tasks that I cannot do because I need the computer in my office to do them, then what does one do? Some have said that one should just ignore these tasks because I cannot do them and move on. I think Mark has said that if you find only tasks in the backlog section that cannot be done due to you needing to be somewhere else or needing some other tool, then mark these for dismissal and then you can rewrite them again in the active tasks portion of the AF4 list.

But this seems somewhat strange to me to dismiss things simply because I am not in the appropriate context. I could see me doing this every evening while working at home. I never had this problem with GTD because....well....I had context lists.

What are the thoughts of others on this? Mark, am I reporting correctly on your advice on this?

My advice to those who work in different places has always been to have a separate list for each location.

My advice has also always been that you shouldn't ignore the rules about dismissing, just because you can't do a task at a particular time.

Both these apply to all versions of AF.

Again my recommendation has always been that if you take work home with you you add it to your Home list. There's no reason why something shouldn't be on both lists. 2009-11-30 23:17
I do a lot of work-related tasks at home, and a lot of personal tasks at work. Therefore, I just keep one AF4 notebook, not two. But I also have separate personal and company email accounts, and separate physical In trays. So, on my single list, I append "@W" or "@H" to tasks where I might get confused; e.g., "Empty In tray @W." I don't think there's anything wrong with waiting to follow the dismissal rule of AF4, if you feel you haven't had enough turns at the closed list in both contexts. For example, if it's the weekend and you have quite a few at-work-only tasks, you might want to wait until Monday afternoon to put the remaining ones on notice. But in general I agree with Mark that it's better to just follow the rules and keep moving; having to consider whether to rewrite a task during frequent Reviews is good, in that it makes you ask yourself why it hasn't gotten done yet, and whether you really intend to do it at all!

Reply by Mark Forster

2009-12-1 22:27
Ubi:

<< I don't think there's anything wrong with waiting to follow the dismissal rule of AF4, if you feel you haven't had enough turns at the closed list in both contexts. >>

I'm not suggesting that you should change a method if it works for you, but I want to point out an additional effect of not dismissing items in AF4. That is that if you leave some items undismissed because it's the weekend, then you are presumably not closing the open list. The effect of this is that for a couple of days you are effectively working with only an open list, and are therefore entirely losing the Autofocus effect for the period in question.
