Eugene,

A lot of us on this forum experience that sort of thing and other issues - Alan B's pile of papers problem resonated with me, I'm doing a MASSIVE decluttering project at home and in my office at present.

Reading between the lines of many posts, I identify others who like me have procrastination problems and a desire for perfection (the two are probably interrelated, at least in my case).

When faced with getting going, I now deploy the "salami" technique which you can see on a recent thread of mine, A Slice of Action (I think). This gets one going and can be used for little and often circumstances.

When I face up to what has to be done, I start with 5 minutes on the kitchen timer, then reset it and add five minutes each reset, ie 5 + 10 + 15 etc. The last time I used this was last weekend and I found I was doing in excess of three hours in one session made up of the time resets.

Works for me.
