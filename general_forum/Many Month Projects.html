Ryan, you have hit *quite* the topic, which has generated many threads on this forum.

The short answer is: use whatever works best for the particular project, for your style and comfort level. You touched on several strategies above, and all of them can work.

For more detail, explore this forum. Project management was a hotter topic a few weeks ago, so page back a bit.

Here is one thread I found to get you started:
"Varying complexity of work"
http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1434887

Some general advice: most of us don't need as much project management as we think. Experiment on a project that isn't too critical but is somewhat complex. See how little overhead you can get away with. Can you just jot various details, ideas, subtasks, and options into C1 and let it churn? See how much you can trust SF. See whether SF tempts you to "get around to it" more than you otherwise would. (It probably will!)

Enjoy the process.
