I handle the groups of tasks in a somewhat disorganized manner. :-(

I add tasks directly to the list. When I find I have related tasks, I put a Post It note on my left page and give it a short title, e.g.., "Call Tony". As I come across tasks that are specificly related, I put a tag at the end of the line [Tony] which tells me there is a list of things for Tony and I add them to the Post It which might be on another page. I also do this if I add a new item to call Tony about.

So I have them grouped on the Post it which provides a visual of how big that group of tasks is getting. I also have individual entries that I come across frequently which serves as a repeated prompt to Call Tony.

If I forget that I really need a Post It it doesn't matter much. Usually when I am going to act on one of these lists on a Post It, I do a quick scan of my whole AF list to make sure I haven't missed anything.

This works well for tasks that must be done in a particular place; for shopping lists; or as a mini project list.

It isn't perfect, but it is easy. It is much easier to do than it is to write about :-)

Scanning the List

With AF4 I don't find the need to do a daily scan of the list, partly because I find the list stays quite small (approx 5 or 6 pages) so it is fairly normal for me to go through the whole thing (both closed and open) at least once per day. As a result I've usually got a pretty good idea of what's on the list. Occasionally if I'm up against a few urgent items I give myself permission to begin the day with a singly reverse scan of the Open List and then I switch back to normal AF4.

Contacts/Calls

For people I speak to a lot I maintain a dedicated Talk To list for each person and just put a "talk to " entry on my list. Otherwise, for the one off kind of contact, I just put a "contact about " on my list. I find this works really well. I always put a left hand margin on my list and use it for the next action code, for example my "contact ..." entry actually is "C :" where C is in the margin. This makes it easy to scan the whole list and pick out any contacts - sometimes I want to just deal with all my outgoing calls or emails and scan the list for these, afterwards going back to normal processing. This processing tweak still fits within the rules as I'm actually choosing not to do any other items as I scan through the list.

Hope that makes sense.
