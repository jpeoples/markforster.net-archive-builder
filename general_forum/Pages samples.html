I've been interested to read how the phrasing of the task entry can make a difference to the resistance to the task. Christine B pointed out the difference between 'Look for item X' and 'Find item X' - the latter offering more resistance because it seems to demand a positive outcome.

For most of my entries I take it a step further, perhaps, and don't even bother with a verb! Fortunately I only occasionally get caught out and think, "what did I mean there?". I also add abbreviations (yes, I can remember what they are!).

E.g.
...Email
Vitamin
NF
SS
Kitchen
Pilates
Washing
FOE
MF forum
Mum party
Mum cake
Suzanne
shears
Co-op
Millie Mon
AFI policies...

As is reasonably obvious, it's a home list. My work is largely self-contained and I keep that on separate pages (1.75 active pages at the mo).

A lot of my home tasks are recurrent - daily or near-daily. I'm hoping that they will become habitual soon so that I can avoid the inefficiency of entering the tasks every day. But I'll keep adding them until I can pretty much guarantee that I will still do them, even when I get home from shift work. I guess this is largely my goal for AF - get/ keep organised despite working intensive shifts.

Happy AFing!

The point of AF is to work, as much as possible, "little and often." Do you have the paint already purchased? Have the walls and moldings already been prepared? Do you have brushes, rollers, dropcloths, and mixers? (Pardon my American English. You might call some of these things by different names.) Do you want to do the actual painting all in one day?

I might make a list for things to buy. I'd then cross the item off my list, and add it again at the end of the list. If it was going to take 8 hours to do the actual painting, I'd put it on my calendar (outside the AF list.)
