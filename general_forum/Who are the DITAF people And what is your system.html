Hi Avrum
I don't use AF. I use a weekly version of DIT with GTD weekly review and some lists. My daily DIT is a combination of my normal habits/checklists and choosing today's work plucked from my weekly aims list that I contructed from my weekly review. The weekly list is comprised of obligations, projects timeline projections, my monthly goal practice and if I want to cull some things from the master list. I was trained to working out of a calendar so I still timebox some of my aims and piecemeal others to bring it forward. Example: Even though Friday has always been my Account Round-up day doesn't mean that I can't crank some of it out on the days previous to it (little and often to help make facing Accounts Day less onerous: I hate accounts work so I try to chip away it in bits. But, come Friday, I have to finish it. I have other round-up days such as weekly house clean, detail house clean, pick a project of my choice day, etc....It's easier for me to mindless rotate the stuff that doesn't change much from week to week or from day to day such as bundling work using my daily check list. The most important rule is my scut cap of 90minutes a day or 9 hours a week. This FORCES me to keep lousy work to an acceptable minimum. That frees up my mind for things I DO WANT TO DO! The only deviation from the 90minute scut cap is when I do more to free up a future day that week. Still, I'm not allowed to do more than 9 hours of scut in any given week unless it's totally out of my control. The suct cap forces me to choose my scut wisely. Example: I used to vacuum and mop my floors daily before I had a scut cap. Now I do it less often with a hardly noticable sacrifice. My house still looks and feels clean enough with doing it on a less frequent schedule. I've also pared down my accounts duties as I was over-doing the "cover my arse" tasks. The computer software helped tremendously with this. Bottom line: Scut work can take over much time if it isn't both systemized and carely culled to be effective. I'd rather use the time and effort toward things what I really feel passion for. (This includes pure leisure time as well.)
I discovered that much guilt is wasted and lots of busy work isn't really necessary. Plus, my system keeps me from avoiding work for too long. Those round-up days and scheduled timeboxes takes care of most of it.
It's much easier to implement than write about! If my damaged brain can work it, it can't be too complicated! LOL!
