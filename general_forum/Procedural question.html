A not-so-hypothetical question:

Today is February 5th.
I have 15 short stories that I must read by February 15th.

I enter "READ STORIES" in my calendar for March 5 (the 30 day mark).

Then I start working -- maybe for 30 minutes. When I'm done -- I re-enter "READ STORIES" in my calendar for February 12 -- the 7 day mark. And I work for another 30 minutes.

THEN WHAT DO I DO?

Do I cross out "Read Stories" from the 7 day list and Re-enter it on the 7 day list. and repeat?

AND should I have crossed off "Read Stories" from the 30 day list by this point? Or do I leave it there because it's still undone. (In which case, while cycling through my lists, I would come upon "READ STORIES" on both the 7 -day list and the 30 -day list).

Any guidance on this would be appreciated!

Steve,

Mark never said that DWM was the answer to every problem. As others have said, it can sometimes be beneficial to supplement a task management system, which DWM is, with a project management system.

If I anticipated that it was going to be hard to achieve the goal of reading all those short stories, I might, outside of DWM, create some kind of tracking system. Maybe I'd count up how many pages total I have to read. Then each time I read, I would know how many pages are left to read. Or, each time I finished a short story, I'd keep a running total of how many remain to be read..
