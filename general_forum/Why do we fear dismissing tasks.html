Something I've been pondering over recently is exactly what is it that drives us to fear dismissing tasks?

In the AF systems, it is that fear that keeps us moving back to those older tasks that we have been procrastinating over to try and "save them" from their ultimate fate (dismissal).

And yet those same tasks may have previously been sitting on our todo lists for days, weeks, months, even years, without any concern from us. But when it comes down to making a decision to let go, it suddenly becomes very difficult.

So where does this fear of letting go of these tasks come from?

We all seem to have it to some extent otherwise these systems wouldn't work. Any thoughts?

Frank, I think we fear dismissing because we translate it as we will *never* do that. eg. I will NEVER read Chapter 2 of 1984. And that seems intolerable.

I don't review dismissed tasks - I figure if they're important enough, they'll come up again. And I've probably dismissed more than anyone on this forum. I've come to enjoy it, it's part of the filtering - as it should be. The way I phrase it though is to tell myself "not right now, you have other, more important things to do. Doing this will take precious time away from those important things."

Those items that can easily be dismissed and are not are simply *clutter* - they're keeping us from doing what's more important. It's a sign that someone doesn't know what their priorities - or limits - are. Everything cannot be a priority, and we do not have infinite time.
