Mark T.

I have written about focusing on three goals and have personally found it very effective. Many years ago, I stumbled upon an article about the marine corp rule of three which discusses the idea of three goals or responsibilities being the optimal amount for a soldier to focus on. When the number increased to four effectiveness fell radically. This concept along with others I found or developed formed the basis of a goal system I use and now market called Desired Outcomes Implementation Technology. I found the way to accomplish lots of goals is to keep the list of goals small. This way you actually get them done and over time get many lists of three completed. This is more effective than listing say your top ten goals and not making progress on them or having it be very slow due to spreading yourself thin.

Additionally, I make a distinction between time management and goals. I have system for time management and then I time block and switch into goals mode. For instance I work during lunch and evenings on my personal goals using DO IT and during the morning and afternoon I am using time management to try to be an effective employee at the office. I have some excerpts of the ebook on my blog. Let me know if you have any questions.
