After a couple of months (has it been that long? I guess so...) with AF4R, I've decided to go back to DWM. The reason for this is purely a "feature" of my personality: I'm a starter, but not a finisher. I have GREAT ideas, I start implementing them and... Well, I can't count the number of half-finished craft projects that are upstairs in my sewing room. It's simply miraculous that my "unfinished" section only had 18 items on it when I shifted everything back to DWM this morning. I need the time pressure of the dated dismissal to keep me moving on all the things I start, or force me to really take a hard look at whether I really ought to be spending my time on that particular idea. I also never REALLY adjusted to not being able to dump anything that needs to be started in the next 30 days on page M and then know that it would stay active in my subconscious.

I'm a Toodledo user, actually (for DWM and also when I was trying AF4R). There are a couple of descriptions of Toodledo DWM implementations here:
http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1351338

And some discussion in a much larger thread about going digital starting about here:
http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1002402#post1002819

My basic implementation is to have two folders: active & dismissed. New tasks default to active with a due date one month in the future. I sort by due date and when I work on something I check it off if completed and clone it and reset the due date on the cloned task for one week in the future. Both the web and iPad implementations of Toodledo make this very easy - there's a shortcut for both one month (although you can create defaults for new tasks in the iPad version) and one week due dates. When I dismiss a task I just change it's folder so I can review it later.

There is a way to combine AF4R with the time-based dismissal process of DWM: Every morning I write the current date on the right hand side of the first unused line in each section. Thus for each task I know when it was created/rewritten. The time base-dismissal rule (parallel to the AF$ dismissal rule) is as follows:
BACKLOG/NEW: one month
PROJECTS: two weeks
RECURRING: one week
UNFINISHES: one week
