@Savitri,

If you're willing to, you could give what I do a try:

For each day just make a couple of brief lists in a small notebook.

On the right hand page put the things you really want/need to do that day, choosing one or two as MITs (most important tasks)

Then on the left hand page put the things you'd like to get done, or can do that day, but can wait if need be.

Then throughout the day tick off items as they get done, add new ones or delete unwanted ones on either list as you feel appropriate.

Also feel free to allocate new items up to a few days in the future, go back to to items on previous days that haven't been done yet, and go ahead to items on future days if you feel led to.

Something else I find that helps is to write grasscatcher/distraction items on the bottom of the left hand page instead of allowing them to interrupt me.

Hope this helps...
