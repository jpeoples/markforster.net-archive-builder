Hi Leon

Thanks for your comment and I totally understand your point. It is very clear that a lot of people work very well with the structure of a more defined system like DIT - which I wholeheartedly agree is a fantastic system. That structure would certainly enable "flow" - actually I hate that term, just not found anything else that is universally recognisable :-) - and particularly in a work environment (with other people around to motivate/interrupt or just add to "the list") I would definitely see that as an advantage. For me, working from home with little human interruption, the tendency to procrastinate is strong, and a structured system makes it worse. That is me - not the system.

The whole definition of "being in the flow" is, I believe, not so much related to the task/s at hand, or the ease or complexity of those tasks, but rather to the feeling that the "doing" of those tasks is happening easily, naturally, and in a way hard to define, enjoyably.

If I go back to an example I gave in an earlier post, that of my "stack/unstack the dishwasher" task, I had a real resistance to the task. It was not difficult. I had the ability to do it. It was not a big task. I could do it in a minute or two. The problem was the fact that that I was imposing an "ought" onto the task, and in an attempt to manage that resistance a "when I ought" to it as well. Once I identified that the actual resistance was the fact that I did not like being told what to do and when to do it (even though it was myself doing the telling!) the resistance went. I am now happy to deal with the dishwasher "when I feel like it". I "know" it "needs" to be done but that is just common sense - I don't need to be nagged (by myself) to recognise that. And now I quite enjoy doing it because, rather than feeling obligated my mind is usually processing other things I want to do next ......

A rather long explanation but in short "flow" or intuitive working is about ease *and* enjoyment, not one or the other. If something is easy but we don't enjoy it we are not "in the flow". If something is enjoyable, but we feel we should be doing something else or feel guilty about doing that task now, then that is not being in the flow.

Hopes that makes some more sense ..... :-)
