2009-04-7 23:29
The task pages are homemade - used the Levenger notation pages, but my tags were spilling over into the tasks, and vice versa, so I decided a DIY solution was better.

If you have the Junior Size Circa, you can goto any paper store, buy a ream of 60-lb double bond A4 (similar to what Levenger uses) and have them but the whole stack in half. You'll be able to "roll your own" at that point, although the Levenger Desk Punch is a must. 1000 Junior sheets may be a bit overkill, but you sure won't run out of pages for a while :-)

2009-04-7 23:57
Glad this will work for you. As for printing, I use a mac, and entered a custom paper size with dimensions of 5 1/2" x 8 1/2" - my printer (a Canon MX850) has an adjustable feeder in the back. The pages go in "portrait wise" and the printing works great.

Since the A5 is smaller than the Junior page size, A5 printing should work, but be sure you have print scaling selected (same on both windows and mac). The result will be just about the same, but the margins will be a bit larger if you are printing to A5 size on 5 1/2" x 8 1/2" paper.

If you can setup the custom paper size (in Windows I think you can do this in MS Publisher or Word), then you'll be using a greater percentage of the available space. I use a .5mm gel pen, which makes a nice, consistent, and fine line on the pages, which can help to cram a lot of info into a small space.
