I stumbled into this, and I thought it was cool enough to share. I bought narrow post-it tabs to stick onto my looseleaf project pages.

I sorted the pages alphabetically, and put one tab on each page, labeling with the name of the page. These tabs are arranged vertically in alphabetical order, so I can see all of them at once when the pages are stacked.
--------
Now I can grab any page I need at a glance based on its alphabetical position.
When I'm done with it I replace the page at the top of the stack.

Result: Pages are in alphabetical order by the tabs, and chronological order by the stack. If I need a particular page, I look for a tab. If I'm reviewing what I'm working on I look at the front pages. Or if I wonder what I'm ignoring I check the back pages.
