I think this is a matter of trusting your intuition and not making the system too complex.

If you are in the middle of a task, anything you need to do to keep working on it "for as long as you feel like" is part of that task. It is no different than adding it twice. When you get to it (or just notice it) on your list, cross it out and take credit for doing it.

Re: Little and often. I did not really get what that idea was until I watched Mark do his demo on AF4. Ironically it is just what the name implies ;-) You just work on something so long as you feel like it, then write it down do to it again and move on for now. When you see it again, you are not so afraid of it because you KNOW you don't have to do the whole thing, but you CAN just drop it after a while if you want.

The flip side of this (conceptually, but it is really the same idea) is to work from a timer and stick WITH a task for a period then take an enforced break. I really hate the hoo-haa about giving it a cool name (like Pomodoro) because it is just using a damn timer and that idea has been around since there WERE timers. There is something about the sound of the timer going off that basically says "Are you doing what you should be doing?" I dunno, it does seem to work.

I used to have a really hard time stopping when my timer went off - my procrastination problem has always been about resistance starting a task, so I didn't realising that a work "binge" when I finally got moving was just as bad for me, because it left me too burnt out to approach the task a second time without trepidation.
