Hi Jupiter,

Here is what I do. I still do a weekly review and planning. I review my AF lists and see what needs to be done this week. If they truly have to be done this week, then these are deadline-driven projects and tasks. While I do enter these into my AF lists, I also use Outlook 2007 for these. That way I can clearly see projects and tasks that must be done this week. I then schedule block time for completing these -- they go right onto my calendar in Outlook. If my week gets to be too hectic, and I have little discretionary time, then I will also block off time just for AF -- again, right into my Outlook calendar.

This approach provides me a way to deal with my many deadline-driven projects and tasks and at the same time, reap the benefits of using AF for everything else.

I hope this helps.

Best wishes,
-David

I have a lot of recurring weekly tasks. I put the letter of the day of week an item is due/must be done by in the left hand margin I only do this once it is due that week. This allows me to quickly check what is due that day and check again before I go home. I find having the letter very quick to scan for - I suppose the same reason an analogue watch is quicker to read than digital.

You can also see whether the task were really due that day if you don't do them

I was extracting these tasks onto another list but started to feel resistance to the list.

I use the same method as David, every Friday I scan the list for larger tasks or groups of related tasks that I want to work on next week and schedule them, then delete from AF list (great feeling).

When working on scheduled tasks I set my phone alarm on vibrate when its time to move on to the next thing, but always try to remember to add any "left overs" back into my AF list on the new page.

As a catch all, I also review my diary from the past week every Friday to see if there are any actions remaiming I may have missed and add then to my AF list.

Hope this helps,
