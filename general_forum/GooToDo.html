Hey acedia!

I am absolutely thrilled with gootodo. I've had a 100% empty inbox since using it. I've never gone that long with an empty inbox and even when I had one a la GTD, I had numerous files of email tasks like Waiting On and Project folders that I never looked at. I read Mark's book, Bit Literacy, and although not all of the material was relevant to me, the stuff that was made a huge impact on me. It is the easiest, quickest way I know of to assign dates to email tasks. It's so fast to redate items--even on my iPhone. And I love that it automatically moves undone tasks to the next day with no overdue notices attached.

I am not using DWM strictly, but I think you could easily use Gootodo for it. Either create an email with the subject being the task and email it to 1m@gootodo.com or go to the site (I keep a tab open with my list all the time) and click on the date one month from now and create a new task. Simple simple.

I'm using a grand mix of things right now that I'm very happy with. The first thing I do is look at my goo (love that - lol) and decide what I absolutely have to do today. The rest gets redated. One thing I have been trying in the spirit of DWM is when I have an article that I'm not that excited to read, I'll email it to 1m from now. For tasks that are more pressing, I send them to tomorrow in the spirit of DIT. Repeating tasks I do and redate. I do not put everything on my list. Only things that I get via email, things I'm likely to forget, or projects and tasks that have deadlines or someone waiting on them. When I read a blog post I want to do something with, I email it to goo. Before I had it starred forever in my Google reader. When I'm waiting on something, I forward it to the appropriate date. I find that I can get through everything most of the time and even have time to work ahead. If I find myself continuously redating some things, I don't feel guilty about it, because I will either a) have a sudden desire to get it done fast or b) decide not to do it.

I hope your trial with goo goes well. I compared using Toodledo and Google Tasks with goo and felt the latter was the best fit for me.
