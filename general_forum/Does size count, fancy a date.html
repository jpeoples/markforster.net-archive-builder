It's good to see forum friends involved in more focused topics again.

Continuing my current practice of operating in paper and pen mode, I intended moving from the A5 notebook to a Moleskine Large (about the same size). She-Who-Thinks-She-Must-Be-Obeyed has joined me here in Damascus for the next 12 months and brought out from the UK some items I wanted with me here.

In addition to the Moleskine Large notebook (ruled lines), I also asked Gill to bring out a Moleskine Small soft cover (about A6) as a pocket notebook for odds and sods, errands etc.

I have always considered A6 as a bit too small for my AF/DWM use, but in reviewing all the entries to be transferred I realised that I could fit nearly all my tasks onto the smaller page size, even if a few have to span more than one line. So my new DWM notebook is the Moleskine Small.

(1) Do any A6/small notebook AF/DWM users out there consider the size a disadvantage and may, if not already, return to A5 or A4/letter? I'll make my own judgement, I'm just interested how other users have found different sizes make for a better or worse AF/DWM experience.
I consider A6 too small for meeting notes and other work, A5 is my preference for that sort of stuff. However, for DWM where I use just the RH pages for tasks, A6 is ok and a better use of paper. I use LH pages as well as empty space on expired RH pages for things like errands, tel numbers, email addresses, recording expenses etc.

(2) Up to now, I have been dating every RH page in the 28 day cycle I use. Thus all pages after the current day 28 (eg today's "last" page, or "M" as some call it, is Monday 31 May) are blank.
One of the disadvantages for me is that my addled mind tends to view the tasks in relation to the date and I have to "force" myself to disregard the date on the page unless the task I am looking at has a deadline date. So the best thing for me as I see it is to adopt the practice that others have mentioned and just have a moveable page marker tag for D (toDay), W (Week = +7 for re-entered tasks only) and M (Month = +28 for new tasks only). In typing this, I realise I can dispense with tag D and just write in the actual date when that page is Today, because the W and M tags will show me where the task entry pages are. Thus the last dated page will be Today, or yesterday when I start the new day.
Again, I can use the Moleskine ribbon and elastic band as markers, but will probably use a small Post-it page as well.

Any comments?

I think taking the dates out of the 28 forward pages will give me a better perception of the several closed lists, provided I do write the deadline date against any task that MUST have a ABF date (Absolutely B****y Final).

I use a Paperchase Soft Flexible Journal with 200 squared pages (about A6 size I think). I use the right-hand side for DWM. Each page has 35 rows, and I draw a vertical line so that I can enter 70 tasks per page (although someone with long task names, or large handwriting, might prefer to stick to the 35 rows).

In front of each task I leave a square, and when I have processed a task I cross it out, then colour in the square, so it's very easy to see what tasks still remain to be done.

I've experimented with using the left-hand page as well, so that the notebook would last twice as long, but it's too clumsy, and besides, the left-hand side is useful for keeping notes, etc.

Because it's only A6, and soft-covered, it's perfect for carrying around outside, as well as using at home.

I am now thinking of ruling a line under Day 28 at the end of the day and starting tomorrow, the new Day 28, under that line. My DWM RH pages will then become a long list separated by a line at each date change.
