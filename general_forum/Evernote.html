I've struggled to use GTD for the past 5 years but, after about 3 months of AF, have really found it to have so much more 'balance'. Big thanks Mark for this v ingenious system.

I'd started using AF with a standard Moleskine notebook and Space Pen - very GTD - but over the past few weeks have switched to Evernote. Anything can be thrown at Evernote from pretty much anywhere - photos of handwritten notes, voicenotes, webclippings etc can be sent from other computers, Iphones etc. I can also access them from any computer (or my Iphone).

It's therefore pretty much the perfect capture tool. I enjoy the paper approach but there are times when typing on a computer is just that much easier. However, by using Evernote, I don't feel that I'm sacrificing the paper approach as I can always take a quick snapshot of my doodles/mindmaps etc with my phone and send them straight to Evernote (into whatever the latest notebook/page is). They even have a very effective way of allowing words to be searched for in handwritten scanned notes.

I've used Onenote extensively in the past and think it's a pretty slick tool - I'm just finding that the total accessibility and flexibility of Evernote lends itself more easily to AF. I'd be interested in anyone else's experience with Evernote. Apologies if this area has been covered elsewhere in the forum. This is my first contribution to this forum although I've really enjoyed reading everyone's experiences with AF so thanks to all for sharing.

Hi Moises

I create a new notebook called PG X (eg 1,2 ,3 etc) for each page. When I get to 40 items on a page, I'll just start another notebook.

When I've completed an item, I'll simply relabel it with a --- at the front. When I've "retired" a page, I simply move all the items to an Archive notebook and delete that page/notebook so as to avoid having too many open notebooks over time. I have 5 actve notebooks/pages at the moment.

I also tend to tag each note with a label notifying the project name or some other quick ID (ie Admin, blogs etc) just so I can get a quick sense of what's on each page before I do my 'quick sweep' of the actual notes.

Totally understand why people like paper but I'm just finding that Evernote is currently a really great solution to satisfying both sides of my personality. If, for instance, I feel I'm losing focus, I'll just draw a quick 20,000ft mindmap on a piece of paper with my priorities on, take a picture of it and then send it to Evernote. If I read an article on line that I want to follow up on, I can just capture it into Evernote immediately. If I've taken written notes during a meeting, snap them and send to evernote.

I'm finding that having the ability to initially throw absolutely anything at Evernote (which subsequently gets done or refined into actual tasks) really seems to gel with the spirit of AF.

I'm a recovering GTDer (sober for almost 2 weeks now). I love AF and am getting so much more done. I'm currently using the Moleskine. I'm really enjoying the pen and paper approach. The physicality of crossing something out and and rewriting at the end of the list seems like part of what generates the momentum of AF. Maybe the same effect can be generated electronically, but I haven't experimented.

I do have two pieces of my system that are electronic. Many actions amount to "Respond to email from X" or "Check out website ...". I just have an Actions folder I move pending emails to and then on paper I write the action and put an 'e' at the end indicating an email is attached to the action. For other items like a website I want to review I write the action and put a circled 'v' at the end. This means I've placed the reference material in Evernote. I keep one note in Evernote where I dump all those extra electronic items I want to review. Since they are also available on my iPhone it's very handy.

I'm giving Listomni for IPhone a test run now and so far I REALLY like it. It's the ONLY IPhone task manager that allows me to not only see the number of tasks remaining per lpage, but allows me to scroll down through pages that I have open without having to go back to the main list and select the next page. The other feature I love (there are many) is not having to retype anything. You can copy and paste items and as you type, previous entries pop up for selection. You can also easily move tasks with your finger from one page to another. The only drawbacks so far that will be rectified by this summer is that there is no website sync (you back up via email) and no task repeat (though copying and pasting serves this function now and it's coming soon).

Listomni is a VERY flexible program that can be used for groceries and any other lists you might have a need for. I do love the integration of calendar and tasks available on Pocket Informant, but the program runs very, very slowly at this point.
