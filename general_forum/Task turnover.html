For me, one day is often very different from the next. I certainly couldn't be called an AF purist at this stage.

Yesterday I got pummeled by clients who had problems and questions and requests beyond the norm. I am a software developer, and supporting my customers is part of what I do, but the demands of this aspect vary hugely and are quite unpredictable. I ran the gauntlet, invoking the common sense do ti immediately rule, and didn't even open my AF book.

Today, on the other hand, I spent almost the whole day on the one task. It was something I had entered in my book about a week ago, and today was its day. I had to move a whole lot of programming code on order to standardise all editions of my software. I knew it was going to be a big job. For stuff like that, little and often doesn't work for me as well as get stuck in and plod through until it's done. I didn't open my AF book until late afternoon.

Why am I telling you this? Well, I'm not too worried... basically I've had a couple of pretty productive days, and I feel satisfied. But I would like to get into a bit of a flow as a AF user, and I don't feel that so far. How can I, with days like that? Anybody have parallel work commitments? Anyone got ideas or comments of how you manage the chunky and the unpredictable?

Hi Steve

My discretionery time varies immensely with the result that some days I cannot get to my lists at all. I had concerns that I was not moving through the lists quickly enough or that things would be missed, but at the end of the day I do feel productive and do not feel worried by my somewhat erratic progress.

The way I "manage" AF with erratic discretionery time is by using the following:

1) Ensure I know what is in my lists in case I don't get through them all in a day

2) Ensure I have a good system to facilitate overview (in my case my schedule and my project system)

3) Check my schedule regularly

4) Check forum daily (this keeps me in the AF mindset) I try to do this at least morning and evening as a general routine

5) Use small blocks of discretionery time wisely - even 10 minutes can be productive. If at my PC I may use those 10 minutes to check the forum, check email, or review my lists. If away from my desk to brainstorm ideas or jot down tasks in a notebook. Alternatively if you have the chance in that time to do a few AF tasks just do them, even if you are not on that page!

At the end of the day chunky and unpredictable tasks are just that, chunky and unpredictable. It is unlikely you can deal with the tasks in any other way. However you CAN manage the systems if there is a pattern to the type of task. If you find the same type of task recurring, and you can create or enhance a system for dealing with that type of task, then the management of such eventualities can be made easier.

It sounds as if AF is working just fine .......

Legend has it that Pablo Picasso was sketching in the park when a bold woman approached him.

“It’s you — Picasso, the great artist! Oh, you must sketch my portrait! I insist.”
