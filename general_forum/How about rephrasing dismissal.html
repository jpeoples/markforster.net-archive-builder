1. Done with or marked by full consciousness of the nature and effects; intentional:

4. To think carefully and often slowly, as about a choice to be made

than "dismissal" which means
a.To stop considering; rid one's mind of; dispel: or
b. To refuse to accept or recognize; reject:

2009-02-7 11:57
After all the discussion about the term "dismissed" I still feel that it is the right term to use. I wanted a word which had a somewhat peremptory sound about it, as in "If you don't do this, you're dismissed". It was intended to denote the moment of truth, when the choice stops being "Do I do Task A or Task B?" and becomes "Do I do Task A at all?"

It's very important to realise that dismissal is essential to the whole AF process, and permeates the whole process even when no tasks are in immediate danger of being dismissed. The knowledge that in the end a task will get dismissed if it isn't done actually reduces resistance - there's nothing you *have* to do in AF. But it also means that at some stage you really do have to make a final choice about tasks. If there were no dismissal in AF you wouldn't be able to throw things in it without pre-evaluation.

My question is about whether or not to keep some kind of record of dismissed tasks.

If you try to delete a song from your ITUNES music library, you are given a choice: whether you want to a) delete the song from ITUNES but still keep a copy of it on your hard drive OR b) delete the song from ITUNES and also delete it from your hard drive, thus making it lost forever.

When I dismiss a task from my "Autofocus" list, I like to know that there is some warehouse where the dismissed task can go and perhaps be reviewed at some later date. It's as though the "task" had been up for parole and didn't make it. But someday, it might.

The "shot at dawn" metaphor is much harsher. The task had many chances to get done, and now "its time is up." And this would suggest that when you dismiss a task, you do not need to keep any record of it thereafter.
