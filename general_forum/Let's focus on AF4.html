This has been a very productive forum where, working and testing together, Mark's creation has improved dramatically. A number of us (I guess many more than actually write on the forum) are happily using AF4. I suspect there are still useful improvements ahead of us, and this needs analysis of the tweaks and adaptations that people are making to it.

I myself am using it exactly as Mark defined, and it seems to work better as time goes on.

I'll give an example of one issue. When a list gets long, one can get bogged down in the closed list. One can also get bogged down in the open list. Possible adaptation: at the start of the day, move to whichever section of the list you were not on the previous day.

Another issue which many face is handling tasks which apply in different locations. I myself am happy using a single handwritten notebook, but I think this issue will only be satisfactorily sorted with software. I believe in embracing technology wherever it is helpful, and this is likely to be one such case.

I am hoping that the synergy of people and ideas here will continue to help Mark and the rest of us to push this unique project forward, though should it have to stay at its current stasis I am still very thankful.

I agree a list can get bogged you bogged down if it's too long. My suggestion is to actively dismiss tasks when that gets to be a problem. Dismiss tasks that you really don't intend to work on soon.

Dismiss means either redefine the task and put into the open list, or delete it. Instead of deleting, you might put the task into a tickler for later reminder.
