Hi - a quick post to share how I use Things.

1. Create (only) three Areas: "Review", "Backlog" and "Active".

2. Associate the tag "Autofocus" with each Area.

3. Drag the tasks you want to start with into "Active" and start the Autofocus cycle.

4. In Autofocus mode, select "Next" and click the "Autofocus" tag. You have your "Review", "Backlog" and "Active" lists.

5. When you've reached the point where you can't do any more, drag all the remaining tasks into Backlog. New tasks now go into Active. Drag undoable Backlog tasks into Review and triage. Rinse and repeat.

6. In Planning mode, select "Next" and click the "All" tag. Drag the tasks you want to into "Active".

7. Set up your sequence of next actions in Projects and Scheduled. Drag the next action(s) into "Active" when you are ready to work on it. Unfortunately, you've lost the ability to group project by Area but I don't miss it. You also lose the task's Project context when it goes onto the Active list, but again I don't miss it either - because of "little and often", it's easy to remember. Drag a scheduled task into "Active" if you want it to automatically appear on your Active list.

So Things is acting as a tickler (which Autofocus assumes you have somewhere) and Autofocus is acting as a processing tool to breakdown the overwhelm when Things generates a load of tasks. You can tag and filter tasks with contexts, and get priority cues with dates.

Very functional on the iPad, too.
