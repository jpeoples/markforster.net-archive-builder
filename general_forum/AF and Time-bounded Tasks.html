In general, time-specific tasks go to the calender. The autofocus list is for use in your discretionary time.

Personally, I differentiate between the time-specific event itself and preparation for it. It put preparatory steps on my af list, but I also put a reminder on the calender enough time ahead of the event. So I have to opportunity to do it by using autofocus, but the reminder ensures that I do it in time if I didn't get to it. For example I really must send a form to my health insurance today, so I have "send form to health insurance" on my calendar, but I also had "fill out health insurance form" on my af list.

In my own implementation - http://autofocus.cc - I ultimately plan to add a separate management for time-specific task and have those tasks that are due be displayed above my af list in a different color.

2009-02-24 11:38
I'm dealing with this slightly differently, and thought I was following one of Mark's instructions! I have a post-it (the instruction, I believe, was for a note card) on which I note things which have to be done today but don't have a time. (Timed appointments do go in my diary.) I put the post-it opposite the autofocus page I'm working on and include it as part of the page when I move through the page looking for what to do now. I'm finding this works really well. All the things to do today get done, but they don't push out the less time-specific jobs completely. It's also saved me from constantly doing email as it comes in. I put 'email morning' and 'email evening' on my do-today post-it so that I know it will get checked sufficiently and then ignore incoming messages in-between.
