Hi Folks,

Thanks to a post by smileypete and others, I think I am going to do the following. First, I am going to maintain my Outlook 2007 setup of all of my time-sensitive tasks associated with my deadline-driven major projects. All other tasks -- the important ones that I never seem to have time to do -- writing manuscripts, book chapters, etc. are going into a paper notebook for Autofocus. Yes, you can put your eyes back into your head. This digital person is going to try a notebook-based system -- EXACTLY as Mark has described. I then will schedule time in my Outlook calendar as "Discretionary AF time". During this block time, I ONLY will do tasks in my AF notebook.

I fully realize this is still two systems, but with my situation, I have come to the conclusion that I need to do it this way. The more I have thought about this, the more excited I have become!

Wish me luck. This way, I can maintain my digital, nightmarish, deadline-driven setup in Outlook....but then can escape periodically in scheduled blocks to do AF from a paper notebook.

Thanks to all who have suggested this approach!

-David
