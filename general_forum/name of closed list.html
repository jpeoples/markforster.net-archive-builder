Goldmund,

When Mark initially talked about AF4 he used the term "backlog". That caused confusion because those things on that list are NOT backlogged. He changed the names to "closed" and "open" because those more accurately describe what the lists are.

The "closed" list is called that because it is ... well ... CLOSED ;-) Nothing can be added ... and that is the only attribute worth calling attention to. The "open" list is in fact OPEN, because things are continually added to it.

You will still see some people here talking about the "backlog list" but that terminology is dead and should be avoided to prevent confusion.

Tessa,

If you understand the meaning of "closed" to be "the list is closed to new additions so that the items on it can be completed before I move on to new things" then is there still a problem? The terminology (and the concept) comes from Mark's earlier works where he advised that you do not "do it today" but rather "do it tomorrow" when it comes to incoming work. IOW, rather than jumping on every new thing you allocate your work for the day, do THAT, and put off new items. This approach solves the problem of things languishing on your To Do list because time is continually allocated to new, and most likely more seemingly urgent, items. Each day then, you "close" your list for the day and take on no new material until you've finished the old.

There is no sense at all of the WORK being closed, or the WORKER being closed for business ;-) I'm a big fan of aligning the subconscious but you really don't need to try to keep an accurate description if that is your intent. Why not just call it your FUN list? Or your POSSIBILITIES list? Or even your PINK list, if that makes you smile?

The think is that names are important to keep you focused on what things mean to you. But if it is not the FUNCTION that is of interest, but rather the MOTIVATION, then use motivational terms and bag the functional description. (I may start calling mine "Betty Boop!)
