Lots of cool input here. For me, I either don't look at my list at ALL on the weekend, OR my dear hubby takes the five kiddos for an outing and gives me a gift of the house to myself for 4 hours -- and then I tackle my list like crazy. (This was with the AF versions)

I've been doing DWM for two weeks now, and haven't looked at my list at all on the weekends (time for hubby to step it up!) and I ran into that problem of things expiring all of a sudden on Monday morning when my list was out again. But what I did was re-evaluate instead of just ditching the weekend pages with whatever tasks were undone.

I allowed myself two minutes to do one of three things with each item (and I have 24 lines on a page, so that's the max, which made for one hour max for the re-evaluation):

(1) a quick teeny something on the task, the teeniest thing possible, then re-entering it seven days out (from Monday, not the original weekend +7 so it would end up on another weekend and perpetuate the original problem). I love to crochet and it's not usually on my list, because I just pick it up when I'm watching a DVD on downtime. But I am trying to get my nephew's afghan done by the end of the school year. A little bit, often, is going to be key for this, so I put "Adam's afghan" on the list. It was going to expire Sunday, so on my Monday morning evaluation, I really did get out the afghan, crochet three stitches and moved the task accordingly!!

or
(2) enter the task 30 days out as a NEW task. i.e. MAKE it new by approaching it a different way. For example, my husband has completed a novel and is in the midst of the publisher/agent hunt. I have tried to be a good helper to him. I was failing. My list said "Find publisher". It's not clear enough, not motivational enough. I re-entered it 30 days out (on one of those Monday mornings) as "Find 5 publishing houses accepting manuscripts". THAT actually got accomplished already!

(3) Let it go!

So far, that evaluation process is really helping me. I think the same will work for me for sick days, vacation days, etc. Although I really like the idea of NOT using the pages when you have PLANNED days off (who DOES that? I really should...) (grin)
