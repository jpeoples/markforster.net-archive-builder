Closed list and open list aspects of AF

Author: Mark Forster | Created: 2009-01-26 16:42 | Last Activity: 2010-04-28 11:46

Original Post

2009-01-26 16:42
One thing which perhaps I haven't stressed enough is that AF is designed to get the benefit of both closed and open lists. I'm becoming more and more convinced as I read the comments is that it's important for the working of the system to keep these two aspects in balance.

The way to do this is to control the amount of "little and often" that one is doing.

Symptoms of not enough "little and often" are:

1) slow movement through the list
2) excessive worry about deadlines
3) difficulty in leaving a task unfinished

Symptoms of too much "little and often" are:

1) tendency to spend most of one's time at the end of the list
2) lots of half-finished projects
3) superficial actions rather than real progress

Ask yourself:

Am I getting through the list at least once on a normal day?

If the answer is YES, you are probably balanced in your approach to AF.

If the answer is NO, then ask yourself:

Am I spending most of my time near the end of the list?

If the answer is YES, you are probably using too much "little and often". Although you are near the end of the list, you are re-entering actions so fast that you hardly ever get right to the end.

If the answer is NO, then you are probably using not enough "little and often" and are getting stuck on earlier pages.

Think of "little and often" as being like a knob on a radio. You can the sensitivity up or down to get just the right balance.

Mel, that's what dismissal is though (for me) - accepting that I don't have the time to commit to that project at the expense of other things that I want to do - for now at least. Maybe that's because I don't feel much resistance to doing most things as long as my energy level is there and I'm not feeling overwhelmed. For me, not getting through my pages often enough was entirely due to the fact that I was attempting to take on too many projects at once - basically overloading the system and creating chaos all around me and overwhelm in my mind. So I dismissed ALOT - I think something like 180+ tasks in my home book.

In some cases, there has to be decision making about things to take on. For example, if I'm doing a home renovation and I have $X and X time to complete it in, I'm not going to put in the "nice to have's" at the expense of the essentials. Sometimes some things really are more important than others.

I've also clarified to myself that getting my house renovated before on time really is more important than having it spic and span all the time. So I've dialed up my little and often on the house cleaning items and dialed it down on the renovation items by changing the wording or grouping or ungrouping things.
