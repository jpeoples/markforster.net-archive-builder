Is this a good or bad idea? I think it's good. It's only a strategy: use it while it helps, change when it hurts.

Have exactly one unfinished task. Choose any task of high value that you want to really progress.
Work this task in bursts of energy. Pomodoros if you like. Or just go until you are slowing, figure what must be next in the project, and stop. Or go until something interrupts you, and stop. Reenter next page.
All C1 tasks are do-a-little tasks only. Pick only one and do it. Do something urgent, if any.
Turn the page.

By this strategy you should be moving forward 2 pages an hour, or at least one an hour, and you'll pass through the list twice a day or more. The important project will be done very quickly. And all those so-called urgent things won't clog up the system because you will easily get to them in time: (write them bold to stand out when you reach them)

Don't forget to include Break type tasks.

Here is how I understand your proposal:

+ In C1, enter only (a) clearly defined small tasks and (b) vaguely defined tasks.

+ Keep one unfinished task in C2.

+ When you turn to a new page, you should work on your unfinished C2 task (for no more than 1 pomodoro = ~30 minutes) and you should also work on one C1 task (for no more than 1 pomodoro = ~30 minutes). This C1 task should be, in order of preference, (a) a small urgent task, or (b) a small task. In this way, you will spend no more than 1 hour on a page -- and likely less than that.

+ Since you are moving quickly through your list, there is no need to enter urgent things into C2. Just keep them in C1. When you come to a new page and you choose your C1 item to work on, that's the time to choose one of those urgent items on that page, if there are any.
