(Inspired in part by Richard's post about how he's implemented AF4 in Things)

I'm in a space where a bunch of stuff has hard deadlines coming due RIGHT NOW. When I'm processing my DWM list I really need to only be choosing from those things... everything else can slide and if it gets dismissed, it gets dismissed.

I'm working DWM in Toodledo and it makes this kind of thing SO easy to deal with (I don't recall who recommended Toodledo to me when I got my iPad but THANK YOU). Under normal circumstances I have two folders: Active and Dismissed. My normal working mode has me using the Active list except when I'm reviewing dismissed tasks. Now, though, I have created a third folder: "DueDatesThisWeek" and moved everything in that category over to that folder. I'm processing it the same way I would the normal DWM list - new stuff gets entered on "page" M (given a due date of 27 Nov) and re-entries go on "page" W (given a due date of 2 Nov). It just reduces the number of things I have to look at and reduces the anxiety that I feel looking at all the OTHER things that I could be doing.

I did something similar last week when I was away at a conference: moved everything that I COULDN'T deal with while away from home/office to its own folder so that it didn't clutter up the list that I was working with.
