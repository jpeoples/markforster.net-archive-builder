The method I have used to combine the 3-task system with AF4 is about as simple as it could be. First I select three tasks using the rules of AF4, then I switch to "3-task mode" and rotate around those three tasks until two of them are finished. Then starting from where I selected the last task on the AF4 list, I select another two and so on.

I don't re-write the tasks when I put them in 3-task mode. I do them in place by marking them with a large dot. Once I've finished the task, I cross it out and re-enter it if it's a recurring task.

Here is how I am trying this method out.

Firstly, I am using it as a time management not a goal management system. I am using DO IT which already focuses on three longer term goals.

Conceptually, I have two "sections" to a single notebook. I am using the last page as my three task system, writing three important items to finish during the day. I am keeping the notebook open to this page during the day and working until I am down to one item or less, I then add two more. At the end of the day this page is ripped out and a new one started using any unfinished items from it and the "capture" section of the notebook as choices for the new three task list.

The front of the notebook is what I call my capture section. So it is for notes, other to-dos that pop up during the day or exist already, project lists, waiting for items etc. It is designed just to make sure I do not forget anything and have all that needs to be thought about in one place, ie called into a meeting, need to jot notes, messages on the voicemail to take off, idea pops into head.

At the end of the day, yesterday, I reviewed the capture section, created my three tasks on the then last page and filed any notes and threw away any unneeded pages.

This could also work with an index card or separate sheet for the three tasks along with a single notebook or legal pad.

The only rules I am following are;

Keep the notebook open to the three tasks page unless you must add to the capture section.

all tasks go first into the capture section so there is no distractions from the current three tasks. This serves are a review mechanism to select important items from the totality of what you must accomplish.

stay on the three tasks until there are only one or less before looking to the capture section to add more tasks.

make sure the three tasks are in fact actionable tasks you can do in a days time or less, so I am using words like, call Joe, read 3rd quarter report, review lease, make wire transfer to bank.

Write in this notebook only, so all you need to think about is in one place for simplicity

It seems to work for me because I have the sense of peace that all that ultimately must be done is captured and I am focused on what I believe to be the three most important tasks are at the moment.

At some point the notebook will run out and I will use this as an opportunity for a review of the unfinished task, notes etc.

After doing this yesterday, it seems like a simple system that will work very well. I will keep using it and let people know. If anyone has questions on this implementation, please let me know. I will refine the instructions and post them to my blog, but I think this seems to work for me.

Thanks

Gerry
