I see exactly one point of conflict: Don't prioritize your tasks. After that, everything Hurst suggests can be done while following an AF4 system.

Mark Hurst suggests moving actionable emails to a TODO list. Let AF4 be that list. To make the most of Hurst's ideas your TODO system should allow you to embed emails, and also use future dates as a tickler system. AF4 operates on the TODAY page only, and date all tasks for when you can start working on them (which is usually TODAY), not when they are due.

In my case, I'm using OneNote for my AF4 and it's easy to push Outlook emails directly into my AF4 list.

Hurst's TODO list suggestions (My responses):
1. Summary and detail view (Good idea.)
2. Priority within a day (Completely opposite AF4)
3. View of today's todos only, and the ability to send todos to the future, and automatic roll-over, at midnight, of any undone todos to tomorrow. (This is beyond AF4 but if followed as above, it doesn't contradict.)
4. One single list of todos - not several lists (AF4!)
5. E-mail tie-in, allowing users to forward e-mails to future days on the todo list - or BCC themselves to create a check-in reminder in the future. (If you can embed email into your TODO list, you have this.)
