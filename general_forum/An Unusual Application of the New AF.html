I homeschool six children and have a detailed and specific schedule for the school day. Much of the time this works well, but at a certain point in the school year, it begins to break down. I believe I understand why. I detest routine. I hate feeling obligated. I thrive on newness, freedom, and fun. I believe that is why AF has worked longer for me than any other approach. There's little scheduling of tasks, duty, and forcing oneself to do things.

So I started thinking about using AF for our school day. I have a list of things that should be accomplished on a given day (everything is pre-planned). I also have my detailed schedule of subjects to teach. I wondered if just taking the list of subjects and teaching the subject that stood out first would work. Then I worried that I would lose the benefits of the schedule should I use that approach every day. Then eureka! I realized I could have a daily list of subjects with the addition of the subject: Use the Schedule. When using the schedule stands out, I will use it. When I would rather teach subjects as they stand out, I will. I am really excited about this approach as I think it will virtually eliminate any reticence I've experienced as a result of feeling obligated by the schedule. Thanks, Mark! I plan to use a checklist in Listomni that I can clear and start over with each day. Because I like to have the opportunity to do some interest-based learning (also called unschooling), I may add this to the list and unschool whenever that stands out, too! If this method is successful for me, I will blog about it and share it with other homeschoolers, too.
