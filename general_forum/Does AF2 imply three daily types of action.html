I pose the above question in order to understand better the AF2 modalities. On reading Mark's instructions I see that one has three daily activities:

First, there is the working through the list of outstanding items, beginning each time from the entries on the last page.

Second, one has to take into account the tasks that have been mentally highlighted when going through the daily reading of the list.

Third, one takes into account that there are tasks (in the older pages) that are subject to dismissal in the following day or days.

Giving, therefore, appropriate weight to the second and third type of action and especially the relative importance of tasks (in the second or third type of action above) or the imminent chance of dismissal of tasks (in the third type of action above), my natural tendency would be to start doing tasks every day by giving priority to the second and third type of tasks before doing any tasks according to the first type of action.

I should emphasise that the above scenario is based on conditions where one has a "mature" list which is, say, several pages long.

Otto and Rainer:

The preliminary at the start of the days consists of dismissing any uncompleted tasks on notice from the previous day, identifying the tasks on notice for dismissal for the current day and reading through the list from beginning to end.

Once that preliminary is finished there are only two things you have to do (with regard to the list that is):

1. Add new tasks to the end of the list as they occur to you or come up.

2. Starting from the last item on the list, look at each item in turn in turn until one "stands out". When you've done it, go back to the end of the list and do the same again.

There is no need to take account of anything else, and you should not re-enter tasks on notice of dismissal (either as a whole or broken down further) at the end of the list.

Well, if I know that I will just do the items facing dismissal at the start of each day, I have less incentive to work my way through the list which has the consequence of breaking up large block of unactioned tasks that may be on the dismissal list in several days. The biggest advantege of leaving items where they are and work them in order, is it forces us to give some attention to everything on the list; by forcing us to make some effort to work to get to the "to be dismissed" items, means we make it easier to deal with "to be dismissed" items down the road (the more we actually work the items as we move to the "to be dismissed" area, we clear up those large blocks of unactioned task which helps down the road.)
