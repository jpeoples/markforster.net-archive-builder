2011-02-14 14:46
DWM *introduced* the concept of unfinished work as separate from new work. The subsequent systems merely built on the idea. The first difference between SF and DWM is the former has a visual separation of new vs. unfinished. So as I posted earlier, follow DWM but digitally flag the re-entered stuff AS Unfinished in addition to putting it on today +7. That is your virtual column 2.

If you want to be closer to SAF which insists you keep working at stuff you start, re-enter everything at day +1. Add urgent stuff to day+0.

I bet you could try something like this:
Work DWM pages one week at a time.
Follow the DWM dismissal rule.
Treat each week like a page for active tasks, and follow SF rules for rewriting.
Hard limit of 4 active tasks.

As for SF:
Filtering... I'm not sure what manner of filtering you did. SF normally insists you stick to a page, then stick to the next page. There's not much call for filtering. When something comes up,

I do use Search to find something I need. You are allowed to use urgency as a reason to do something now/soon, rather than when you get to it in the list.

"Stands out" still applies.

An outline structure with drag/drop should work, except for dismissing. The tricky thing about digital SF is that deleting or moving tasks (not by crossing out) screws up the "start next page when full" and "dismiss when full" rules.
