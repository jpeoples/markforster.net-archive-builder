Hi Margherita

My own recommendation would be to try AF exactly as Mark recommends. As you have seen there are a number of posts regarding scheduled items and Mark suggests keeping those items separate in whatever diary/scheduling tool you use. Standard To Do Lists often leave you with a reluctance to put certain items that we don't want to do on them. One of the things I have found is that, because AF does away with that reluctance but allows - even encourages - us to throw everything at it, then the lists may well grow quicker than you think and what will you then do when your page is filled up already?

If your existing scheduling system is not working well for you then try using AF to create a new one, e.g. list a task like "set up new scheduling system". That is what I did and it really helped me - if anything comes to mind that needs scheduling, rather than stop what I am doing, I just add a task "schedule item x". I also have a task "check schedule" which gets relisted at the end of my list each time I action it.
