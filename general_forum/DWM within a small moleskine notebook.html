I went through AF1 to AF4 using the moleskine pocket book (about 9 X 14 cm). I liked it: very small, can be carried everywhere, and it's a pleasure to read through the tasks.

With the appearance of DWM I was quite reluctant to switch because I had to move to a calendar. After some time I gave it a try. For a couple of month I used an A5 calender with one page per day. After some time, I noticed that I used it less frequently than I should. It didn't feel as "intimate" as the small book, it had a lot of empty or rarely filled pages between full pages, and it was too big to be carried with everytime. I started a try with a A5 ring binder with 4 sections for the coming 4 weeks to avoid the many empty pages - but it didn't feel right.

Thus, a couple of weeks ago I started again with a moleskine pocket book. Problem: How to deal with the expiry dates and the two entry points (one-week-from-now, four-weeks-from-now)? And how to avoid leaving much space which is not filled in the end?

My solution is as follows:

At the 4-weeks-from-now entry point I only fill the pages by half (or less). The top part of each page is used, the bottom part is left open. Tasks which go at the 1-week-from-now entry point fill the bottom part of the pages. If a page has been completely filled, its expiry date is set to 7 days. Thus, a task entered at the 4-weeks-from-now entry will get its expiry date only when the 1-week-from-now entry has reached and filled the page.

And I found a very easy way to deal with days without activities: I start every active day with adding a bar to each active page which is completely filled. All those pages which get the eighth bar are subject to dismissal. On days where I have no or almost no time to work from the list, no bars are added to the filled pages - thus they can live one day longer.

As a result, I have my compact notebook back, and I am working according to the basic princeples of DWM. Works great for me and brought me back on track.

Seraphim:
Yes, you understand the system correctly (and explain it possibly better than I did). And you describe exactly the "lifecycle" of the tasks: The life of today+28 tasks are dependent on the rate of entries at the today+7 entry point: As soon as their page is filled by today+7 tasks, their expiry date is set. This date is not known from the beginning. Therefore, I have to observe a little bit the distance between the two entry points and add more or less tasks on one page at the today+28 pages.

The bar is a vertical line at the top of the page, the fifth line crossing the four previous ones.
