I have been struggling with AF. The issue is one of overwhelm, feeling like I'm drowning in tasks. I had exactly the same problem with GTD. I left my job six months ago to take a six month break. The break has now ended and I'm starting my own business so I've got a lot to do.

Yesterday I took my notebook and put the date at the top of the page and jotted down a couple of things to do, put a dot next to the one I decided to do and did it for as long as I wanted. Then I crossed it out and readded if it wasn't done. I added things as I thought of them.

Today I started afresh with a new page. I haven't decided what to do with the undone's from yesterday and the other lists of "things to do" including the old AF list. OK not a system but its got me started and feeling better.

I think it IS a system. It is YOUR system. Why not try what we have advised here so often.

- Just add to the list, don't start a new one each day,

- Don't do anything with items from your old lists, wherever they are from. Perhaps scan them to see if there is something you should be paying attention to ... if you find such things, add them to your list in your book. If not, just trust that they will come up again on their own ... they will.
