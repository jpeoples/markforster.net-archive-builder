This has probably been discussed before, but I'm too lazy to look for it. I'm at the point where I have filled up one bound notebook. I see four possibilities (P) for continuing into a new notebook…

P1. Just carry both around until the last few pages of the old one are completed, dismissed, & reviewed.

P2. Finish visit to the current page in the old notebook, then dismiss it and the rest of the pages. Add "Review dismissed tasks from old notebook" as the first task in the new one. Then go back to the old notebook just once more, when this new review task stands out.

P3. Copy the remaining tasks into the new notebook. (For my current situation, this would require two pages.)

P4. Copy the remaining pages as they stand, including completed, dismissed, and crossed out items. (For my current situation, this would take up four pages.)

P4 would function in the same way as P1, but require more tedious work.

I think I'll choose P2. I like the idea of starting the new notebook without lots of old entries. But I'll probably reenter most of them soon. I would enjoy reading about how others have handled this issue. I think this is the first time I'm starting a new notebook using the same system (SF Version 3)!

I've tried all those (with AF or SF), plus:

P5. Enter a new task in Column 2 of your new book: "Copy forward tasks from old notebook". On each iteration, copy as many tasks as you feel like copying. Keep moving this task forward in Column 2 until it's done.

Even with only about 15 pages with tasks remaining, this was time-consuming, and that old notebook hung around for a couple of weeks. What a bother.

The one I liked best is your P2. But I think I'd give myself one more pass through the old notebook. Anything left after that final pass is dismissed. Presumably I've had plenty of opportunities to take action on most of the tasks that are still in there -- the final pass is there to take care of anything that's still fairly "current" or "fresh" -- probably in the last few pages of the notebook.

Of course I'd enter a new task in my new notebook: "Review dismissed tasks in old notebook". I'd keep re-entering it in Column 1 of my new notebook as long as I still have any anxiety about that old notebook. Once the anxiety is gone, I'd finally delete that task and put the old notebook in a time capsule for future archaeologists to puzzle over.

Hi Ubi and others. Yesterday I moved to a new notebook. I had 30 active pages in the old notebook. I copied all the tasks over into Column 1 of the new notebook, using a thick coloured line to show where a page-worth of tasks in the old notebook ended.

So although the new notebook only has 6 actual pages, there are 30 'old' pages in it. For the purposes of dismissal, each 'old' page will be treated individually.

Of course, this will affect the way Column 2 works: Column 2 on the first actual page will cover 6 'old' pages, so it will fill up more quickly than normal.

I listed all the dismissed tasks from the old notebook on the back page of the new notebook, and have a recurring task to review them.

I ended up doing a variant of P2. I changed my "review old dismissed" task to cover only the most-recently-dismissed Page N, added "review old dismissed p. But the rapid review worked out well, probably because SF seems to keep my list to only a few active pages (compared to earlier systems). And I chose not to reenter several tasks, so my new notebook is not too overwhelming.
