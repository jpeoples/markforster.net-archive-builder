Reply by Seraphim

2009-08-31 5:48
I'm finding my time dominated by Reverse Mode. I thought it was because I've been dealing with a lot of urgent tasks lately, and doing a lot of overtime. But now I think, like others have said, that the "tyranny of the urgent" just takes over and prevents "Forward Mode" from feeling ready to be done.

And when I do switch to Forward Mode, I usually stay there a VERY short time -- looking at just one or two pages, before nothing feels ready to be done and I switch back to Reverse.

If I do happen to stay in Forward Mode longer, like I did yesterday, it's usually because I come to those neglected middle pages. Half the items on those pages aren't even applicable any more, because they got re-entered later during the regular course of things, and got dealt with in Reverse Mode. So I mark them all as completed and look for anything interesting that's left on the page. Might find one or two things, take a small action, and then add the item to the end.

Yesterday I spent about an hour or two doing that, going through about 50 pages in the neglected middle, till I finally found a page that didn't have anything ready to be done. It felt a bit like AF1, but somehow I didn't really get anything important done.

Here is what really stood out for me. It seems that Forward Mode is designed to be where you spend most of your time, after all your urgent stuff has been dealt with in Reverse Mode and you feel enough on top of your urgent tasks that it's OK to switch to Forward. Forward Mode is really supposed to help you work on the "important" tasks-- some of which are big projects that are hard to break down into manageable chunks.

AF1 dealt with these things really well, especially when using the "little and often" approach. But Forward Mode doesn't seem to have the same effect. I really don't feel patient enough to settle in and work in Forward Mode long enough to do any effective or meaningful work on the important stuff. I keep itching to get back to Reverse Mode -- the siren call of urgency keeps beckoning. And eventually I heed its call, before I've been in Forward Mode long enough to really get into the swing of things.

Also, I really do miss the AF1 dismissal process. It just seemed really intuitive and effective. Instead of dismissing, Forward Mode just sends you back to Reverse Mode when nothing feels ready to be done. At first I liked this, and felt it made sense, because sometimes it seemed nothing felt ready to be done in Forward Mode, because I was getting nervous about urgent things and needed to go take a look at them. But now, nothing stands out and I switch back to Reverse Mode, just because I like the adrenaline rush of always working on what's urgent.

I HAVE been working pretty effectively in RAF. But I think that's because, lately, I'm doing so much overtime and MUST focus on the urgent things. Now that some of that urgency is going away, I am feeling nervous, and feeling a bit uneasy that maybe I've been letting other important things go too long without the attention they deserve.

It's made me take a serious look at the TRO training and software that has been discussed on a different thread. I'm holding off on that, since I really don't want to abandon a paper-based approach and always be tied to my computer, and also since I am looking forward to whatever Mark's got percolating for us... But yes, I am also feeling that my middle pages are getting neglected, and more importantly, the AF1 ability to focus on what's really important doesn't really seem to be working for me in RAF.

One more thing -- yesterday I got into a good groove, getting lots done on several different fronts. I was working in Reverse Mode, and I was using a timer to make sure I didn't spend too much time on any one task.

I had half a dozen urgent things I needed to work on, and I knew they were scattered across my most recent 3-4 pages. I'd set the timer maybe for 10 minutes, maybe 15 minutes, maybe 30 or 40 minutes, for any one task, whatever felt appropriate at the time.

I kept finding that I needed to force myself to ignore my most recent page, and keep scanning back a few pages, to make sure I dug out all those items. Eventually I got them all on my most current page.

But that didn't last long, as I quickly started a new page, and after a couple hours, yet another new page. The tasks on which I needed to focus were again scattered across the last several pages. And I couldn't just focus on the last page. It certainly had urgent things that needed attention, but I also knew there were a few other tasks that kept falling to the 2nd and 3rd most recent pages, and I needed to make sure I kept moving them forward as well.

Reverse Mode made this somewhat difficult. I kept wanting to go back to the tasks I had just recently worked on, the ones that were still right in front of me on the last page. But this wasn't working, and was leaving those other tasks neglected. I had to keep going hunting for them. I was really getting a lot done, and moving forward on all this work, but there were just too many to keep in my head at the same time, and I couldn't remember what was already completed and what wasn't.

I found myself wishing for AF1 to deal with this. You work the current page. If your timer runs out but your task isn't done yet, it gets moved to the last page. Out of sight for a bit. Which is nice, because it forces you then to work on something else for a while, till you come to that last page.

I almost started up a temporary AF1 list just to deal with this particular day's work. But before I did that, I actually got to the end of this pile of work, and finished off nearly all those tasks, so it became a moot point.

But again, my key observation from this is that I really miss the AF1 approach. I really liked the rhythm and balance it provided, the focus on what's really important. It certainly did have issues with handling truly urgent tasks, and I'm NOT saying that I'm going to go back to AF1.

But RAF doesn't actually seem to provide that auto-focus character any more. It's still effective for getting lots of work done. But I don't have the confidence that I had with AF1, that the IMPORTANT stuff is getting done "automatically", while the rest is dismissed "automatically".

Hi Cruisader,

Good thread. When I started RAF I'd say I spent 99% of my time in reverse mode, 1% in Forward and none in Review.

Since then I've liberally scattered "Switch to ... mode" for forward and Review, adding one of each every couple of pages. This has helped me a great deal and RAF is now working very well for me. Percentage-wise I'd say it is now 60% Reverse, 35% Forward and 5% Review.

Standard approach is Reverse which hits all of the activites added over the past 3-5 days. Moving to Forward mode knocks out tasks from prior to that. Spending time in that mode feels different and I try to stay there as long as possible, and also add a new Switch task to the end of the list when I finish so that I can easily go back in to Forward (once I've done a little Reverse to clear anything urgent/due) and continue on a roll. I like this over AF1 in that then I was missing deadlines - RAF enables me to do both.

Review mode is occasional for me. "The creeping line of death" (early tasks which get marked for Review) is slow, steady but powerful. A lot of tasks go into it over time, and a few get done. When it feels right to revisit them, which tends to be when there's a few moments peace or when travelling, most stay where they are In Review, and a few make the leap to new tasks (reworded, and with a feel that they wll get done this time).

It feels like AF1 was a faster yet "scarier" ride, moving the start of the open list much faster than RAF does. I would consider putting a whole page into Review in the future, rather than just the fist block of contiguous tasks (as these are often now only 1-3 items). I prefer Review to Dismiss as a term too, enabling not-yet items to stay put, on review, out of mind but not out of sight.
