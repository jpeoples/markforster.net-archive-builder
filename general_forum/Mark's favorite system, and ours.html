It stands for the Latin "ad Jesum per Mariam" (to Jesus through Mary) and is something that some Catholics (not me!) write at the beginning of written communications. It's similar to the way Muslims when writing a formal letter will put "Bismillah ar-rahman ar-raheem" (in the name of God the compassionate the merciful) at the top.

2010-10-31 9:05
Alan: I strike through with a black straight line (gel pen) as soon as I have done the task, or put an arrow on it to defer it (mainly due to context or physical location).

During review sessions, I highlight the completed ones in green (and the cancelled, can't-be-bothered to do it, dismissed tasks which have a black wavy line) and the deferred tasks in yellow.

Strangely, I find during reviews that the UNhighlighted tasks DO stand out, maybe because of a hopefully increasing green 'lawn' growing on the pages. Other users may find the highlighting gets in the way somehow, but it works for me and that's what is important, for me.

I'm toying with marking Dismissed for Review in orange, but I do not want too many colours on a page.

I also use a Moleskine pocket book to cull MITs, 'Threes' or similar priority tasks from my AF lists every day. Work tasks are listed from the top, Personal from the bottom up, planned Errands go in between along with any other tasks done (like 'below the line'?) or short notes eg email address, tel number, whatever. I apply the same highlighting rules and strive for a green only appearance at the end of the day, but not always successfully.

Thanks to nuntym and others, I keep maintenance tasks separate, located in Outlook and which come up three times during the day, eg check email, return calls, review AF.
