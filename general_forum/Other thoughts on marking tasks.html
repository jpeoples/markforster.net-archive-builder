This is interesting. It looks like a nice way of keeping track of progress on projects in order to decide what to do next.

I don't feel a need for such a system in AF, as I don't need to worry that I will forget to progress on a project; I trust that I will make progress on everything little by little. A system like this might even interfere with my ability to make a decision for a next task (I would probably start comparing tasks and trying to choose those I can finish quickly or those I have barely worked on at all).

Perhaps this would help some of those who are having trouble figuring out which tasks "stand out".

Thanks for the link. I think I'll have a go at trying a circle notation in the margin rather than crossing out finished tasks.

In my AF notebook the messiness of crossed out items makes it hard to see the wood for the trees. My attention needs to be on the items that need doing but the items that grab my attention visually are the crossed out items.

Just looking at an ugly messy page full of crossed out items is enough to start the hackles of resistance rising and put me off looking at my AF notebook (or at least looking at the important messy pages containing mostly remaining high resistance items).

Just my £0.2. I'm sure people with neater writing find this less of a problem but does anyone have similar issues with lots of crossed out items?
