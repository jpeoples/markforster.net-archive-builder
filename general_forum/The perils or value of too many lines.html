I have about 300 notebooks I could use for AF, but when I decided to give a paper version a go, I chose one that has 40 lines per page, for some reason.

Starting out, this is far too many lines, so I cut the page in half with a line. However, now that this notebook has been going for a week, I find the twos pages on one thing to be a bit annoying. So I am starting to go back to viewing every 40 lines as a unit.

The main difference I find with this is there are many more tasks to do on each page before none stand out. Over time, the pages still get dismissed, and I move through all my tasks. I guess it helps me dismiss tasks a bit easier, as by that time, I have had lots of opportunities to select them.

I have read a number of posts on too few lines, but does anyone see any big issues with too many lines?

Chris,

The only issue I have with too many lines is that it slows progress through the list ... IOW, it can take longer to get to other pages. You might consider implementing a rule that forces you to move to other pages more quickly such as doing no more than X items on a page before moving on to the next page if that becomes a problem for you.

My rule of thumb is that I must review the entire list at least once a day. I do this by a defined review period where I JUST review the whole list (all pages) with no intent at all to do anything. Then if I see any gotchas on the list I cull them to a Post-it so they will be in my face that day and I'll not rely on serendipity to get me to the page they are on.

Of course, if I did ONLY one item on each page and then was FORCED to go to the next page, I could probably safely ignore that review process. The review process has the advantage of priming the subconscious ... however it tends to lead to prioritizing which is more against the spirit of the rules of AF than adding the rule to do no more than an arbitrary number of tasks on a page.
