In another thread, Seraphim wrote:

> I could never work with a limit of 50 items; I typically have 300-500 on my DWM
> list at any given time. (which is less than I had with AFx: almost never less than 1000).

Having retrieved my jaw from the floor, here's my own situation by contrast.

My current initative is to get working my DWM list faster - much faster. Mark said somewhere that one ought to get round the list daily. Someone else said that 60 items was their personal maximum for the list, and that sounds reasonable to me.

My list has typically been around the 115-item mark, and I've been taking a week to get round it. I just don't spend that much time on it daily. After work (self-employed) and resisting work, and after domestic life such as cooking, walking dogs, actually being in the company of my wife ... a long list is a deterrent. I tend to unbutton, flake out, binge on blogs or telly and let the list go hang. I want a shorter list.

So I'm going through it cutting out repetitions, items that have got done but not been removed, packing away project-related items into notes attached to a general "Work on X" task, and dismissing stuff without waiting for it to go over the waterfall.

I'm also altering the one-month period for first entry to three weeks.

All this for the sake of keeping the length of the list down and making it less depressing to contemplate.

Am I scrabbling down the wrong badger hole here?

Chris

Chris:

Although my list has about a hundred items, I am able to filter it by the three locations I use, Home, Studio or Office. When, e.g. I am at the Studio, I set that filter on and the visible list reduces to say 60. I have a single bookmark item that I drag round and round the list, which shows in all filters.

When I go home, I change the filter from Studio to Home, and carry on from where the bookmark happens to be.

Today's date I highlight red, the date a week from now is green (under which items that have been worked on are dragged), and the dates in between highlighted amber (traffic light coding). Every morning I delete the amber items, adjust the highlighting of dates and add a new date a month ahead for new items.
