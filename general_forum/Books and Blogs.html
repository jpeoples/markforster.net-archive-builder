Nicki,

It might not be what you are looking for but I first thought of the book "Deep Work" by Cal Newport when you mentioned "alternative approaches " I have been greatly influenced by the book and his blog is one that I have searched quite a bit for unique ideas on how to approach things. My main take-away was to work on ways to focus longer without distractions

I'm also glad you asked because it prompted me to think of other books (besides Mark's of course) that have helped me in the pursuit of getting better at doing... One I have referred to often is "The Practicing Mind" by Thomas Sterner. Chapter 6 seems to especially apply where he talks about the 4 'S' words: simplify, small, short, and slow. I really liked how he described a work day where he focuses on delibertly going slow and methodically and was surprised that he actually finished everything faster by simplifying and going slower.

Remembering these two books was a timely reminder for me that sometimes it is about my.mindset not just my tools or algorithms.

Brent

Some other books that have helped me with time management and related topics (in no particular order):
- Tom DeMarco - Slack
- Clarke Ching - The Bottleneck Rules
- Tim Harford - Messy - The Power of Disorder to Transform our Lives
- Eli Goldratt - The Choice
- Charles Duhigg - The Power of Habit
- Stephen Covey - The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People
- Peter F Drucker - The Effective Executive
- Andy Core - Change Your Day, Not Your Life

Some of them have one or two key ideas that really could have been communicated in an article. I'm thinking Andy Core's book especially. The book seems kind of repetitive after you grasp the initial concept.

Speaking of articles - here are a couple good ones:
- Dave Snowden, "A Leader's Framework for Decision Making" (on the Cynefin Framework) - http://hbr.org/2007/11/a-leaders-framework-for-decision-making Also see the Wikipedia entry http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cynefin_framework
