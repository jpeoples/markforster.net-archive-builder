Hi all,

Been through the forum and couldn't see this obviously anywhere, but feel free to post a link if this question has already been answered...

How do you remember to monitor for responses to things from other people? I could do the obvious thing and have it on my list, but when all is said and done, I can't do anything with this until it comes in. If it comes in, fine, I can then deal with it but it might be that I don't get a response for several weeks (which is perfectly valid in some cases), so do I simply dismiss the page if that is the final item, and re-add it to the last page (and do this repeatedly until I get the answer, or decide it's time to chase it)?

Hi Donna

My first posting.

Say I have to send an email and I expect a reply, I would write the email, cross of the original item "Email Fred" and add to the end of the list "Has Fred replied to email?". Then as I look through my lists, one page at a time, I find it's almost invisible to me and I skim past without a second thought, but becomes more obvious when a reply has become overdue in my own mind - without expressly putting a due date against it. Then I know it's time to chase for a reply.

I discuss it in a few posts. I used a W with a circle around it when I was doing AF and just left it there as a reminder of how long I was waiting for the item. Obviously you can use a calendar to schedule the due date.

I have also used the rule function in outlook so when you type [w] in an email you are sending it puts a copy in a waiting for folder automatically. This way when you email and ask someone for something you know a copy will be in the waiting for folder which is easy to review.

In my E book the Simple Guide to Time Management I identified Waiting for items as one of the key three things to track based on my experiences over the years with various TM systems and suggest a Waiting for list as a part of this simple system.

If you are managing a team of people it works really well to put reminders at the end of your list. It has completely solved my previous problem of delegating tasks and then never knowing if they had actually been completed. And it keeps my team on their toes too.

If you are working on bigger projects, as I do, this approach also helps to keep a paper (or email) trail of where the responsibility for an action lies. A reminder email, judiciously cc'd to relevant persons, works wonders...

I have to say that AF has completely transformed my efficiency - not just in getting tasks done in a timely manner, but in getting the less important ones done - these are key to creating a good impression with clients and under other systems they drop off the list as they are relatively low importance.

There is also a handy way to do this In Google Mail:

If you have the "labs" feature activated (settings), you can select custom "stars" to tag your email. For example, I have a red star for everything I have to respond to or do something, and the "orange-guillemet" (looks like ">>") whenever I am waiting for a response.

Then, I set up "quick links" (another labs feature) in the left pane for "waiting for". The quick link is basically a search for "has:orange-guillemet". I did the same with the red star ("Has:red-bang"). A nice tip from lifehacker - come to think of it, now all I need is an AF entry to check this "waiting" - list...

The way I follow up on emails is to blind copy the email to myself. When it pops back into my Inbox I decide how long it will take them to reply (it will normally be 2 days). I then have separate folders set up for each day like this:-

07 Sat
08 Sun
09 Mon

I then drag the email to the appropriate folder. When I'm clearing my email I always check the relevant day folder to see if anything is outstanding. Often the emails have been answered. Sometimes they haven't!

It works really well for me...
