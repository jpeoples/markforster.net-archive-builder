I'm in a similar position, but I'm going to try to maintain the system as one list. The reason for this is that I *can* do home tasks during the week, just not all that many of them (I work quite long hours). With AF, there was really too much choice when I got back home from work, and I tended to just slack-off. While that's normally fine, I like the 'hard' dismissal rules of AF2 - any given evening there's likely to be one or two home tasks which will be 'on notice' for dismissal, and that and the restricted choice of tasks could be just the motivation I need to get them ticked-off during the week.

If there isn't anything coming up for dismissal any time soon, then I can slack off without guilt (or add 'slack off without guilt' to the end of my AF list!). On the other hand, if some home tasks get dismissed, I can always pick them up when I do a weekly review at the end of the week, and do them on the weekend; at least the dismissal process will help me choose exactly what these tasks will be.
