I went back this weekend and reread Mark's book on "Do it Tomorrow". I have been here since those days and have gone through all of the versions of Autofocus. I know this is not great insight, but I think the current version of Autofocus -- version 4 -- is very much a hybrid and close to what DIT was all about. You have a backlog list you work and then your active list. Of course the rules are different and more flexible, which I like, but when you look at the basic tenets of DIT, you see AF4 in essence! So, Mark, we have come somewhat in a circle, back to an Autofocus version that one could say uses the basis of DIT as its foundation. And this is why I think this version is by far the best.

Mark, am I totally off in my assessment of AF4? What do others think?

2009-09-15 10:10
David:

I think the resemblances between DIT and AF4 are fairly superficial. Will has put his finger on the major difference - in DIT you are making a statement of what you will do, whereas in AF4 you are processing a list of tasks you may do.

I quite like the idea of allowing some work on tomorrow's list in DIT, but the major problem in implementation is the question of what you do on days in which you don't have time to work much on the list.
