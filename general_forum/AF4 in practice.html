Re: ANY list (either open or closed) you process according to the rule. IOW, don't worry about WHY you don't "feel ready" to do a task, just move on. IFF there is nothing on the list that you can do and the reason is that you are in the wrong place ... I would "pretend" that you did one and continue processing as usual. IOW, don't go through the exercise of reviewing items and deleting them just because you are not at home or wherever. So it would be option B ;-)

It is for this very reason that people will keep separate home and business lists. I think the complexity outweighs the advantage. I just do what I can when and where I can. In practice, I've never come across the situation where there was nothing left to do because I was in the wrong place.

I second Mike on how to deal with location-specific tasks. I work from home 2 days/week and in the office 3 days/week and the workflow is more balanced all around if I have just one list. Right now my closed list has a bunch of things I can't do in the office, so I'm just pretending they don't exist. ;^)

As for things that have to happen in the relatively-distant future: I put reminders in my calendar. For example, I have a reminder in my calendar for next September to call to schedule maintenance on the furnace (so that we can get it done before we have to turn the thing on for the winter). When that reminder pops up, I will cycle the task into my AF list and treat it accordingly. I do the same thing for things like sending out reminders for a monthly meeting and backing up my blogs - calendar events that "trigger" me writing the tasks in my AF book.

I am not consistent in how I deal with projects - it depends on the project and how my brain is working at any particular time. Sometimes I put every project-related task I can think of right into AF, other times I just put down the name of the project and maintain a list elsewhere. Which one works best depends on how I'm feeling about the project and how many other balls I'm trying to keep in the air at the same time.

About home/work tasks:

Why worry? You can mark them for review, and the next time you scan the list and find the highlighted tasks, you have the option to re-enter them at the end of the open list, if you consider that you couldn't work on them for a valid reason.

I try to be honest with myself, though, not just re-enter them without thinking why they weren't done. Sometimes, there are tasks that I simply won't/can't do. You can delete those, or move them to some kind of "freezer" list, or something.

In my case, after I highlight tasks, I usually have at least one day before I'm back to the closed list. Knowing that, I put an extra effort when I'm back home to "save" some tasks. It works. It really stimulates me to move things forward, even if a little (remember that just working a little on them is enough).

If I didn't have that "push", these tasks would stay stagnant forever, since when I'm home, I just want to have dinner, and watch a movie or something.
