I recently came across a term I hadn't heard of before, though I knew of the principle. The term is "house blindness." This refers to the ability of your brain to overlook clutter in your house, so that you don't "see" it.

Your house-blindness is cured, whenever you learn someone is coming over. All of the sudden, you see everything.

I think we get that way with our tasks. I think that might be the genesis of the arbitrary dismissal rules. It is the jarring effect that cures your task-blindness.

I think that the little-and-often principle goes a long way toward keeping one from contracting task-blindness. We should probably rely more on the little-and-often principle than rely on the "shock" of dismissal.

Just a thought.
