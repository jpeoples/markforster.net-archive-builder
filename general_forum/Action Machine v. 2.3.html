Derek Franklin has announced another version of the Action Machine. It's supposed to be the last for the time being, apart from bug fixes.

The author lists the following improvements/new features:

* The are a few cosmetic changes in reports (nothing big)
* When you place your mouse over the app icon in the System Tray,
it will now display information about any playing or paused timers
so you can easily keep track of your progress without opening
the entire interface (no info will be displayed if there aren't
any currently playing or paused timers)
* When you move a timer to a new timer group, it will be added as
the LAST timer in the group instead of the first, as before
* HERE"S THE BIGGIE!: Timers remain activated, playing, paused, or
completed when you reorder them, move them to a new group, or shut
down the application! YAY! :) The only thing to be aware of here
is that the countdown of time only continues when a timer is
visible on the screen.

See http://www.theactionmachine.com

2009-06-3 15:34
Hello Everyone,

Someone mentioned to me that the Action Machine was being discussed here, so I thought I'd join it! :)

First off, thanks to everyone who who purchased the Action Machine. I appreciate it.

It seems there are several questions/comments about the product and the 'Genius Mechanism' videos, so let me address those.

1. The Action Machine and resources - On my test machines, TAM uses very little resources, virtually 0 most of the time. I'm not sure why resources are being used to the extent some of you mention. I will say that TAM will use a lot less resources when minimized, as opposed to simply hidden behind another window. Please try that approach.

2. The interface isn't customizable - I'm a one-man business. As such, my time is limited, so I have to choose my battles. Adding interface customization to an application is one of the most time-consuming tasks in developing an app. Instead of spending too much time on interface customization, which I received 3 requests for over the last 2 months, I spent my time on adding things that I received a barrage of requests for. I wish I could duplicate myself. :)

3. No trial version - Once again, as a one-man business, supporting a trial version and dealing with people who want to hack it to get the app for free just isn't an option. I offer a 8-week no questions-asked guarantee to allow time for people to use it to the fullest. If they're not happy with it, I seriously don't ask questions when they request a refund.

4. Why not give it away for free? - When it comes to productivity, TAM is all I have to offer. There aren't any backend seminars, workshops or coaching I offer that would pay the bills otherwise. I'm still waiting to get adopted by Oprah, but until then, I have to charge for the software. :)

5. What's up with the 'Genius Mechanism' videos? - I felt, and still feel the videos offered a LOT of value! How many people would have watched them if I had said, 'look at these mind map videos'? Probably next to none! The system for collecting knowledge that I shared in the videos has helped me immensely over the years, and anyone who watched them and actually uses the strategy I laid out will see a dramatic increase in retention and understanding of the topics they choose to learn. True, if you're an avid mind mapper already, chances are you didn't learn anything new, but I'm pretty convinced that to 95% of the population out there, this was something new and valuable. I chose the name to convey an end-benefit to the viewer AND to pique their curiosity enough them to view it. The video doesn't do anyone any good if you can't drive them to at least click the Play button. Know what I mean?

That's it for now. :)

I'll check back here occasionally to answer any other questions anyone might have.

Once again, thanks for sharing your input about my little productivity app.

Have a great day!

Derek
