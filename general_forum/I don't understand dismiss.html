Hi Zack

By the time you get to the point of dismissing an item, you will already have had several opportunities to do it so that "the system" is identifying the fact that you don't intend to do that task at the present time. Those tasks need not be dismissed for ever. Just add a regular task to your list to "review dismissed items". If appropriate you can then readd any of those tasks back into AF.

Hope that helps .....

Put a question mark at the end of the item. If the answer is yes, add it to the end of the list and cross it off your current page (Mark details this somewhere on the discussion forum.)

Ex. Buy new bed

It's the last item on the current page but I won't have the funds for several more months. When ended with a question mark, the answer is yes so I process it accordingly.

Zack:

The instructions are actually quite clear about what happens to dismissed items:

"It doesn’t mean you can never re-enter them, but you should let some time pass before you do and consider carefully why they were rejected, whether they really need to be done at all, whether the time is ripe for them to be done, whether they distract from your main goals, and any other factors. When you do re-enter a dismissed item, it is often best to break it down or re-phrase it in some way."

Well, I think it's clear anyway!
