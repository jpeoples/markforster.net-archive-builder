I'm just getting started so I'll recap where I am so far.

I started with my Composition book ... I was using it for AF4 so I started a new one to use for this. I started dating the pages but got so horribly confused I had to find a better way.

Since I want some degree of portability I opted to use the Circa Compact (nominally 4x8 or so, I think). (I was already using that for my schedule ... calendar, daily lists, etc. I use the Circa Junior for my plans and projects.)

I started dating the pages. I already had the Jan-Dec dividers with one page for each with one page for key dates for each month. You know, one line per day for birthdays, tax filing dates, car registration renewal, etc. I also had some blank dividers I was not using so I took three of them and labeled them (in different colored inks) "Today", "Re-Enter), and "New". Those are the 1, 7, and 30 day points, respectively.

I decided to try dating them. It seemed to be a good thing to have them dated as I might also use them for some appointment things ... maybe in a box or a different colored ink. Mark seems against that but hell, it seems excessive for me to have two calendars going.

Then Alan made his brilliant observation that you could just number the pages 1-31 and forget dates entirely. This appeals to me because I could stop and start the system, as when I go on a vacation, with no trouble at all.

In talking with Jacqueline, I realized that I could ALSO just block out my vacation dates (when they get close) and skip over them as if they did not exist at all. That would work as well. I'm not sure what I'll do. I have my book dated up to the end of March so I don't need to decide anything yet. I'll need to decide when it gets to the 1st of March what to do about April and beyond. I may want to number rather than date the pages then. I'll see.

I took my old AF4 book and began going through it discarding a lot of stuff and feeding what I was going to get to into the 30 day point. Initially I just put a handful of items in. Gradually I've been adding more and more. I'm also not worrying too much about exactly where I put things. I know Mark mentioned that he did not care awfully about that either.

My pages are set up as follows: The Compact has places for 25 line entries, if you use every last inch. I drew a vertical line at 1.25 inches. That gives me two columns or 50 items. I have no clue how much I'll need. I started dating each PAGE. Now I'm thinking that I want each SHEET to be be a separate date. My thinking is that it will make the moving of the pages easier. Now I have two different days on each side of the page so it is still a bit confusing to move pages. It would be MUCH nicer to have only one day per sheet. I'll surely do that come April.

Maybe less important to you is the pen. I am portable so I want to put some kind of loop on the thing. There are things I can buy from Levenger but given how much they charge for junk, I'll probably make something. I also have an industrial strength rubber band I wrap around the book to keep it closed as I travel.

One problem I'll probably deal with later is the paper itself. With all the flipping around I doubt the ring holes will last. The paper is just not heavy enough. I can fix that by printing my own and punching it. If I do that, I'll type my tasks in when I can so they'll be mostly printed as well ... or maybe not. The downside of thicker paper will be a thicker book. right now I'm only keeping the pages I need plus a month. (IOW, right now I've got Feb, March, and April but no further.) I've already had to replace the rings the book comes with and move up to 1/2 inch rings. I may wind up at 3/4" if I go to heaver paper.

The other thing to note about this setup is that I MUST use a fine point pen and use VERY few words to get it all in 1.25 inches. It is tight but it will work.

Let me know what you come up with. I imagine you could work in the letter size format and maybe put more than one day on a page ... maybe even a week.

Oh, and as I mentioned elsewhere, Levenger sells calendar sheets for the various sized Circa books. Of course, the price is astronomical, most likely ;-)
