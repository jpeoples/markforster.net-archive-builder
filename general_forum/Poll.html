2010-05-8 13:34
Another way to look at it:

DIT and DWM, both diary-based, could be regarded as more structured approaches than AF 1-4, which in turn are simple list-based systems.
Based on the latest numbers (May 4), here are the "retention rates" for DIT and DWM on one side and all AFs combined on the other:

DIT/DWM: 45.3% (38 out of 64)
AF 1-4: 14.4% (28 out of 195)

This seems to show a strong preference for a more structured approach by many people, which is somewhat in contrast with many posts in the forum, where there seems to be a preference for AF1/4.

OTOH, the validity of this conclusion is questionable, as anyone who didn't stick to DIT, and went through all systems, has been through all of the AFs and arrived at DWM for the moment. So constant switchers will dilute the AF numbers, and strengthen those for DWM.
In addition, the May 4 numbers show that the majority *has* been through all of the AFs.
Regarding DIT: Given that DIT has been around the longest and wasn't made available on this website only, but also distributed by the book, it should be the system that has been tried by many more people than the others. Most DIT users, whether satisfied with the system and still using it or not, will not have taken the poll. Most will not even know it. Someone who tried DIT a long time ago and abandoned it, might not be visiting this website anymore.

So this "retention rate" thing is probably moot.

BTW: It would be nice if the additional comments (personal tweaks etc.) from the poll could be made available als well.

Just my 2p, and now back to re-starting DWM...
