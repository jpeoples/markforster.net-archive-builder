As I gather we are still beta testing DWM, here's my assessment.

The new dismissal rules have cracked the issue of the list which expands faster than it is processed. Mine went quickly up to 150 and then dropped to a steady 120-ish items which has been the list length for several weeks.

A caveat - I have found it necessary to use an electronic system for easy filtering by place. I can see a case for carrying paper and pen for quickly jotting down things for later re-entry.

While in a sense DWM is DIT, I have also found it to be AF, as the things which do not get deleted become apparent, over time, as the items of focus.

I recently took a 2 week unexpected break from both my home & work DWM lists. Getting back on track was much easier than I'd expected.

I reviewed the pages I missed and crossed out whatever got done 'offlist'. The stuff that was left, if it was something that was now time-urgent, I added it to a 'must do today' short list. Everything else was dismissed per DWM rules.

I added new stuff on either +30 or +7. I don't usually add new stuff to +7, but I was catching up the list so anything that was in progress while I was offlist I added to +7. Everything else went to +30.

Pages between yesterday (well, yesterday when I went through the list) and +30 didn't get changed. I didn't re-number or re-date the pages. I reviewed them to refresh my memory about what's on the list and I did cross-out whatever got done (and re-adding them to +7 as needed).

I am using paper, but do the dismissal part much like you describe if I have been forced off-list for one or more days.

Depending on whether I am actively working on clearing items from one of the pages that are up for dismissal, I occasionally give myself the latitude to let a page or two not get dismissed rather than rewriting to a "must do today" list. It lets me just work through the backlog without that distraction.

I try to keep this to only days when I have been forced off the list for some unexpected reason. But there have been times when I have worked through some items on yesterday's page that was up for dismissal prior to dismissing the rest of the page.

As long I don't let this get out of hand, it seems to make me feel like I am in control of the list instead of the other way around. I still dismiss many more items than in the other AF systems and don't seem to get as bogged down.
