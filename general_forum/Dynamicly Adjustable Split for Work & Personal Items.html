Hi All,

I work from home so do everything from one location. I wanted one list but also wanted a way to keep my work items a bit separate. I have an idea which I believe is an improvement over the Mark's suggestion of splitting the page in advance e.g. 2/3 Work, 1/3 personal.

It's very simple. For me I list my personal items from the top of the page (which were the majority over the weekend when I had the idea), but for my work items I start at the bottom of the page and work my way up. When and wherever the two meet, I draw a line in between.

I think having the line be dynamically adjustable depending on work-load and day of the week is very useful.

What do you think?

Jon

Mike,

There's a couple of reasons why it's helpful for me to group the tasks:

Although I work from home, I am employed, so have certain times when I'm supposed to be working (although I will do the odd personal in between work sessions).
Also, when my boss calls me and wants to know what's on my To-Do list, it's much easier to look at the bottom of all my pages, rather than go through every entry on every page.

Most of my tasks are in fact personal items. But a lot of the personal items are routines which can be done very quickly. I have a lot less tasks listed for work items, but these are usually more time-consuming tasks.

So you see, to set the size of the split in advance does not give me any balance, because balancing the number of tasks in work/personal is not the same as balancing my time in work/personal.
