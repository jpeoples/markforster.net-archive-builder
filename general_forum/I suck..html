I also have the habit of throwing myself into an idea as soon as it pops into my head -- mainly because I have always been afraid that if I didn't act on it immediately, I would lose it, since I didn't have a trustworthy system.

But now, I just add it to the AF list. And I am SO GLAD that I do that! When I finally do come across that idea on the AF list, half the time I think, "what a hare-brained idea, what in the world was I thinking?", and I am so glad that I didn't waste a couple hours taking action on it immediately. The "thrill" of that idea has completely gone, and is replaced by the thrill of doing what's really important to me instead.

When I come to that item in the AF list, and I still want to do it, that means it *wasn't* a hare-brained idea (or at least, not so much of one), and I have the AF system to help me track it and prioritize it.
