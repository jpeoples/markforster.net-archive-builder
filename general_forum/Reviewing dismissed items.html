RevDave,
You could use another of Mark's techniques - the 'current initiative' - where you do some work on the backlog or project every day, before you work on anything else. I've been using this approach with a short timed burst most mornings to tackle a lengthy and boring 'clear the clutter' task, and it's making it surprisingly easy.
Or you could enter 'do one item from AF1 list' at the end of your AF2 list every day. Good luck.

RevDave,

How often to review? As often as seems right. ;-) I'm leaning toward putting items on my list that I EXPECT to do within one week ... I need to experience that longer to see how it plays. In that case, my inclination is to review at weeks end when I review things and decide what to do next week. Some have mentioned that they prefer to let dismissed items cool their heels for a month or so. Basically I think a good rule is:

- Put items on the list that you are committed to doing within a "reasonable" time (defined by your unique circumstance.

- Review the dismissed items at the end of that period to see if anything is really screaming to get back on the list. (Remember you have to COMMIT to doing it in the upcoming time frame so some will not even ask for readmittance because you'll realize that the time is not ripe for them.)
