Hi Mel
My brain isn't really up to par today but I'll try to highlight it. You do it almost identically to daily DIT except you're you're pulling your daily MIT's from last week's incoming and project progress charts. Of course, you still can add something tomorrow that came in today if it would be a better use of your time. Afterall you have a week to finish the stuff!
And, when absolutely necessary, there's the below the line option. Below the line is also great when you finish your work early and you want to free up another day.
Because it's a weekly format, you need to have strict office hours. Otherwise, Monday through Wednesday, your leisure time might get sacrificed in attempt to churn out a weeks worth of work. The checklist covers the regular occuring chores and acts as a vehicle to conveniently batch your work. (email, inbox, calls, etc) I use round-up days for my broad categories.(Accounts round-up, Projects review/plan round-up, Detail Housework round-up , etc) On the exact day, that category must be completed but I can chip away at it on previous days.

The beauty part is that it's VERY flexible and also it ensures that your work doesn't bleed into your leisure time unless you pre-scheduled over-time to free up a future day(s)...example: vacation , all day special event, or an all day work party. The flexibility also accomdates emergencies, opportunities, wellness, sickness, etc. The flexibility aspect works with your resolve and aims, not against it.

Also the flexibility allows you the ease of doing your normal habits and giving the ebb and flow of life to happen with a welcome attitude while maintaining control over nonsense distractions or wasting time on piddling stuff at the expense of what you deem to be worthwhile (even if it's not the exciting stuff)

You learn to measure your efforts against your aims within the work hours allotted. That alone keeps reminding me the reason I'm doing the scut....to free myself up for the grander aspects of living...........inviting the passion of your life's work, cuddling a baby, learning something new, taking in a cultural event, sharing a cool one with friends, community work, etc...whatever you consider the grand aspects of living.
