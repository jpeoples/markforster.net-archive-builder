Leon,

Your items are a sort of mix of various items and systems. It looks like you really have a list of two or three projects. I am not sure the difference between 2 and 3. If you were to use the new system, you would take everything and put it on either the backlog list, the goal list or into the diary at a future date. The 10K run would be put on a goal list and then you would put on today's page go for a 3 mile run or whatever you needed to do to get ready for the race. Selling the chair is actually on the goal list, as it does not have to be done, unless it does to have to be done, ie to pay for a necessity. You would also plan your day making sure you get today's work done and if time permits, then items from the backlog.

I actually think this system would be very helpful in your instance, but you would have to stick to it and forsake all others to give it a shot at working. I think it is actually a bit more focused than the other systems I have written and is good for getting lots of tasks done and reducing backlogs.

Lean & Smileypete

I tried to stay away from the idea of deadlines and focus on must be done or would like to do. If it has to be done, the deadline will be known. If it never has to be done, but you would like to do it, you 1. keep current with your work, 2. reduce the backlog and then 3. work the goals into your life. So things that you would like to do but don't have to be done, by definition do not have a date, so as long as you are current on your work and the backlog is reducing, you work these items in as time permits and as you feel.

I have generally used Do It for much longer term goals once I have decided positively to do them. The idea of the new system is for things to be tracked and worked on based on time and intuition. This is more akin to a someday/maybe list in GTD versus an absolute must do life changing goal which is what Do It is about.

Personally I separate work from personal, so I am working on my Do It stuff at defined times of the day like lunch and after work or on the weekends, so there is a separation. This was something I wrote about in one of the ebooks maybe the first one. But, for me it works better to use one of the time management systems for work and Do It for the personal big goals. On these, I don't really need time management, I need the focus of the Do It system to identify them and stay on track over a longer period of time until they are accomplished.

No worries about negativity, I appreciate the feedback. I think how you treat the goals list can be a personal decision. The goal is never to have another backlog. The backlog list is a closed list. You are supposed to put the new work into the system in a manner that leaves enough time to get it done with some "bandwidth" for the backlog list and goals list to get get placed into the system and reduced. If you can not keep up, like Mark suggests, you have too many commitments and no time management system can really fix that over the long term.
