I was going to put this in another thread about using SFv3 in the IT world but I think it may deserve it's own thread.

I believe that there is a good SFv3 habit we can all benefit from. It's the condition of when things go weird.... How do you react to it.

What I mean by this is:

You have a Task you start to work on it because it calls to you. When you get started on it you believe that to get it done you will:

"Do X, Y and then Z and then you are done."

However, when you start to work on it you discover that "Y" isn't working. When that happen it's in our nature for some of us to dig in and work heads down to try and fix "Y" or figure out how to work around the impasse to "Y"

Instead it may be better to realize that the task has just gone WEIRD. Stop working on it; cross it off and re-enter in in Column 2 of the next page as "figure out why Y isn't working" and them go back to the current page.

Basically the task has changed and it's duration has just changed. When you picked it up you had an internal commitment and expectation of what you are about to do; but that's just changed; you have done what you could and now it bares reconsideration it may not be the right thing to do just then based on time available and brain power available.
