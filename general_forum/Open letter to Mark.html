I appreciate the desire to understand the rules, however, the reality of this system and others by Mark is they are not like GTD in the sense that you could create a flow chart that gives you a 100% certain way of dealing with everything life may throw at you.

Mark has purposely designed systems with much less overhead than GTD. The result is that you get up and running faster, learn the system quicker and spend less time maintaining it. However, the trade off is that there may be instances where there is not a hard and fast rule to deal with a situation you are in. Personally, I have simplified my systems and use only a couple of rules. I trust my instincts to guide me as to what I need to do and what I can dismiss and when. I do not look to rules for this. I know if I write my list in one place and maintain a simple A - Z filing system I will be about as productive as I can be. I work on what I feel like I need to work on and dismiss something when I feel it needs to be dismissed, not according to some rule that the page is full or some other criteria. I am not saying this to be critical of SF, but to reiterate that one needs to view a system in light of their own needs and skills. While I tend to think people tweak too much too early, the reality is there is no perfect system for everyone and you must figure out what works for you. As J Krishnamurti said, "Truth is a pathless land."

Gerry
