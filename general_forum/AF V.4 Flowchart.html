Hi Grant,
Thanks for that. I was wondering when someone would do a flowchart of AF4.
I think you've missed a few bits out.
When you finish the closed list (I think Mark has suggested changing the name from Backlog to closed list), IF you have worked on any item on that pass then you need to go back through the closed list again. And again, and again - as long as you keep working on at least one item in a pass through. Only when you pass through it without working on any item do you get to move to the active list (where you are only allowed a single pass).
Also, if your very FIRST pass through the closed list doesn't lead to any item being worked on, then any of the un-crossed-out items are highlighted for review on the next pass through (after marking the new closed list and working through it, and any new active list).

Does that make sense?
It's a simple and elegant system, but strangely difficult to capture in a flowchart.

I believe I understand what Grant is asking, and I think the answer is yes. If you get to a task in the Active list and work on it but don't finish (or know that you will need to do again soon anyway), then you cross it off and re-enter it at the end of the Active list, which means, obviously, that you will hit this task again before reaching the end of the Active list. I actually love this, as it works very well for things like "process email," which I want to deal with on a regular basis but with other tasks in between.

Heya fellow flowchart geeks, I'm here to join the party with my own contribution! :)

http://screencast.com/t/vyb2qzZP

I made mine with the intention that it will guide me in everyday use, so it doesn't include information about how to set up the list in the first place and it doesn't explain much about the difference between open and closed lists -- it just shows what to do when working through them and transitioning between them. It also doesn't really specify where to start, although item in the top left corner was purposely chosen to remind me that the point is to DO the things on the list, not just have an awesomely designed list with a great system that might maybe help me get through them.

I also coined a term for myself that sums up the notion of a task feeling ready to do: I consider it to be RIPE. This evokes the idea of a juicy fruit hanging from a tree, ready to be plucked and eaten. Yum! I also use the term when I am dismissing items on the Closed List if I made a pass through without doing any: I say the highlighter 'ripens' the tasks. They must then either be done on the next pass, or dismissed, or revised and readded, or whatever, but the point is that they must be handled some way or another or else they'll start to rot and stink and nobody wants that! :)

I also tend to scribble out processed tasks completely (because it's fun!), so if a dismissed task might be worth doing someday, I have to add it to a completely different list. (I use Toodledo, and might eventually figure out a way to use it for AutoFocus, but I first want to work from paper so I can get a feel for what I need to make Toodledo do. If you want to check out Toodledo via my referral link -- http://www.toodledo.com/index.php?ref=td470bfd1829679 -- and if you end up liking it and become a paying customer like I am, you'll bless me with a 3 month extension on my account.) This also means that I don't have anything in the flowchart about how to handle highlighted tasks: they are marked as ripe, and then I am forced to deal with them, one way or another, on the next pass. And then I scribble them out furiously!

I designed my flowchart of appropriate proportions to print on an index card, but I zoomed in for the screenshot I took of it so it would be easier to be read. It's a PNG so it should be able to be shrunk down for printing at whatever size you like.

I might revise it a bit as I get practice using the system, but I felt like sharing this now even though it's a work in progress. I should point out that this sharing of the flowchart isn't actually ON the list, and so I really shouldn't be letting myself do it at all! I must still be procrastinating tasks on my list still... but I am giving myself to be a work in progress too.
