Have you guys done this or are doing this while working? It seems that, in doing tasks, to visualize or imagine what I am doing A FEW SECONDS INTO THE FUTURE with REDUCED (not totally removed) attention to what I am doing presently has actually improved my work: work is done faster and better with less mistakes.

I mean it is weird. It is as if, by imagining I am doing what I will be doing a few seconds from now, I avoid mistakes because I can SEE the results of whatever I imagined to do. Also, it seems to give myself some kind of "muscle memory" or something because once I imagined what I will be doing I need minimal attention to actually do it with smoother actions. Finally, it removes distractions from stray thoughts because my imagination (the main cause of my distractions previously) is now engaged in my work: I actually am more focused in my work than before in spite of having LESS attention to what I am doing PRESENTLY. Yet I will still catch any impending problems in my present work, as if I can intuitively catch any impending troubles.

Weird....but is this how you guys work? I just caught myself doing this a few weeks ago, and ever since I have consciously used this way of working.

I refrained from commenting on this thread for some time because, honestly, I lost it. The day right after I posted this thread I could not replicate the feeling of concentration I had using this! I honestly felt bad about it, so I put it in the back burner of my mind for the time being.

Then, right after dropping off my nephew at school today, while I was still in the parking lot, I remembered Alan Baljeu's post about "Micro Goals": http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1302019 What if I made "microgoals" in my mind while I was doing something, concentrate on the microgoal two steps away, and when that microgoal is my next step concentrate again on the microgoal two steps away?

Well, I tried it...on the drive back to my house (I know, I know, it's foolish to try a new procedure on something so dangerous as driving, but I made sure I did this gradually and carefully, so no one flame me, please? ^___^). While I was driving, I imagined markers on the lane I was driving on using shadows along it (it was morning so the shadows are quite long). I concentrated on getting to the marker that was just beyond the marker nearest to me, then when that marker is the one nearest to me I concentrated on the next further away, on so on. I also used markers on where I should turn, etc. And what do you know, the concentration I had before was back: no distractions, no daydreaming, no accidents just pure graceful concentration on what I was doing. When I got home I used the microgoals again for each of the chores there, and it was still working.

I got the groove back ^__^ Thanks Alan! And I think that is the easiest way to get to this level of concentration: divide your current task into microgoals, concentrate on getting to the microgoal two steps away, change to the next microgoal when the previous one becomes your current microgoal.
