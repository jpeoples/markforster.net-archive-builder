A few years ago I read some studies that showing that writing and speaking are equally effective forms of expression, in terms of their therapeutic value. Thinking is singularly ineffective,.

So, each morning I would turn on a voice recorder and talk while I drove (alone) to work. I did that for about two years. Eventually I stopped. Now I use the time to listen to books or magazines on my Kindle.

I do think that the time was well-spent, though. And I still spend time "free-writing" every workday. In my case the research seemed correct, writing and speaking out loud are equally helpful.

I think, and there is evidence to back this up (see books by James Pennebaker as well as Mark Forster) that emotional well-being is enhanced by expressing thoughts and feelings. As I mentioned, I still write my thoughts and feelings daily. I usually do not save what I write.

Likewise I would record my out-loud talking. But I would erase it completely almost all the time.

You can call it journaling, or self-coaching, or being your own therapist.

There's a lot of evidence that sitting with someone regularly and talking about your thoughts and feelings improves mood. That person can be a coach, therapist, religious leader, or a sham therapist who has been trained to nod meaningfully and occasionally ask, "And how did that make you feel?"

There is also evidence that this benefit can be had by speaking or writing your thoughts or feelings without there being another person present.

NLP for the car commute:

on my commute I experiment with using parts of the car as NLP "anchors" - I find an open road and when safe I can daydream; gently projecting my future ideal self onto the windscreen, my inner dialogue I imagine coming from the speakers, and the boot is the baggage I no longer need. I visualise the air flow and light flowing thru my body to clear the cells of junk. You get the idea.

Personally I believe speaking and writing help turn the mental and emotional toward the physical for creating the future.

For pure creativity exercises I enjoyed Julia Cameron's "Vein of Gold" which has many exercises which are great fun.

For me self-coaching and NLP state change and journaling have been life-improving skills.
