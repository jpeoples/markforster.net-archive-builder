AF4 is sweet.

I have a little trick I'm using that you can add to your arsenal for overcoming resistance. I now call my list (but only to myself) my "Adventure Field" (ie. "A.F.").

There's already a bit of excitement in working the list, but by simply "relabeling" it, it helps to focus me on "expecting something good".
My AF list is full of different 'adventures' that I can go on.

Of course, it helps to keep adding little "treasures" in the list too - don't only add turgid dry work, but things that you really want to do as little treats for when you're feeling you've done enough other stuff to justify it (eg. 'read a chapter of xxx', 'look at AF forum', '10 mins online holiday research'). For me, 'tidy desk' functions like one of these 'treats'...but maybe that's just me.

Along a similar line, working out a way to make a task sound fun can help, as can directly attaching a reward to the completion of the task. (eg. 'When I've finished xxx, I can open those biscuits'). Clearly, too many of these will just clutter things, but a few can help.

The challenge is as much about our attitude to the list as it is to keeping track of everything.

Well, this treat's finished. Time to return to my Adventure Field. I wonder what's ready to be picked?
