Alex

I think the best thing you can do is to jump right in and start using the system!

You scan down the first page once. Then a second time more slowly until an item catches you eye that feels right to do now. Once you've done enough on that item cross it out. If it's not finished add it to the last page. Then scan the page again, I think Mark suggests from the next item after the one you've just crossed out. If nothing catches your eye then start again at the top of the page and continue down to the item just crossed out. If in those scans something catches your eye continue on that item as for the first item.

Do this repeatedly until nothing catches your eye. Then on to the next page.

Remember you only HAVE to do one thing on the page and ONLY for as long as feels right i.e. it can be the tiniest thing 'just get the file out', before you cross it out and add it again to the last page. This keeps to the little and often effect which helps reduce or remove resistance.

Read the other quick start instructions and don't worry too much about getting it exactly right straight away. It's more important to do it that do it perfectly.

As questions arise, try to figure out the answer but check with the forum when you need to.

And no, I wouldn't add all your backlogs. Add tasks as they occur and as you start processing your pages (start with just a few pages say) you will automatically start adding things to the bottom of the last item on the last page as things occur to you. Over time you can start feeding in backlog tasks as it feels right to do so.

I hope this helps and that I have interpreted everything correctly.

Good luck

Alex:

Hannah and Christine have given good advice, but just to confirm here are the answers to your questions:

1) Correct: you read through the page you are starting only. However I recommend that you read through the entire list whenever you have been away from it for an extended period.

2) No, you continue going round the page from top to bottom until you have done a full pass from top to bottom without doing any items. Only then do you move onto the next page. N.B. You can't find that forum post because it doesn't exist!

3) If your backlog is really as big as you say it is then by all means deal with it as in DIT. But make sure that any re-entries or follow-ups of items in the backlog go into the main list.
