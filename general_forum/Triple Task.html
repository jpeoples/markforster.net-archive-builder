Hi Leon,

I saw the American Digest site and it is in my opinion very much like the TT page

I am still following triple task as I outline on my website and it is working great. If something moves from capture to TT page I do cross it out of the capture section. If for instance I need to call someone and I get their voicemail, I will cross it off of the TT section and will write in the capture section, W - Phone call from Bob. When reviewing the capture section my mind lets me know if the call back is overdue and I need to put into TT call Bob again.

I follow a few rules.

I add tasks to the TT page when there are one or less (ie I did all three).

I never write a task directly onto the TT page, I always put it into the capture section.

This rule serves as a buffer so I am sure it really is an important task to do. Also, I am very careful to write the Triple Tasks as tasks that can be completed in a day or less. This way you rarely end a day with unfinished tasks on the TT page. I am not using it to put things like work on project X. I define what I am going to do on Project X that can be completed that day. So I would not write work on building new house, I would write call three architects and ask them to send their qualifications.

The TT page may end up having several items a day on it, just 0-3 at a time.

This system is the best I have worked and really ties what I was trying to do with my previous ebooks together. The key is in the disciple to stick to the TT page and its rules, the capture give me peace of mind to know I have everything identified.

TT is really very simple and easy to use. Good luck

The capture section could be for reference info, like notes, but since the notebook is ultimately used up and noted filed, you may want to keep checklists somewhere else so you are not rewriting them when the notebook runs out.

Reply by Mark Forster

2010-11-22 14:57
DS:

Yes, you can in principle use 3-T with any other system acting as a feeder of tasks to it. Or you can use it on its own.

As for SuperFocus, the right hand column is not intended to be a 3-T column. It is an extension to the page, so that you can add additional tasks to an already full page or re-enter unfinished tasks so you can do some more work on them before moving on to the next page.

When you are working a page in SuperFocus, all the tasks on the page are available to be worked on - whichever column they are in.
