I've done this extensively and my conclusion is it's better to do minimally. Do with some key items you will work on where there's no confusion about what to do next or if something is better off dismissed. Do when there's a natural group of tasks that is useful to examine at once. Often it's good to take that list and write the next action y in SF beside the list reference.

But don't do too much of this things stop standing out.
