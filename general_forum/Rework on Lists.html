My elder son started a company late last year, I've been giving him a hand on various issues.

It's early days, but it's been a fascinating journey so far. In the UK, Gareth and me, in South Africa his colleague in Durban and HIS colleague in Cape Town. The two latter guys run a web development company, so they've helped set up the web site and other related stuff.

They encouraged us to use a couple of applications that facilitate co-operation and progress via the Web. One of these applications is Basecamp, a project management and communications facility, developed by 37signals; no, I have no idea from where the software people got that name..

It's been quite a learning experience for an old dog like me, but it IS amazing to work firsthand with tools one only reads about usually.

The folk of 37signals have just brought out a second book, Rework, on lessons learned and how others could go about things. Yes, it's another business success story book, but this one is quite good.

Here is an extract from their 1.5 pages on Lists which illustrates a couple of aspects which we know about on this forum, thanks to Mark's original ideas:

"Long Lists don't Get Done" is the section title on page 126, with a tasks checklist in the background.

"Long lists are guilt trips. The longer the list of unfinished items, the worse you feel about it. . . . . There's a better way. Break down that long list into a bunch of smaller lists."

Sounds like DWM to me. more so than AF!

More: "Yes, you still have the same amount of stuff left to do. But now you can look at the small picture and find satisfaction, motivation, and progress. That's a lot better than staring at the huge picture and being terrified and demoralized. . . . . Don't prioritize with numbers or labels. . . . . Instead prioritize VISUALLY (my capitals)."

Have a look for this book at the airport, better than Dan Brown and other textual chewing gum. These guys are successful mavericks.

www.37signals.com/rework

That's an interesting observation which I recognise because I have been doing DWM manually for the last 10 or so days and obeying the rules, most of the time. I have to be honest and state that my ListPro version was not done as well, so for me for the foreseeable future, manual/paper is the best so far of any task management system I have ever used.

When I started the current way, I did so in accordance with the rules, but I have to say it's worrying to see so many pages from the original 28 days (which is my timespan) with so little on them.

As others have sensibly advised, dumping all the tasks on Day 28 from the start is not advisable. Instead of spreading them over the "new" 28th day, I have indeed spread them over the original 8 - 28 days where possible.

I am also entering a few tasks in today or a bit later as they do need to be done soon. It's a practical tweak which I try to minimise as the beauty of DWM is the closed list effect on pages 1 - 6, 8 - 27.

It's working well so far and I'm now tracking the stats to make sure I do improve my performance, eg yesterday saw 17 tasks on the page, 15 were dealt with, 2 re-entered on day 7.

In setting up a convenient way to measure my DWM progress, I have noticed the tickler effect AND in advance, as you point out, SP!

I want to share my manual/paper methodology later on once it has been running for ab out two months.

However, I will still use my computer for hard dates, reminders and for breaking projects down into manageable chunk and tracking these, unless manual/paper proves better in projects as well.

I will cease the cheating practice when I get to the original day 29, around 28 April
