It would seem that the less I use autofocus, the less effective it is. When AF4 came out, I used it willy nilly with poor results. For the past couple of weeks, I've tried using it for at least one hour a day. The results are much better. When I do tasks outside of AF, the internal mechanisms of intuition & pressure are dulled when I return to AF. There's this little voice in my head going: "Eh, you don't need to do any of these items now because you can always do them later... when you're not using autofocus".

Perhaps Mark's approach is best - AF all day, all night.
