Hi Marc,

I do still use the current initiative approach in my working with AF. And I do end up with new tasks and complete ones on my AF list as a result of working on an iniative. What I do is this. I keep a small notebook -- not my AF notebook -- but a smaller one just for this purpose -- on my desk that I use only for my initiatives work. I add the date when I am working on the initiative and then under that, if new tasks come up as a result of this dedicated work, I add those here. If I have completed specific tasks during my work on the initiative, then I add those here as well and indicate that they were completed.

Once done, I go through my AF lists and look to see if any of the tasks that I completed during the initiatives work are here -- if so, then I cross them off accordingly. I then add the new tasks to the last active page of my AF lists.

This works quite well for me and takes very little time. An added benefit is that I have a running diary if you will of my initiatives work by date.

At any rate, I hope you find this helpful.
