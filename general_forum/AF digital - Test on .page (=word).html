Hmmm...does seem like a lot of stuff. I know you have written about your experiences elsewhere, but have you considered the following:

1. If its part of a project, collating individual tasks into a separate project action list? That would help you become "project-centric" and allocate your big rocks into focused project action tasks;

2. Checklists for regular or upkeep tasks, something Nunty talks about in another post. I remember Mark talking about batch processing related tasks - emails, paper, etc. Both are similar concepts which I have found useful. For example, a financial upkeep task could be a list consisting of (1) checking bank account (2) reconciling statements (3) budgeting for the month (4) doing ad-hoc transfers.

Just some ideas. Apologies for the repetition if I've talked about things you may have described in other posts and are already doing.

Big Rocks I think are nothing more than our own intuition wanting to pick out items or projects that are important to us. So by maybe having a less detailed AF list and a separate detailed projects list can help you de-focus and choose the big rocks that are important to you.

One thing strikes me about most of the conversations here.
Sometimes, we try to chuck everything to 1 system hoping it will solve all our needs.
I don't believe there is such a thing...

Why?
Well, every system is designed to do one thing very well and that's why it works.

AF if a system to help you make decisions about what's important to you.
It helps with the flow of work and relieves procrastination to a very high degree.
All that at an "actionable" level.

It is NOT a documentation, nor a planning system.
But it is a very good system that engages your mental faculties,
your intuition and your feelings to choose what to tackle next.

So AF is a good springboard to decide which project or activity you will do next considering your present situation. Once you start on that next thing to do, you will probably need another system to let you know what has happened already about that and what's next...

If people want to master Life or Time management, they will need a few tools and knowledge. Think of AF as the motor in your car that will make things move. But you'll also need a map to know where you are going right? Does a master carpenter use a hammer to cut a piece of wood? No, he takes a saw... Same thing if you want to master the discipline of time management.

Sometimes, using the wrong tool can be detrimental...
Don't use a spoon to dig a trench and a shovel to eat your soup.
Both LOOK and ARE basically the same, but they are designed for different purposes!
