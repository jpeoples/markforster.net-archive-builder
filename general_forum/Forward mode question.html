vhj,

Yes, I think it is a feature. Just as you say, you have to bite the bullet and do at least SOMETHING on at least ONE of the items on that page.

Of course, as Mr. Forster mentioned in another thread on these forums, one has the ability to completely delete items before they even come up for review if one knows these items are unactionable (i.e. circumstances change, you already did it, you don't want to do it anymore, someone else did it).

The caveat, I suppose, is that one must be careful of the PHENOMENAL COSMIC POWER of being able to just delete an item from the list, because one might be using that technique as a way to procrastinate or to otherwise undermine the review process of AF.

Anyone have any thoughts on the above? Am I on the right track?

veg, I think you are assuming that you will not want to do any of those page 1 tasks for the next week. Why is that? Is it an assumption or more of a concern? If it's the latter, try to trust that your intuition will tell you what is the best thing to do and when. Remember that using reverse mode in no way prohibits you from reaching page 2 in a timely fashion. When I am feeling this way, I very quickly skim backward through the list until I hit those page 2 tasks that are now begging to be done.

vegheadjones:

<< For the past few days, I have decided to switch to forward mode. I take one look at this page, say thanks but no thanks to all the items, then move back to reverse mode. >>

You may of course be exaggerating for the sake of effect, but if that is literally what you are doing then it's not surprising you are getting stuck there. What you should be doing is reading the items on the page through once, then going through them again one by one to see if one feels ready to be done.

Assuming that you *are* doing it correctly, then Mel has picked up the salient fact that you have eleven items on the page which you are convinced you are not going to do for the next six days. If you *know* you are not going to do them, then delete them all completely and have done with it. This is not a matter of waiting until the items come up for review. There's no point in reviewing them since you know you are not going to do them. Delete them for good and all.
