Hi Mike

One of the things I like about AF is that it is infinitely flexible as to what tasks you enter, and I think that, to a fairly considerable extent, the tasks we list will be linked to the benefits we perceive or need at any given point in time. Certain tasks I will do automatically, at other tasks those same tasks will find their way onto my lists. The reason for that is really nothing to do with the task itself but my own mental attitude to it at the time.

It's more akin to the resistance aspect of the task. If I have a resistance to a task, I know that the very act of putting it on my list will force me to confront the reasons for that resistance, but in a safe and non-threatening way. Rather than the "I don't want to think about that, I'd rather just grit my teeth and do it" approach, we can identify the reason for the resistance in an "oh, that's interesting" sort of way and we then have the choice whether to do anything about it or not. But at least we then know we are making that choice.........
