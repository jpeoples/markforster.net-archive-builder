Hello everyone,

I hope no one will hate me for this ... I stumbled upon AF through the Lifehacker article and was immediately hooked by its simple elegance. I started filling a lined pocket moleskine, but soon found myself wondering how I could use AF digitally. Since none of the available options seemed quite right to me, I decided to implement my own digital autofocus system.

I have reasonable skills in PHP and MySQL, so I downloaded the latest version of Zend Framework and got coding. Four days later, I had a basic system that does most things I need. Then I decided to share my work with anybody who was interested, added user accounts and uploaded it all to freshly registered domain.

So now I'd like to invite everyone to test it out: http://autofocus.cc

Features as far:
- Add tasks to end of list (duh)
- Configurable amount of tasks per page
- Re-add done or dismissed tasks with one click
- Jump to next page OR jump to next active page
- Forces you to do at least one task or dismiss page (Some people will not like that one)

Here is a screenshot: http://autofocus.cc/public/img/screenshot.png

It's not perfect, it doesn't look too good yet (I designed it with mobile devices with low resolutions in mind, I plan to make a shinier and faster AJAX-Version), it may explode the moment more than two people it. With you help, I will try to bring it closer to being a good autofocus implementation, if ...

Mark, I hope you are okay with me doing this. I kinda got carried away, and now I realize that you might be against this. If you don't want me to host this service, especially under the autofocus.cc name or at all, I understand and promise to take it down immediately. I didn't want to steal your ideas or anything, I give you full credit on the page, I just thought it would be cool to have an AF web app instead of the nth GTD web app.

Regards,
Andreas

Reply by Andreas

2009-02-19 18:32
Laurence:

For now, there is only one list per account. I plan to add a multiple list feature in the future. So far I never thought about having a task on two lists at the same time.

SInce I use a relational database, I would implement that in the following way:

- a user-list (1:n) relation
- a user-task (1:n) relation (that's what I have now)
- a user-list (1:n) relation
- a task-list (m:n) relation

The app would then feature to operations for a task:

- move to other list
- copy to other list

Moving would remove the old task-list relation and create a new one, copying would also create the new relation but keep the old one.

If I then mark a task is done, it is marked done in all lists, since exists only once in the database.
