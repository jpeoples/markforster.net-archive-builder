Hi Henrik,

I use a "Composition Book", one of those sewn books with a marbled cover that school kids use. Just recently they were on a blow out back to school sale and I got some for $0.50 and others for $0.25! Beats the hell out of those over priced $20 books LOL ;-)

If you search this site you'll get all kinds of good info. Basically, people here have tried EVERYTHING ;-) I can tell you from experience (mine and others) that simplest is best. The more colors you use; the more tags; the more symbols ... the more fussing you end up doing and the less work. That said, some things that seem to work are:

- Put a "-" or "." or "[ ]" to the left of the item. This can be used to indicate which thing you are working on and then altered to mark the task as complete. So if you use a "-" for example, you can turn it into a "+" when the task is done. If you extend that to the full height of the line, you end up with a long vertical line connecting done tasks and it is easier for your eye to pick out what has yet to be done. Likewise if you have a "[ ]" you can fill it in and then you'll have a long vertical bar if you connect them.

- Put (date) after the item if it has a due date of some kind.

- Put a small horizontal line (maybe on the left side of the page) to indicate the date the following items were added. (I'm not sure what anyone gets out of this, except if you want to develop some kind of statistics.)

- Put (reason for doing task) after it. This seems to help some as it reminds them of the WHY of the task and that can help in procrastination.

What has not worked well for many people:

- Tags (such as "work", "home", "project xyz". It just clutters things up.

- Lots of colors and highlights. YMMV, but it is visually cluttered with all of those marks.

- Progress indicators or status indicators. There have been some elaborate systems of symbols to indicate how much work has been done and who it was delegated to, etc. Again, the visual clutter takes over.

In all of this, YMMV, of course, but we've been at this a while (almost a year now) and have tried EVERYTHING ;-) The bottom line is that simple is king.
