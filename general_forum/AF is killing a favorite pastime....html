Hi Avrum,

First of all, I usually avoid sites like that. There is seldom any useful information in them and almost never anything new. It seems that people love to re-publish things others have written, to get themselves exposure. This results in the same stuff appearing over and over. A real time waster.

As to the specific advice, that has been the core of my system forever, and continues to be. AF is what I use when I have free time that is not scheduled. The important stuff is scheduled or it will not get done. I usually plan tomorrows work at the end of the day and then just review my plan, in the morning, making a minor change or two if necessary. I don't review every hour, but I did pick up the advice: "Ask Lakien's question" from his book. The question, at every break or task switch is: "What is the best use of my time -- right now?" That takes less than a minute ;-)

Too true Avrum, I decluttered 90% of my self help (including time management) books after a few months of AF. I kept thinking that because I wasn't *doing* what I knew was important it was because I didn't have my goals right or I needed to get over some psychological issues (although I didn't know what they could be).

All I had to do was:
- reduce some resistance to unpleasant tasks
- work on the tendency to set unrealistic standards for what was possible to achieve given the fact that my life is pretty full and I don't want to be a working machine
- figure out how not to get overwhelmed

AF1 either resolved this in the case of resistance or revealed it and then resolved it.

That's why tweaks don't really interest me, nor does AF2 so much - my tweaking is done in how the tasks are phrased and what goes on the list (and I wouldn't have to do that if I didn't have a whole house to renovate in my spare time).
