Going to work is the type of task I have in mind. What happens is you're processing things to do, then you leave for work, and the home list is dropped. Later you return from work and pick up where you dropped off.

Now going to work typically starts with preparation (grab lunch, grab laptop). Returning requires unpacking and putting away.

Here's the new thought: all this is in SF terms, one task "work". When time comes to head for the office, this work task is forwarded to the next page as unfinished. When you get home, you finish the task in putting stuff away.

The above idea also applies to shopping, sleeping, vacationing, working all morning in the woodshop, playing football.

I leave those types of task listed on a separate sheet on the bench - otherwise I'm likely to forget to take the meal I prepared, or leave something else essential behind that can't go in the bag before last minute. Personally, putting those into SF would not help, because I am not in the habit of looking at it as I rush out the door, and doubt that I would try to incorporate it. SF is working brilliantly for me to guide my 'work at home' time productively, but due to the total non-fit in my 'work where they pay me' time, it can't bridge the join between the two.
