Hi,

I did well for the Lentan challenge, sticking with the Bounce and with paper. But though I persevered and was quite productive, I couldn't help but think about what was missing. My main issue with the Bounce was though , in hindsight, I got most of the right things done, it felt like i was (dare i say it) bouncing around from task to task with no rhyme or reason. I wanted something that felt more purposive and substantive.

I tried once before creating an ultimate list (Ultilist) in excel, one that algorithmicly blended different MF systems together. But it got bloated and difficult to maintain. I think I solved that this time with Ultilist 2.0. It combines some of my favorite MF systems, RAF, DWM and the Bounce in Excel as follows:

Macros are used for quick entry, start and stop times and to highlight if a task is in progress.

One column calculates a number from 0-30. An item is 0 if:

a) It was entered today or yesterday (in the past 24 hours)
b) it was worked on today or yesterday (in the past 24 hours)
c) It is due today
d) It is marked as an "MIT" (Most important task, I may mark up to 3 of those a day.

Every other task is a 1-30 based on:

a) It's due date (if there is one). It will count down each day to 0 on that due date
b) It's entry date. It stays a 0 for one day, then becomes a 30 if not acted on that one day and counts down to 0 by day. (DWM rule).
c) It's in progress date. It stays a 0 for any day it is worked on then counts down from 7 to 0 (again a DWM rule).

I start the day working on the "0" items (which is pretty much the FAF active list plus due dates and MITS) sorted by when added to the list, using the bounce method.

When nothing stands out on a full pass, I go to the day 1-7 list, which are a mix of things that are in progress but were not worked on yesterday, recurring tasks not worked on yesterday (they are treated as "in progress,") and new tasks that have not yet been acted on but have been hanging around for three weeks (note since I just started this system these do not exist yet).

When nothing stands out, onto the 8-30 list, which are new tasks up to two-three weeks old.

As you can imagine, most time is spent on the 0 list, and then the 1-7 list. The algorithm will move the other tasks to those lists, but it is good to check on 8-30 once a day.

And that's the set up. Let me know what you think. If I stick with it for a month (30 days) and it doesn't blow up I can make the sheet available to share.
