Working the Closed List (AKA Backlog) is like AF1 -- compared to AF2 and RAF, it happens at a more leisurely, contemplative pace. There isn't the tyranny of the urgent always demanding attention. You can really let your rationality and intuition work together to focus on what's really important.

The only time there is the pressure to get past it and move to the Open List (AKA Active List) is when there are urgent tasks there, which are (consciously or subconsciously) pressing on you.

So, when you do finally turn to your AF4 list, after your time of randomly working on urgent tasks, I imagine you'd still have some anxiety over outstanding urgent tasks. So, you'd probably skip through the Closed List and quickly start working on your Open List. And here, you'd find several items are completed already. And maybe a few are still left that need your attention. So, you work on them. And maybe work on them some more. And then move on to the Closed List and work in that mode for awhile (unless the Tyranny of the Urgent calls you back to the Open List).

I just don't see how having some of these items completed already when you come to the list is a problem.

Good Morning Avrum,

I can't speak directly to AF4 because I don't have experience yet. I just spent my first full day being a Mark clone ;-)

But my previous (and possibly future) approach was to use AF as a part of my system, the part that I used when I was not using the other parts. So my approach was:

- Start the day
- Get my coffee, rub my eyes, and scan my AF list
- Cull items of urgency to another "Must Do Today" list
- Review the Calendar and do the same
- Think about how I want my day to go
- Lay out a schedule ... blocking time for my Must Do items
- Block time for my want to do items
- Block time for some fun things

- START WORKING on the Schedule
- At every "choice point" or "break" of any kind, review the Must Do list
- When I have a break in the Schedule, and when the Must Dos are stalled or competed
- Start in on the AF list
- After a while processing the AF list (usually another "break" or "choice point") go back to the schedule and repeat the cycle

Does the day always go so perfectly? Of course not. But at least I am centered around a schedule and my priority items and when I am "drifting" for some reason, I have the AF list to offer me lots of choices.

As to it feeling like work ... egad, being in Mark's skin yesterday was exhausting. Keeping a time log kept me honest and I was only able to work for a little over 6 hours before I was seeing double. So I do see your point "a", above, But I did clear out a lot of things that had been hanging around for a good long time ... and not BIG things either. Nits, most of them. I have no idea why they had not gotten done sooner. And the result of that is this morning I'm energized and eager to get to work again.

Then there is the long standing advice: just put more "fun" items on your list. IOW, you'll hit something that takes you off of the AF list for a while and when the time seems right, that's what you'll do. The trick is having a way to get back on the list again.

As to loosing the "pressure" by doing things out of order, I don't see that happening with AF4. The pressure comes from the whole sale dismissal of the closed list if you go through it without doing something. And I found that going through the open list only once before having to go back to the closed list, put pressure on me to do as many of those things as possible before I had to leave it for several cycles through the open list. It does seem to work pretty well. But time will tell ;-)
