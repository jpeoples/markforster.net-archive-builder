I'd liike some help please. I have settled on AF as the best system for me but I am having a dilemma. I have three main areas with projects within each.

1. Business one - with a few large customers who each have projects I am working on. (This is a business writing, bid writing and training business).

2. Business two - lots of small customers, lots of smaller tasks, with a big output every couple of months (it's a magazine publishing business).

3. Home - lost of projects and daily/weekly/ monthly tasks.

What I am finding is that:

a) if I put everyone on one long list I get a bit confused with the two businesses, deadline stuff and the projects tasks and customer tasks all get mixed up so it's hard to see an overview for each customer/project/business

b) if I break it down into businesses/projects and so on - I sort of end up with a number of lists which can be confusing and I feel I have too many reference points.

I'm probably overthinking this (too close to the problem and am only steps away from being overwhelmed). I am only feeling overwhelmed because I can't see the wood for the trees, theoretically I do have enough time to do most of what I need to - I just end up procrastinating because I feel disorganised.

Is there an easier way to 'work the list' so to speak to sort this out? Should I list only customers and projects and other ad hoc tasks and refer to another list to see what needs to be done? I'm very interested in finding out if anyone else has similar challenges and how you overcame them?

Many thanks in advance

Tag your list items then.

B1 - get quote for XYZ

B1 - etc.

As for projects I prefer to write the project details elsewhere and just name the project in AF. It's up to you how big or small a project should be to get included in AF. Be flexible and adapt as you feel is right.
