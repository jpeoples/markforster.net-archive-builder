IMHO - Don't dismiss the task, just do it at the first available opportunity. There's no need to be a slave to the system - common sense still rules.
I keep 2 notebooks - one for work and one for home - but the one for home, I carry with me to work and occasionally do things in it over lunch. What I do is flag (I just write a U in the margin) those home items I can do at work (phoning, emailing, buying on-line etc.) - and those become a small subset list of their own while I'm at work so they are all I choose from. If there weren't any flagged this way on one page, I would skip to the next page with the flags and not dismiss a thing.
As time goes on, you will probably find that you change how your write your tasks so that you eliminate the dependency issue and either phrase your item to encompass being able to do either task (because they're likely part of a project of some sort) or you'll stop writing dependent tasks down b/c you've been burned by it before. But in the meantime, why not do them out of order? Realize you've learned something about the system and resolve not to do it again... :-)

Walter:

In answer to the three questions you ask:

1) If you have a page with a single task undone and you are in the wrong place to do it, my recommendation is to dismiss it anyway. The reason is that by the time you get down to one or two items on a page, you will have had mutliple chances to do them. If you haven't done them already, you probably still wouldn't even if you were in the right place.

2) There can be no standard answer to how many notebooks you need because everyone's circumstances are different. My recommendation is to have one notebook for everything if your circumstances allow it. Bear in mind that it is usually quite a few days before every item on a page is actioned, so that they will get many occasions to be done at the right time.

3) The task depending on another task: I recommend that this type of task is put into the list with a question mark, e.g. "Consolidate departmental reports?" If the conditions aren't met, then your answer to the question is "No" and you can cross off the task and re-enter it without having to dismiss it.
