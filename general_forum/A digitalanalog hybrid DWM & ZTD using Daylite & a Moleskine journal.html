How it works:

a. In the evening, I choose all "due tomorrow" tasks in Daylite and enter them into a personalized to-do sheet (loosely based on David Seah's forms). I also choose two tasks based on the following criteria: 1) Something that scares me 2) Something that is a game changer.

b. During the day, I use the right side of my Moleskine for sketches etc (a la Danny Gregory's The Creative License), and the left side of my journal for aforementioned to-dos, errands, notes, etc.

c. In the evening i update Daylite.

There are so many advantages to a hybrid system. Most importantly, the updating of my journal and Daylite provides a built-in daily review of tasks and projects.

DWW rules keep things fresh, and me honest.

>> Fascinating though I'm not seeing DWM

All new tasks/projects are given a due date of "next month"
All re-entered tasks are given a due date of "next week"
Any tasks "due today" that are left undone are automatically dismissed.

>> The picture side I've never heard of before. What's it for?

In Danny Gregory's The Creative License, he encourages you to use a daily journal for sketches, to-do's notes, etc. By keeping it alll in one place, you get a snapshot of your day. Inspired by this idea, I sketch/draw on the right side of my journal, and tape a form (loosely based on David Seah's Emergent Task Planner) on the left side of the journal.

I don't cycle through the list. I use Leo Babauta's suggestion of working on 1-3 MAJOR tasks per day. I use the rest of the day to work on any "due today" items and other admin stuff.
