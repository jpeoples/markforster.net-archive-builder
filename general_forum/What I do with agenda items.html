I keep separate agenda lists for those whom I work closely with but then put in my AF "Call so and so" or "Email so and so with agenda items". These agenda points are on 3x5 cards in my AF pocket Moleskine . This way I don't need to go through 10 pages trying to find all the items that I need to talk about with my wife and this also eliminates me contacting a coworker numerous times in order to take care of individual items as they come up.

Does anyone else have other ideas for agenda items?

Jonathon

A dual approach.

For people I collaborate frequently eg my directs at work, I have a task for each person in Outlook with the persons name as the subject. I then list the things to discuss, follow-up etc in the task notes section. For my direct reports I have scheduled 121s in my calendar. For other people I put an entry on the AF list eg "Call xyz - see Agenda". This is a hangover from my GTD implementation and works well so I see no reason to change it.

For one off contact I just put an entry straight onto the AF list eg "Call xyz - re project status". Occaisonally I do end up with more than one entry on the AF list for the same person. However, as I scan the list 2 or 3 times per day I can quickly recognise this and either schedule a short meeting or find that one of the tasks stands out anyway and I cover them all off together.

Any project or clearly definable area will generally have it's own set of records, whether they be files, physical items or lists as mentioned above. Having an agenda list for example for a project or person is a very practical and workable tool but if I think of anything that needs to be done I would usually add a task item such as "add [item] to [agenda list]" with a subsequent (and possibly recurring) task to "call x re agenda items". That would enable me to use the AF list as the "catch all" for the original thought without the distraction of finding another list, and as Andy says will then probably naturally action all of the similar "add item" tasks at the same time.
