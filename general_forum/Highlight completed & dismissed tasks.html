My whole list is written in black, and completed tasks are crossed out in black. When I say 'crossed out' I do a double row of zizags so they're REALLY crossed out. It's a fantastic feeling - "be gone, sucker". Way more satisfying than a simple line strikethrough.

What I'm doing right now:
-> when I enter an item, I draw a box in the left "margin" (my next AF notebook will have actual margins!) I have always loved drawing ticky-boxes, I don't know why. ;^)
-> when I act on the item, I put a tick mark in the box, note the date at the end of the line, and re-enter it if it's not complete / a recurring item.
-> when I dismiss an item, it gets highlighted yellow. the date that the page was dismissed is noted in the top margin.
-> when I review a dismissed item, the ticky-box gets an X and the date of the review gets noted at the end of the line. when I review items, I either re-enter them on my list in some form or create a timed reminder, so this closes the page - the date of the page closing is also noted in the top margin.

The issue I'm having is that when I get down to the last couple of items on a page, it starts to get hard to find them, as many of the previous posters have noted. I'm mulling over various methods of dealing with this but would love to hear ideas about ways to make them stand out that are not very pen-intensive (I am trying to stay as close to one notebook, one pen as possible).

Sarah,

Have you tried filling in the tick box completely so it is black? That would make it easy to spot open items on a scan of the left margin. Also, someone posted that circle system of notation which is a bit more complex but when done you end up with something similar.

Another idea I've seen is to mark items initially with a "-" then cross it so "+" but make the vertical line the whole line width so you end up with a long vertical line down the left and any break in the line (i.e. "-") stands out as an open item.

Given your coding system, you might want to use a simple vertical line at the very left of your current system.

Personally, I find that simplicity works best for me. IOW, my eye has to pause while my brain decodes the symbols unless I have only one symbol I'm looking for ... like a break in a long vertical line or series of big black filled in boxes. That is much simpler to spot.

Just some ideas.
