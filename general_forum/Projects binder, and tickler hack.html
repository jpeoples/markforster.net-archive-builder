I keep a binder with one-page projects, sorted alphabetically. These projects each are mentioned in my AF lists, so I get to them according to AF principles. At the front of the binder is a one-glance tickler system:

Start with a wall calendar, a pair of scissors, and a hole puncher.
1 Cut the pages apart.
2 Punch holes on the bottom of the calendar.
3 Put the pages into the binder. The higher dates are by the binder rings.
4 After every week, cut that week out of the calendar.

Result: I always see, at a glance, the next 30 days' schedule.
I use this as a tickler at the front of my binder. If I need notes with an activity, I make a project page, and file it alphabetically referenced by the calendar square.
