AutoFocus was invented after DIT was written, and was not intended to have any connection to DIT. AutoFocus does require a calendar or something to manage future tasks.

If you're following DIT, it doesn't use AutoFocus methods or lists. The upcoming DIT2 is rumoured to blend these concepts but we haven't yet seen how. So if you want to handle your "Will Do" list "correctly", set aside all the AutoFocus ideas and just use it on its own.

If you're less concerned about "correct" you can invent your own blending of the two but there is no proven way to do this.
