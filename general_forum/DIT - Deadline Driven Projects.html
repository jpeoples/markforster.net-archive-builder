I have a question related to DIT. I understand that according to Mark projects fall into three categories, never ending, no deadline, and deadline driven. And I also know that he says that never ending projects should be given a specific time slot each day, no deadline projects should be taken on one at a time as a current initiative, while deadline driven projects flow as tasks into the "one-off tasks" bucket each day.

My question is how do you handle multiple deadline driven projects in DIT? Do you only work on one at a time or do a little bit of each outstanding project each day, etc?

Any help would be appreciated.

Hi Philip, I have been working with DIT successfully for over a year. I would say it is best to make a start the deadlined project as soon as you get it and then work away at it with the principle of 'little and often' that Mark suggests.

This means that you may be working on several deadline driven projects during the course of the day, maybe only for 20 minutes or so, depending on what needs doing. I often find that projects go into a quiet phase (usually when I have put the ball back in the other person's court) - so then I schedule a chase up review date in my task diary. At this point the project may pick up again.

Also, I don't bother with a 'projects list' as such anymore - I use my Task Diary to keep them current by planning in work on them and review dates.
