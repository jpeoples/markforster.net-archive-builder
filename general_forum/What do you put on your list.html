What I meant by saying it's up to you what you put on your list is that you need to experiment to find what works best for you. I put those meals on the list which I eat on my own, but not the ones I eat with my wife. I put doing the dishes on the list because otherwise I forget to do them.

Reply by Tijl Kindt

2011-02-14 21:41
I agree with Mark. You put on your list whatever you feel should be on the list. I.e. whatever you feel you might neglect, forget or postpone otherwise. I definitely have dishes on there because otherwise they don't get done for weeks. But if dishes stand out naturally for you, then you don't need to put them on there since they will be done automatically.

One thing I do still wonder about is whether to put 'fun' stuff in there. Those items will happen whether I put them on my list or not, but putting them on the list may help me keep them at bay so that I *only* do them when they come up in my list. Unfortunately this seems to work better in theory than in practice so far...
