I've never tried SAF before, because, when Mark first revealed it, I was in the middle of my DWM2 infatuation. However, when Mark announced that he was revising it six days ago, I was curious on how SAF actually feels on using it, and so I tried it right there and then.

I am surprised by how it actually fits my personality. I am by nature very impulsive and intuitive, and I have always wanted a task management system that will support my tendency to drop things in the middle of doing them to do something else that just pops in my head that feels to be more important for the moment. Well, SAF fits my needs: if I have to do something while I am in the middle of something else, I'd just cross out the item that I had been doing from my AF list, rewrite it in Column 2, write what I am about to do also in Column 2, do that something, cross that item out when finished, then resume what I was doing before (reminded to me by the item just next to the one I had just crossed out.

The problem with SAF that I found is that it has even a stronger tendency for easy tasks to be done at the expense of more difficult ones than the other AFs. This was solved very neatly by the Gatekeeper System (GKS: http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1384353 ) that I am using: now I am forced to do at least one difficult but worthwhile task before I could open Column 1 of the next page.

Since the notebook I am using is quite small (half-US letter size, or 5.5"x8.5") I am using the left page as Column 1 and right page as Column 2. It does not actually eats paper like we do potato chips, but I can see that one can quickly run out of paper with this, if that person is busy enough. However, aside from that SAFv1.2+GKS has been working very well, no problems with it currently.

Of course, I have the sneaking feeling that when SAFv1.3 is revealed my own management system will be placed by the wayside ^___^

The SAF page-size problem with a small notebook is easy to solve: Count an entire "spread" as one page (i.e., left side and right side of an open spread = one SAF "page"). The left side is the left column - the right side is the right column.

Has worked very well for me, using a pocket-sized Moleskinesque notebook (3.5x5.25" or 9x13cm).
