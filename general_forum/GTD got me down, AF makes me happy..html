To me, AF is like good project management skills. You can give a dozen people the same project. They'll all plug away at it. Most of them might even get it done before the deadline. But the one with the best project management skills will get it done faster, more efficiently, and with fewer headaches. Their results will probably be the best, too.

Anyone can make lists of things they will/want to/need to do. And, however they work from those lists, most people will get at least some of those things done. But AF gives you the skills to work from your list in a more efficient and effective way.

I've been using various TM systems (all of which tell you to keep lists of one kind or another) for quite a while. And AF (the easiest of the bunch) has helped me become more productive than any of those more complicated systems did.
