2009-06-29 23:24
Welcome back, Christine! Don't be afraid to start from scratch with AF2, as Mark recommends - he's given us a cracker of a system (thanks, Mark). I have a task to "cherry-pick tasks from AF1 and add to AF2", which I will keep re-entering until I'm happy I've transferred everything I want to. It gives me the chance to add to my AF2 list anything that's urgent and/or important (or just that I fancy doing soon) at a rate that I choose. I think it would have been much more daunting to start AF2 using the current AF1 list, especially with as many active pages as you have. I'm still using the same notebook, but started on a new page for AF2; so it's easy to access the AF1 list.

So far, I've found that I'm working on AF2 for much more of the day than with AF1. For example, I have some tasks that I like to do first thing in the morning, every day, and under AF1 I found it easier to do those tasks "off-list" and get them out of the way before working my AF1 list. However, I often had some resistance to doing them, and grudged the time they took before I could get started on AF1. Under the new system, I add those tasks every day to the end of the list, and they are therefore all instantly available, which wasn't necessarily the case in AF1, where they maybe appeared several pages on from my AF1 starting page, and I'd have to do at least one task on the intervening pages. Under AF2, these every-day tasks usually 'stand out' and get done before I go on to other tasks.

Best of luck, and I'm looking forward to hearing how you get on.
