Turning away from angels on pins, links between cancer and Dutch Elm Disease, I'd like to know how resolutely some DWM devotees are adhering to the basic rules.

I've been on the manual/paper patch for quite a few weeks now. In common with others, I have tried a few tweaks here and there.

Adherents of DWM have mostly stated that they follow the Day 7 and Day 28 (or Month) principle of having those as the ONLY points of entry for Re-Entered and New Tasks respectively. Has anyone successfuly (in thier own view) departed from ONLY Day 7 as a Re-entry point, maybe by entering on a "closed" page with very few items already on it?

Any takers or am I being more of a DWM heretic than a DWM maverick ?

Sarah,

Regarding my objection to "the flipping of the pages," I did try the canonical DWM method for a while, and had many pages with very few items on each. I think this type of system *requires* that one cycle through all the active pages at least a couple times per day, and I found it cumbersome with so many mostly-empty pages. My modified two-facing-pages-per-week system is great - I quickly glance at each of the five double-pages, and find that I can keep a mental map of what is where throughout the day. It's so clear that I often just jump directly to the next task that "stands out" without having to cycle through each item on each page.

I do augment the system in one other way: each morning, I pick out three items that I really want to get done, and put a little circle to the left of each one, then copy those three to an index card labeled with today's date. This way, I know I'm getting a few important (or about-to-expire) tasks done each day.
