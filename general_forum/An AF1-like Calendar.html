In my post entitled, "Systems are losing simplicity" I mentioned that I had an AF1-like calendar that I keep. Scott Hutchins asked me to describe it, so I'm putting it here in this new thread.

First, here's the reason for having it. I wanted a calendar with my tasks, so that eliminated an electronic calendar. And, I found that printed calendars didn't give me enough space to write. And, I needed some way to keep reminding me of the appointment or due date or whatever. So, I came up with my method to eliminate the problems I was experiencing.

In a separate section of my Tasks notebook (separated by a divider) I have 6 pages. And, since I use Levenger paper, there is a blank column to the left of the lines on the page.

These 6 pages are Titled at the top:
– This Week
– More than One Week Away
– More than Two Weeks Away
– More than Three Weeks Away
– More than Four Weeks Away
– Long-Term

In the blank left column – starting at the bottom – each day I write the appropriate date for the sheet, scratching out the prior date listed there. "This Week" will be dated Today's Date + 7. "More than One Week Away" will be "This Week" + 7. Actually, after initially calculating it, the next day is simply a matter of incrementing the date – there's really no more need for calculation after that.

Example:
I have a trip coming up on February 25. It is entered on my Long-Term sheet.

When the "More than Four Weeks Away" page is dated February 25, I will scratch the trip out on the "Long-Term" sheet and write it in on the "More than Four Weeks Away" sheet. That means for an item that is entered on the Long-Term sheet it will be rewritten 5 times.

I don't mind the rewriting, because it is this that serves to keep this before me. By the time the event comes due it is thoroughly engrained in me.

That's pretty much it.
