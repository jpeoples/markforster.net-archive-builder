Zenhabits.com promotes the idea "less is more". One thing Leo, the Zenhabits blogger, stresses is for one to choose a goal and then focus on that goal for a month. After the goals becomes a habit or is accomplished then pick another. (Sort of like Current Initiative ideas)

He states, "Focus on one goal. Just as focusing on one task at a time is more effective, and focusing on one habit at a time is more effect, so is focusing on one goal at a time. While it might seem very difficult, focusing on one goal at a time is the most powerful way of achieving your goals. When you try to take on many goals at once, you’re spreading thin your focus and energy — the two critical components for achieving a goal.
What if you have 5 goals you want to achieve? Pick one to focus on first. Break it into a mini-goal you can accomplish this month, if it’s a longer-term goal. Pick an action you can do today. Keep doing this until the goal is accomplished — do an action every day, finish the mini-goal, pick the next mini-goal to work on. Then, when your One Goal is completed, focus on the next goal.
Some goals are ongoing ones — like blogging every day, or exercising every day. In those cases, turn them into habits — focus exclusively on turning the goal into a habit, until the habit is ingrained. Then focus on the next goal."

This fits with DIT Current Iniatives. However, I am wondering how this would align with AF emphasis on little and often.

Autofocus encourages a "bottom-up" approach to goals. Instead of determining what your goal(s) should be, you allow them to emerge and develop as you work on them.
