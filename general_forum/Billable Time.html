While I am enjoying using AF2 to plow through my work, I'm still trying ot find the optimum solution for billable time. I work from home and I try to put in 4-6 hours of billable time daily. If I follow AF (any variation) I end up with a mix of billable and non-billable tasks. All of them need to be done eventually, but I want to make sure I am meeting my minimum of 4 billable hours per day. I've tried using the calendar to block out project time, but this does't always work. I may block off 2 hours to work on a project only to find out that 30 minutes into it I need to clarify something with the client before I can proceed.

I always have enough work to fill in the hours, but how can I make it stand out from the rest of the tasks on my list? Should I run a separate list for billable items? That doesn't seem worthwhile because sometimes items that start out as non-billable end up being billable once I start working on them...like "Call client abc" who then says go ahead with the changes which sometimes I do on the fly while on the phone with them so they can review them instantly in their browser.

Does anyone have any thoughts on using AF to manage this?

I work from home as well, and have to track everything I do for a two-month trial we're running with my company, with me as the guinea pig.

A lot of my work is writing, which as most have discovered, isn't always possible for hours on end. I have a ton of stuff assigned to me, and the only way I can get to it all without the last-minute-essay-panic feeling is by using AF2 and breaking everything I have to do down into manageable bits.

Best free solution I've found so far. I had to find a solution that required a really minimal amount of clicks in order to track time (anything web based or too complex is just too much overhead), and this was it.

I know I'm digging up an old thread, but I had a little brainstorm about this today. I've been blocking time on my calendar for client projects (a la the Mick McCool and Joe Slobb example from DIT).

I think the missing piece for me is being able to connect the calendar with an invoice...and today it hit me. When I add the client to my calendar, I can add the open invoice number after their name like this: ClientName INV#12345. This allows me to search Google calendar by client name to see all appts or by client name/invoice number for a specific project for them or just "INV#" to see all billable timeblocks in a list.

And to clarify what I decided on form my original post, I keep separate client lists and then block off time to go through as many items on the list as possible. I keep repeating the timeblocking until the project gets completed. This makes my billing much more accurate and gives me a history of when and how much time it took me to do various things. If I think of something for another project while I'm working, I just add it to my active AF list and process it to the appropriate client list later. This seems to work the best.
