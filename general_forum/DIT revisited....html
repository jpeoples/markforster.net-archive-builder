DIT is the work of a genius. Except perhaps for Dave Allen, there has been no one in the past 50 years or so who has come up with an original idea on how to manage time that truly and consistently works. Mark Forster's book contains so many novel ideas (such as 'declaring backlog' and getting your current work under control, "little and often", "closed lists" and "limits") that are so original that one would not find them anywhere else. DIT is the only system that ever worked for me (and is still working). There was no one more surprised than I when I completely and without effort cleared my inbox of over 6000 emails in a month by taking it as my current initiative. My inbox now doesn't have anything except that day's email. For me, there is no better system for managing your life than DIT - not even Autofocus in all its glory and many versions.
