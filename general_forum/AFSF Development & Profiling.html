I may be proved wrong, but SF3 feels closer to the culmination of the AF experiment, rather than yet another enticing variant (although of course, I wouldn’t put anything past Mark). Whether or not this proves to be true, SF3 has certainly generated more excitement and activity than I can recall on this site for quite some time.

I thought it might be helpful to Mark (and to one another) if we started a post which could be used as a baseline to measure people’s changing experience of SF3 over time. As Mark has indicated in a recent post, one week is much too early to accurately judge the effectiveness of a new time management system. However, it might be useful to measure people’s initial responses against their experience of SF3 a little further down the line.

So I propose that people add their responses to this post in the very simple layout below:

1. What system are you currently using and what had you been using previously?

2. How long have you been using your current system?

3. In less than 10 words, what do you most like about it?

4. In less than 10 words, what are you struggling with/concerned about?

5. Do you use your chosen system at home and at work (I recognise that for some of you, your ‘work’ may be at home)?

6. How would you best describe your (primary) work in terms of the nature of tasks (eg. mostly non-urgent project based tasks)?

7. Do you use your chosen system electronically or paper?

8. On a scale of 1-10 (10 being brilliant / MF), how would you rate your own competence in time management?

As you can see, I’ve thrown a couple of additional questions to appease my own curiosity.

If people are keen to respond to this post, then could I suggest that people stick very rigidly to the format (ie. keeping responses very short and posting any developments in a different/new post)?

If this post gets a good response, then we can repeat it in a month’s time and perhaps again in three months’ time. The results may prove illuminating.
