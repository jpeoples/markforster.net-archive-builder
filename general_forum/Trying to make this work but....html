Bliss149:

<< Dismissing tasks and making pages inactive…
Confession: I have yet to make a single page inactive! ... I have completed many tasks and HAVE dismissed quite a few. >>

It sounds as if you're going wrong here. There can't be dismissed tasks on active pages. I suggest you re-read the rules on dismissal carefully.

<< But I go back to my first page…and there are things on here that I really do need to DO!!! I just don’t want to do them TODAY. How do I just dismiss and not relist if they are things that DO have to be done? >>

If you are following the rules correctly, you should already have had multiple chances to do these tasks.

<< (It also bugs me that dismissed items are highlighted because my eye always goes to them and not to the things on the list left to be done.) >>

If you have one or more dismissed tasks on a page, every other task should be crossed out. So there shouldn't be a problem.

<< Not having categories. >>

You've stated yourself what the problem with categories is. One tends to spend more time categorising and re-arranging and prioritising than you do doing the work. However it's your system, so if you want to use a few symbols by all means do. Only just use it where it's really necessary, not for every task. My own view however is that SF works better without.

<< How/where do I outline the steps of project? >>

In the project documentation - which may just be a file or even a sheet of paper somewhere. Most small projects don't need the steps outlining. Really big projects need a lot more documentation than you can fit on a time management list. There is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question.

Welcome, Bliss!

All the things you mentioned can easily be solved using AF1. However you may want to consider the new SuperFocus (SF). It's only slightly more complex than AF1, works in the same fashion, and in the end seem to work better.

http://www.markforster.net/blog/2011/2/10/rules-for-superfocus.html

By the way, "dismissed" doesn't mean gone forever. It means you put the page aside until you get around to reviewing dismissed pages. A task called "Review dismissed pages" should be on your list. When you review these pages, you may delete some tasks or re-enter some. For the latter, make sure you figured out why you ignored it last time, and are sure things will be different this time.

Bliss, I've also long struggled with the idea of not wanting to dismiss anything cause everything i put on there is important, in fact, I still do.

I'm realizing this is due to not following the little-and-often principle at all. When I feel this resistance it's because I tend to view tasks as a whole (needing to be completed in one go).

This is contrary to the principles behind AF. AF simply wont work if you view each task as one big scary thing needing lots of time blocked off to do it. It will lead to just the sort of bind you are in (lots of pages, with tasks needing to be done, but not done, and always running into them thinking "I should do this, just not now").

Instead when you get to it, just do something small, 5 min, 10 min, and write it again at the end. If you can't do even that much, then you have to dismiss it and seriously think about rewording it or if it needs to be done at all, as per the original instructions.

Hi Bliss149,

"What I was doing was getting rid of a few that I know I'm not going to do right now but I shouldn't dismiss any task until i go through the page and there is no task on there that I am willing to move forward in some way TODAY, is that right? Then I dismiss everything that I'm not going to do today."

I notice that you are referring to 'today' here, while in fact it should be 'at this very moment'. When you come to a new page and there is not a single task that you are willing to move forward on right now at this very moment, than all tasks are dismissed (highlighted).

When you then come to your task "Review dismissed tasks" you can go through the highlighted tasks and cross off all tasks that you do not intend to do any time soon (in the next few weeks), you can re-add all tasks that you do need to do or do intend to do, and add anything else to a tickler file for "some day".

As Alan already mentioned, do try to rephrase tasks when you re-add them or split them up into several tasks since that may make it easier for you to actually do them. Otherwise you might end up dismissing and re-adding them again without making any progress.

I would also suggest that you try out SuperFocus. It really is a big improvement on AutoFocus 1 and is only slightly more complex.
