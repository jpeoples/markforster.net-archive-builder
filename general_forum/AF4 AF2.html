Suggest using AF4. The PDF is connected to AF4.

You may have only one list if you like. Many people would rather not consider "Clean Bedroom" when at the office. This is especially an issue when it's time to dismiss a list because you took no action on it. Do you really want to throw out your chores because you're in the wrong situation to do them?

Now if you are free to go home midday and tackle some chores, no problem. Otherwise, one book two lists is the way to go.

If your projects require planning, keep the plans outside AF. In AF put project tasks you plan to work on, or else just put "work on project X".

Reply by Mark Forster

2010-01-28 15:46
Alan

"eventually one place is done, and you must dismiss the tasks on that other place"

That can happen but it's far from always the case. If, say, you finish all your home chores in the closed list one evening, you can move on to the open list for any further home tasks you want to do.

Then the next day, you can work both the open and the closed list at work. Provided that you make sure that you finish your work either in the closed list or near the beginning of the open list, you will have all your home tasks available to you that evening.
