I'm happy to report that I've begun using the Auto Focus system - thanks to my colleague Avrum bringing it up time and again on our support calls (miss you Avrum!). I'm also happy to report that I'm getting a lot more done. However, I've seemed to develop what appears to be a bad AF habit. I don't have a lot of time to work the lists, so when I approach them (work/home), I scan through it, as directed, then start the things that jump out at me. I can't bear not to mark items, as they need to be done right away and I'm afraid I'll get distracted with other items. The bad habit is I then start scanning and randomly picking items to work on, cross them off, then when I get back to the book later on, I start randomly picking items again. I can't seem to keep track of where I left off and of which way to scan.

Believe me, getting this far with focusing on overall tasks is a huge win. However, I'm wondering if there is a quick and easy way to keep track of where I left off, or should I begin at the beginning each time? (I read Revised AF and see there is a Reverse Mode option) but it seems like too rules to follow. I'm a scan sort of person and enjoy just picking out what jumps out, without a lot of rules about it. Maybe I don't have a bad habit after all - or is there a good rationale for breaking this habit now?

I use a stiff-bound portable notepad with two rubber bands. One to mark the page I am processing, the other to mark the final page with entries, so I can instantly flip it open and add items as they come to mind.

Also the idea is to mark, consider and work on one item at a time.

Hope this helps.

And another paperclip for the first open page.

I actually use three paperclips: one to mark the first open page, one to mark the page where I am entering new tasks (each of these is on the top of the pate), and one to mark the item I'm working on at the moment (on the side of the page).
