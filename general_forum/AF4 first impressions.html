Hi everybody,

Just wanted to share my first impressions of AF4:

Mark, I think the idea is brilliantly simple and this might very well be the 'final' Autofocus system!! I love the idea that the closed list idea is used but still allowing the freedom for the 'now' stuff to be done as well. It really makes sense...

The fact that my whole list (269 items) is now a closed list is very inspiring. On the first run I already managed to identify 34 items that weren't relavant anymore / double entries / already had been done. This shows how in AF3 I had been ignoring a large section of the list. This cleanup feels great. 'Only' 235 items left...

As with every system the proof of the pudding is in the eating (for a few weeks/months)... but at this moment things look very promising!!

I think the system is very much comparable to DIT. The difference is that you don't have a closed list for each day but for as long as it takes. This makes the system much more flexible than DIT. For me the problem with DIT was that I have some days with a lot of discretionary time and also days wth hardly any discretinary time. I remember having thoughts that (kind of) resemble AF4 when I was trying DIT a year ago. Though I never implemented it...

What are the first impressions of the rest of the 'Autofocus Community'?

The simple cunning of AF4 (after all of one day using it) is that it forces you to keep squeezing the 'backlog', bit by bit, and to focus on MAKING DECISIONS about the things you've been dilly-dallying on for too long...AND it allows the flexibility to get to the end fast when you need to.
The genius of AF1, which was masked for me in AF2 and AF3 was the introduction of a delay between idea and action. For someone as impulsive as me the delay is essential.
AF4 manages the tension that (i) you want to keep picking at the backlog so that you don't have to make a decision about every single thing in it (ii) you want to get to the end for all those juicy things that are out of bounds as long as you're at the backlog.
Because it greatly limits the time you can spend on the active list, I'm finding myself enjoying wrestling with these tensions and working in a little and often approach much more effectively.
Thank you Mark. I'm not waiting for AF5. I'm a happy customer (not a paying customer yet, but I'd be glad for that to change when you want it to).
