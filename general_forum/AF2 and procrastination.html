After some time with AF2 I kind of start missing the "gamely feeling" of how AF1 handled a page as a closed list. "Do something about one thing on this list or feel the pain of having to dismiss all of it!" - this rule sometimes got me attacking something unpleasant just because it was LESS unpleasant than the rest!

The big advantage of AF (and, so to say, it's source of energy) was that it eliminated procrastination to a bigger degree than any other method I ever encountered. And playfully.

AF2 handles urgent items better than AF1, but I am not sure whether this comes at the cost of more procrastination.

I would like to know what others think.

Hi Andreas,

I can identify with AF1 giving you more energy and in a playful way encourage you to do high resistance items!

That said though with both AF1 and AF2 there is the risk of *easy* items 'standing out' all the time. Unfortunately this actually means you are simply procrastinating on what you know you should to be doing right now.

AF1 makes it easier to do this because you are on different pages all the time. And hence you can procrastinate on the main thing you should be doing legitimately. Since you're sticking to the system you might not even consciously know you are procrastinating most of the time but deep in your heart you always know....

Could it be that AF2 makes this sense of procrastination come to the surface more for you since you are now every time considering the whole list when you pick the next task?

Maybe this is a good thing?

The bad news... You now have no excuse to not get started with the main high resistance item.

Attack it head on, may the force be with you!!
