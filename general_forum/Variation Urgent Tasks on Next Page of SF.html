2011-02-12 20:34
I don't think it would work for me. I do have things come up that need to be dealt with very soon, as in the next 5-15 minutes -- sometimes more than one thing, which does require me to write them down.

Being able to take immediate action, while remaining WITHIN the rules of the system, is one of the key advantages of SAF. (I don't count the "do it now without writing it down" rule, as a real system rule -- it's more of a way to "give permission" to the user to go off-system whenever it's really needed).

I could easily imagine examples like this: I am currently working on a page. It has two or three items already in Column 2, and several in Column 1. My wife comes into the room and we need to talk for 15 minutes about some pressing things going on (with six children, goats, chickens, working from home, etc., there are ALWAYS pressing things going on!). I open up my SAF book and start writing. Most of the writing happens on the Last Page, column 1, but there are a few things that really need to be addressed as soon as possible, so (following the new amendment), I write them on the next page, column 2.

So, my wife leaves the room, and I get back to work. With AF's help, I've come to be pretty good at focusing only on what is in front of me right now, so I start working my page again, and forget all about the urgent tasks my wife just gave me. I spend an hour working some Column 1 items and clearing all the Column 2 items, finishing some, and moving others forward. While doing this I am interrupted by a phone call, a couple IMs from co-workers, and an hour-long teleconference (pre-scheduled).

Finally I get to the next page, where I wrote all that urgent stuff from my wife. Oops, it's too late now to make that important phone call she needed me to do, it'll have to wait till tomorrow. Oops, I can't finish this other task today, I need my daughter's input and she already left for her afternoon class. Oops, she asked me to check if two people had replied to our emails yet, that could've been done in 5 minutes, but I totally forgot about it. And I've got all my OTHER stuff on this page that I already had to do, also.

None of that would have been a problem if I had written it in column 2 of my current page.

Also, this problem could be much worse if I happen to have one or two pages where Column 2 is already full. Let's say I am currently working on page 1, when I meet with my wife. Following the new amendment, I try to add these urgent tasks to page 2, column 2, but that column 2 only has room for 1 more item; the remaining items must go on page 3, column 2.

Now, when I finally get to page 2, I have an additional problem. First, everything in column 1 now faces dismissal, since the page is completely filled, and this puts real pressure to bear on those column 1 tasks -- causing me to delay further taking action on my wife's urgent tasks, most of which are on page 3. I might end up spending an hour or more on this page, if the items really need action and I can't just allow them to be dismissed.

So, I finally come to page 3, and it's been 3-4 hours since I met with my wife. It's a real problem by that point.

Sticking to the old rule avoids these problems (as long as I've got room in your current Column 2, which is usually the case!) and lets me take action immediately if I need to.

I get the feeling that the need for this variation will depend on the type of work that you do.

If most of your tasks are originating from you or your own projects where the deadlines are most organized and goal drive, then putting urgent things on the next page will help drag you forward through the list.

however in a customer service driven position putting urgent items on the current page will help you not miss obligations to others.

In my unique corner of the world, we get requests for urgent tasks from our customers that come in via phone, email, IM, support tickets etc. They have a variety of priorities and importances but usually they fall into two camps of due dates: (1) anytime in the next 3 weeks or (2) due 15 minutes ago, aka they arrive already over due.

Putting urgent items on the current page handles this well as it doesn't force rushing the existing column2 tasks artificially whille absorbing what can be rapid fire arrive of must be done today items.. What maybe unique in our world is these are often tasks that can be done at the same time as they involve extended waiting periods that don't justify dismissing the tasks, aka installing a software patch that takes twenty minutes..

If I was to use this alteration it would have to be of the form.. Is the task urgent to a customer and will I break a promise if it does not get done today, then it goes on column two of this page, if it was urgent to me only then it could go on the next page...

Again this just shows that the system is flexible as all get out. In my case on Monday's and Friday's I might never get off the current pages column 2 on tue-thurs I will probably blow around the page cycle several times and get a ton of column1 items down.
