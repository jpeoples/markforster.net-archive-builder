Melinda,

Just follow these two steps:

1. Set up your 43-folders "tickler" file. See

http://members.optusnet.com.au/~charles57/GTD/tickler.html

for details. Put all the project sheets and reminders into this system, in the appropriate day or month folders. Don't forget to consult this file first thing everyday.

2. Just use AF4. Follow Mark's instructions at

http://www.markforster.net/blog/2009/9/5/preliminary-instructions-for-autofocus-v-4.html

and "trust the system" as they say.

List at least one "next action" task for each active project you are working on. Continue adding items as you think of new things to do. Once you're up and running with your 43f/AF4 system, throw out each page from your previous filing system, unless it's a project list or reminder that you need to refile into the tickler.

Your life has now been saved. You're welcome!

2009-10-8 19:33
The daily task list can be included in the AutoFocus list. In fact, that's a good list to start your AF4. Write them in. After you do one, you copy it to the end, and it will be there for you tomorrow to work on again.

For your tickler question, yes. The tickler is for future things. So if you have a weekly task that's due, put it into AF4, work on it, and put it back into the tickler for next week. Next week, when that date comes up, you pull it out and put it into AF4 again.

In case it's not clear, the stuff in AF4 is not all expected to be finished in one day. It's up to you to choose which get finished as you cycle through the list. Your regular chores probably should be among those which you do finish same day.

Melinda,

You asked (Alan): <>

I just keep each recurring task (e.g. "mop floors - every 2 weeks" or "water plants - every week") on a separate sheet of paper, and file it on the upcoming day that I think I'll have time to do it. I also use the same sheet to log the last time it was done. That way, I can see if my plants are about to die!

As Alan said, "AF doesn't tell you to finish something on any day." And the tickler is just a mechanism to bring something forward to be listed, scheduled, or started on a given day, not necessarily completed. The whole idea is to keep track of things without having to think about them.

The other three tools I didn't mention above are an In tray, Reference files, and a calendar. The In tray is where every (paper) thing goes. You empty your daily folder from the tickler, and put anything else (e.g. mail) into it. At the end of the day, it should be empty (trashed, filed in Ref, or back in tickler). Depending on how much paperwork you're handling, you might also want a Pending tray and/or an Active projects tray/drawer. Anything that really must be done on a given day might be best to put on your calendar, instead of or in addition to the tickler.

Hope this helps!

Melinda,

If I understand your plight, it seems to be more about your not consulting your lists rather than the list themselves... am I right?

If that is the case, beside maybe simplifying your lists a bit, use AF as a reminder to consult your lists. Better, put in place a PROCEDURE (to become a habit in time) to consult your lists before you start your day. A daily "review" each morning will do the trick.

As to simplification, I had two systems in place some time ago. I had an "EVERY" list which was a single page which had all you describe. EVERY DAY, EVERY WEEK, EVERY MONTH, and EVERY YEAR sections. (I posted info about it here that you can find by searching ... I even posted a PDF of it on an associated list ... with a link to it on this site. Try searching "EVERY DAY" perhaps.) Basically it was just a reminder, not a lot of information.

For household chores I used a 3x5 card system which was like the 43-Folders system before there was a 43-Folders system. It was just color coded reminder cards which were filed by Month and Day (the month cards moved to the day cards each month). The ones under the Day numbers (1-31) were indeed to be done on those days. Otherwise, when I got the cards for the month out, I filed them under a day of the week. If I did not know what day, I just put them under Sunday (I think) and they moved around as necessary. After the task was done, I moved it back to next month ... and at the end of the day, I moved the ones I had not done to the next (or next appropriate) day. I also wrote on the card the last time I did the task (in pencil) and each time I deferred it ... so I could see if it was being neglected.

I suggest you focus on building habits for this kind of thing. (Well, the frequent ones, anyway ;-) Another thing you can try is to write the upcoming items in your calendar. If you use a planner with enough space you can put a task list of things to get done in any period when you do your weekly review. IOW, if you have a page-a-month setup, write (or attach) a list of goals/tasks/focus areas for that month. If it is page-a-week, do it for the upcoming week.

I think what will untangle it for you is do do lots of reviews. That is how things get lost ... they fall under the table or behind a cabinet (in our minds ;-) Personally, I do a weekly review on Sunday morning (over coffee with my neighbor who also does that ) and consult my monthly goals and allocate them to next week ... and see where I am slipping. Then I do TWO reviews a day ... really, I mean it (LOL ;-) but they are very quick. At the end of every day I set up tomorrow. I review what is on my lists and allocate them to tomorrow if appropriate. Then first thing in the morning, I do a quick scan to see if that still makes sense. It is very quick. As you can tell, I am not a "pure" AutoFocus person. I like to block time for big projects and I use AF to fill in time when I'm not working those blocks.

The other thing to do is separate (by highlighting in someway, perhaps) those things that MUST get done and those things that you'd like to do but the world won't end if they slip a bit. A good example is "Change the Furnace Filter". That needs to be done every other month (in my system) but if it slips a few days (or even weeks, or even months ... life as we know it does not end ... but the furnace life may be shortened ;-) However, "Take out the Garbage" is much more critical and I have elevated it to a habit so I no longer even think about it. It is critical because the garbage is only collected twice a week and if I miss a day I have to live with it for four days or so. If you grossly prioritize your lists that way, it might simplify things.

AF is great as a continual reminder tool. The only problem that may crop up is if you get so used to seeing and ignoring a task that you become desensitized to it and it slips without your really grasping that it is slipping. So that means that you need to use the AF system more strictly, I would think. IOW, when you see an item THINK about it for a beat or two and let it sink it. Don't just "scan" so that things fly by. If you THINK a moment, you'll have to answer some mental questions about why it is there ... STILL ;-)
