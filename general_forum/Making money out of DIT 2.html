One thing to consider is that there are a lot of lurkers on here that just read but don't feel like they have a lot to offer (and thus don't post) but would gladly donate.

I guess since I'm posting here I might as well give my two cents on what I think you should do.

1. Publish the instructions (let's say as a 5 page ebook) with a suggested minimum donation (e.g. $5) I think this is really important, many people won't donate if they don't have an amount.
2. Ask people to only spread the instructions via the website or if they summarize in a blog post to also include a link for more details and donations.
3. Finally (and here's the key to making this a success) tell everyone that you will work on a full book (self or publisher published) if you get a certain number of minimum donations(say 1000 or 5000 or whatever.) This can act as kind of a publisher's advance by giving you upfront cash, while also showing you that enough people are interested to justify the time and effort put in to a real book. This also makes all the regulars feel empowered since they can go out and tweet about it to encourage people to read the instructions (and hopefully donate) in order to get a potential book out of it.

Anyway just some thoughts. Hopefully you can derive something of value here. And thanks again to you Mark for the systems and also to all the regulars for the great discussions!
