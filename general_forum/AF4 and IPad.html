I, on the other hand, am using my iPad - not for AF4, but for DWM. Based on the recommendations of a number of folks here, I am using Toodledo (www.toodledo.com) in the cloud from my computer and with the app from the iPad. I like being able to add notes to tasks in Toodledo - not a lot, but enough to jog my memory about things.

It can work, but I would echo what others have said - if what you are doing is working for you, don't change just for the sake of changing. I changed because I really liked DWM but from the very moment Mark laid it out, I wanted to do it digitally. It was the first of Mark's systems that screamed DIGITAL at me.

I have recenty bought an iPad*, and it has helped me with my AF4/DWM**. But it has not replaced my notebook; it has instead helped me cut down the time I spend mindless browsing the web. (Some random browsing leads to some serendipity finds, so I have not abstained completely :D).

I still sometimes got trapped into checking the news or Google Reader way too often, even inside my system. Now I do most of my reading on the iPad, including news, blog posts and scientific papers.

My workflow is the following. I have two actions on my list: "read links - delicious" and "iPad". The first one use to take an hour or two of hours everyday, scattered throughout the day, and I also had lots of pdfs/articles to print and read at my leisure. Now this takes 20 minutes at most --- I just read very short articles on the computer screen and mark everything to be read on the iPad. Usually in the evenings, when I sit down to read books, magazines and what the like, I also read most of today's articles on the iPad. It is much more comfortable than on the computer screen, and now I don't print all the things I used to. And I never read more papers than now.

But I don't think I would ever replace my AF/DWM notebook. Paper feels so much better.

* I wanted an e-reader especially for my papers in pdf. I wanted e-ink, but Kindle seemed way too complicated/locked. The iPad may be locked and all, but works wonderfully for my papers.

** I am using DWM. However, I have been at a work trip for a month, and I decided to use AF4 during the trip (starting afresh). When I am back home, I'll be back to DWM, also starting afresh. This has worked great, so far.

The BigGest problem with any digital approach is, too much flexibility. If you're the type who is always changing, always searching for better, digital media make this problem worse.

In my case I have a very particular implementation in OneNote which largely ( but not entirely ) bypasses this problem. Because my approach is very manual and very tied to the format of a OneNote notebook, which resembles a paper notebook, there's very little I can tweak. It workS for me because it is limited (follow the notebook format) yet flexible ( arrange the content freely within the framework). Evernote might fail me because it's too flexible. And more list-oriented programs cause me trouble because they're too constraining But their automation has too many features.

The notebook format is best for reigning in unbridled creativity. I don't think iPad has this.
