Hi all,

One thing I think that is really lacking from GTD, AF1 and AF2 (but is present in DIT) is that it doesn't encourage you to make a *plan for the day* but suggest you pick your items ad hoc. This is the reason why these systems don't work for me.

My current system is as follows:

1) Capture everthing I want/would like to do in a number of lists (laptop+phone is the main list but I also have home, work and shopping)

This is the main thing in GTD that really works for me, the rest is too complicated for my liking.

What I learned from AF though is that it really works to keep this list in the order (also add dates for every new day) of when tasks were added. I usually 'hang out' around the bottom of the list. Every now and then I clean up and scan for 'old' items I do actually want to do and highlight them and dismiss stuff I don't. The big advantage in maintaining the order and dates of when an item was added is that you get a sense of how long you have chosen *not* to do it!

Doing some work towards the item allows me to add it to the end again. Great idea borrowed from AF!

2) Make a *plan for the day* by picking items on the lists and start with the most important (often the most procrastinated) item and so on. I take some time to do this proparly!

If I don't make a plan of the day and pick items *ad hoc* like GTD and Autofocus suggest I always end up procrastinating on something important.

Maintaining the order of the plan (unlike DIT) and starting with the important stuff is a biggie too, since else I end up putting the high resistance item mid way the list and often find a way to avoid it.

3) Appy the http://www.pomodorotechinique.com to actioning the list (only discovered it two days ago and totally love it!)

What do you guys think? Anyone else use a similar approach?
