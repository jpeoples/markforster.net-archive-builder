Reply by Hannah

2009-01-9 13:57
John

Surely the whole beauty of Autofocus is that by starting each page with the same number of tasks (apart from the last page where you're still entering tasks of course) it allows your rational mind and your instinct to work together to choose what to do next (the task that 'stands out'). If you start fiddling with the basic, well tested, premise and end up with a number of pages STARTING out with only a few entries per page then this whole process never gets off the ground.

Once you've done several cycles of your pages and there are only a few items left on the page you then get to see whether you are resisting doing any of those tasks, or if there's another reason they're still there. If you're feeling resistance you can start to analyze why and either rephrase, break them down into smaller chunks, dismiss them or figure out any other reason for the resistance. However, I would think starting with fewer tasks and not having consistant starting quantities on your pages would stop this from happening. With only a few tasks to start with you may feel resistance straight away which doesn't seem to happen when you have a page of mixed easy and harder tasks (as well as the chipping away effect you get from doing A bit at A time).

Just my two pennyworth...
