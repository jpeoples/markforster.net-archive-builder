Each time management system has it's own overhead. The system helps you to use your energy to do tasks, but it uses part of the energy to run itself. The more complex is the system, the bigger is the overhead. So simplicity is the key. To reach simplicity we have to reduce number of types of objects we operate with and number of operations we do with them.

GTD has a lot of different objects:
-tasks in "bucket"
-tasks assigned for some date
-waitin tasks
-current projects
-maybe probject
-5 levels of planning
etc tec

It also has a number of operations:
-complex procedure of emptying of incoming bucket
-procedure of task executing
-reviews: daily, weekly, monthly, yearly

etc etc

So the system is very complex, overhead of running it is very big, it saps all the energy it is supposed to organize.

DIT is simple and AF is even simpler.
DIT has three kind of objects:
-planned tasks
-new tasks (under the line)
-Current Initiative

AF has only one type of object: a task, and very few operations with it.

I can't imagine a system that would have less complexity and less overhead than AF. It's the champion! :)

P.S. "Ttoday" index card increases complexity, so it is a god news, that it' abandoned.
