My question was buried in my last response regarding DO IT so I will post it here. Would anyone be interested in a Beta Test of DO IT, like what Mark did with AF? I would provide the ebook for free to a limited number of people. The only thing I would ask is that people follow the program exactly as it is laid out for say 30-45 days and then write a concise review of the program and if it helped them to achieve or get closer to the three desired outcomes (goals) they identified during the program.

DO IT is not a time management program so you would continue to use whatever time management program you are using for "everyday" time management and run DO IT in parallel. So when you are working on your three desired outcomes, you would be using the DO IT method.

Please let me know your thoughts on participating in this experiment.

2010-08-9 1:51
Hi Gerry,

Here is my feedback on the beta test:

What I thought worked well:

Focusing on specific goals for 100 days is a powerful method to make some really good progress on some big goals. In 2 of my 3 goals, I have made some very good progress. No doubt, I am much further ahead than I would have been otherwise.

What hasn't work well:
I am unable to consistently make progress on all 3 goals every day. There were many days where I simply could not make the time for them. Also, travelling for work precluded my ability to continue every day.

The goals I chose were probably too ambitious. In retrospect, I should have scaled back the size of my goals. Each goal was probably do-able in 100 days, but not all 3 together given the time I have available.

For future 100-day cycles, I will give a lot more thought up-front on what the goals should be. Doing anything for 100 days is a big commitment. The 3 goals that are chosen must be ones that I really, REALLY WANT to accomplish. A strongly burning desire is required for a 100 day journey.

So, in summary , I will try this in the future, but with my eyes wide open on the size of the goals to pursue and with an up-front check on my motivation for the goal.
