Are you trying to track what you need to do once you have the item, or the item you are owned.

For what is owed to you, depending on your system, you can enter a new task or calendar entry to call the person in the future to follow up on the request, which will remind you if you did not get what you need. You can also keep a waiting for list of all the things people owe you.

For the task you need to do, you should enter it into your system, you could also put a note next to it that it requires something from someone else and you can maybe handle both issues with one entry.

Gerry

Here is how I handle it. Suppose the following scenario:

Scenario: I can't do X until Mark does Y. I need to track if Mark's done Y

I write the task this way:

- ? Mark done Y (0) 1-3

The question mark tells me I'm waiting on something. The something I'm waiting on follows the "?".

The "(0)" tells me this is the first time I have written this down and I wrote it down on January 3rd. This means I informed Mark on January 3rd, what I needed done.

So, a couple of days go by and I check and I see he still hasn't done it. I strike-out the first entry and I re-enter the following at the end of my tasks:

- ? Mark done Y (1) 1-5

This tells me I have checked on this 1 time and the last time I checked was January 5th.

Depending on how pressing the item, after the increment gets to a certain point, you will need to follow-up with a phone call or an email.

That's how I do it.

I've read some of the best delegators (which I ain't) would use an agenda.

They give an assignment to a person, explain its importance, and ask the other person for a commitment when it will be done. They record expected completion date on that agenda.

On that date check if it's done. If not, maybe express disappointment, but ask when it will be done.
