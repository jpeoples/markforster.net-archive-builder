Existing AF pages can't be used well with SF because it expects the second column to be mostly empty. Plus, SFv3 insists a full page must be dismissed immediately. So what's a good way to get started?

I created an active task "Grab stuff from old list". The effect is a little bit of ping-ponging. Each time I advance to the next SF page, I do the task "grab stuff from old list", scan the old list for something else that needs doing, then return to the SF page, work through it, and move on.

At the moment I each time pick something that ought to be done now and put it into the action immediately. Maybe later I'll pick things for the end of the SF list instead.

(In my peculiar case, I also happened to have 4 half-full pages which I grabbed to jumpstart the process.)

If you have problems with getting the 2nd columns into your notebook, you can do without them: Just have two separate sheets of paper in addition to your notebook. One is called "urgent", the other one is called "unfinished". Then work through your notebook in the normal way, using the urgent list in addition as the second column of the current page. Whenever something from the page or from the urgent list is done but not finished, put it on the unfinished list. Whenever something urgent comes to your mind, put it on the urgent list. If the urgent list is completely done, go to the next page in the notebook and swap the lists: urgent (which is now empty, i.e. all the items are crossed out) becomes the unfinished list, and the unfinished list (which contains the unfinished tasks from the previous page) becomes now the urgent list. Then work on the current page and the urgent list as before.

I do like keeping them separated, but not SO separated that the work list is invisible when I'm on "personal time", and vice-versa. That's why I always liked the stalagmite/stalactite method for other versions of AF.

The separation helps me stay focused on appropriate tasks. I often work from home, which can tend to blur the division between work and personal. But at the same time, I like to stay aware of the other list, in case anything urgent needs dealing with.


For now, I'm trying something new for this problem, rather than stalagmite/stalactite or backwards/forwards. I am using separate pages, but all in the same SAF book, and all in one sequence. So far it's working just fine.

For example, when I started up the new SAF list a few days ago, I started a new Personal page, and also a new Work page. As new tasks come up, I put them on the appropriate kind of page. (Right now, I have two active pages of each type: first a Personal Page, then a Work Page, then another Personal Page, then another Work page -- all interspersed.)

During working hours, I process my Work pages, following all the rules strictly. I glance through my Personal pages whenever I happen to pass through them, especially the 2nd column, and I might take some action on this or that, but it is purely a matter of "taking a break" for a few minutes -- or in one or two cases, to deal with small but urgent personal tasks that arose during the day like calling the phone guy to come fix our phone line (can't call in the evening!). I do not enforce ANY of the SAF rules on those pages, and usually just skip through them.

Likewise, on personal time, I process the Personal pages, strictly following the SAF rules, but still might glance thru the Work pages, especially the 2nd column.

Sometimes there's a bit of confusion on handling the urgent tasks. Let's say it's working time, but I realize I need to make an urgent personal phone call in the next hour or two, and it's going to take more than just a few moments. Do I write it on my Personal page, 2nd column? Or on the Work page, 2nd column? I've decided to just write it on the 2nd column of whatever page I happen to be on, not paying any attention to whether it's a Personal or Work task.

Another kind of potential list-confusion: If I am at home with the kids, but I remember some urgent thing I need to do at work the next day, which 2nd column do I write it in? In this case I write it on the next open Work page, 2nd column.

In the end it doesn't seem to matter much which 2nd column it goes on, since no matter which "context" I am currently in, I am still scanning the 2nd columns of the other "context" also. So it has worked great so far.

(But it's only the third day, so maybe I'll find other issues!)
