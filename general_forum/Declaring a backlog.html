Well, I finally did it: I declared a backlog on my AF4-list.

When starting AF4 I was reluctant to start a new list, and initially I've been working with my old list, consisting of ~230 tasks. I had the impression I could keep up with that, but over the past weeks I've noticed that it takes way too long to draw a new closed-list-line (7 weeks since the last time :-). Because of that, I kept working small bursts on tasks that otherwise I would have dismissed weeks ago, and as a result my list kept getting longer and longer.

So now I'm back to a fresh list, with 33 tasks over 2 pages. Heaven!

2009-11-13 0:52
When I moved onto AF4 I had a pretty large backlog, around 40 pages. I tried it in the list, but never made any progress at all - too depressing/daunting/... and other words beginning with d. Then I removed it and started from a clean slate - didn't work at all for me, I suffered a lot of stress about the significant stuff on the old list that was now out of sight/out of mind and would never be seen again.

What worked for me was putting one or two (max) pages of the backlog into my filofax (easier with a ring binder of course) at a time. This meant that my closed list was running around five pages long. I cleared my backlog in four to five weeks.
