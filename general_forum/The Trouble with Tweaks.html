Original Post

2009-05-11 6:20
I have come to realise that pretty well all the tweaks mentioned here so far may affect how well intuition can work in Autofocus.

In particular, the major issue (for me as well) seems to be the desire to visit every page at least once a day, which for some of us is essential.

If we are failing to do this, in the normal run of things intuition will cause us to skim rapidly through the pages on the first pass, processing items with very low time cost, and postponing the nitty gritty till a second pass.

But if we are using an alternative method such as index card or moving backwards from the current, that process of intuition will be inhibited.

So while the tweaks I have seen are very clever and are doing the job, I am back to using the original at present. Who was it that said "It's so simple, it must be true."

Hi Laurence,

After working this long with AF, I've found that one important realization I have had is that following AF has made me conscious of how important it is to work with rather than against my energy levels. I had never really paid much conscious attention to my energy level before, being so used to either pushing myself or letting myself fall into long periods of inertia. And I've also learned that I can manipulate/increase my energy level by bucking the system. If I do that, work (and play) can be an entirely different experience. As such, I could make the argument that I'm taking intuition one step further by letting it tell me by my level of resistance to following the rules, when it's time to *not* follow AF according to the rules.

So I don't just have ONE tweak for AF, I probably have three or more that I choose from based on how I feel. Some days, I have no difficulty following AF exactly according to the rules. But sometimes I will start off following the rules, and if I find myself not wanting to continue or to do some procrastinating type of behaviour, I'll ask myself if I am willing to continue if I don't have to follow the rules and can do anything on the list that I want. Usually that works for another couple of major tasks, where otherwise, I might have just stopped and called it quits for the night. And then there's days that I don't want to do anything on the list, but am willing to work very hard on something else that isn't on the list but is something that will move me towards my goals. And I can guarantee that on Saturdays, I have a strong desire to finish off at least 2-3 of my oldest active pages, and I'll go through it in order like a dreaded to-do list without any regard to intuition at all, and feel wonderful about it.

But the beauty of the system is that I am learning how to go back and forth without disrupting anything (too badly) and making a heck of a lot of progress on what's important to me - which is the point of it all, isn't it?
