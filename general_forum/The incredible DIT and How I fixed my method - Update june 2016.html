Plan
1. Collecting all information about contacts for making emailings and call easely
tool : NIMBLE. It sort the best people and goes on the social networks to get information about what people gives. At one glance you know who people are and what they are doing.

2. Collecting informations. Use a paper note book. Here is my method.
a) Skip a few pages 3-4
b) On the first available date it « tomorrow » ie Friday june 24 th.

PLANNING

TOMORROWS PAGE : Treat it as an inbox
If you have an idea during the day put it here.
IF it is a note about anything put it here.
If you plan to do something put it here.
If something in incoming client etc put it here.
If there is and urgency put it here (but do it after writing it)
If you are writing something from others put it here

As there will be many information use colors (yellow for must doing things), green if you are waiting for something from other. Use bullet symbols dor that tasks (-), notes (.) urgencies (*)

TODAY’S page.
For starting the system just list what you plan to do today. You may use priorities.
report from Nimble, notes or every paper notes what is important and worth to do.

Now Act! Work on your today’s list, Do it ! report incoming stuff on tomorrow’s page.Dont worry tomorrow you will work and treat tomorrow’s page.

TOMORROW MORNING
- use a 4 colors bic
red make a revision of your notes on your paper book. Cross stuff.
- If nothing is available on a page cross the all page. This will help you to focus on what is important and left.
- Then make priorities. If you have an idea which absolutly needs to be done today report it on today’s page (only exception of the system)
- Choose you big rocks for today (NO MORE THAN 3)
- TRY to plan your core projects and think about how much time they will take. 3 minutes.

Then
Report stuff for following on NIMBLE. Make your prospection. Takes appointments. Make analysis of your buildings or projects Use your note book and the help of NIMBLE. ACT ! ACT! and ACT ! Forget all the system. Take habits.

Hope that's help.
