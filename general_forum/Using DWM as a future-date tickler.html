Just thinking out loud here.... I have a task to 'dryclean my winter coat' which can be done no sooner than mid-April-ish (weather permitting LOL). I have 2 choices ....

- Put it on a separate 'do in April' ticker list and move it to DWM when I review the tickler list in early April and the first time it's added to DWM it goes on the April 30 page (new task, today+28).

or

- Put it directly on the DWM April 30 page.

Since my current active list is February 23 - March 23, having the item on Apr 30 is just as 'invisible' on a future DWM page vs a separate tickler list. In both cases, it comes into the active pages in early April (28 day month) (or very late March (30 day month) ). So on the face of it, there isn't a difference between the two except it's in on one list - which seems to be a plus for DWM.

I checked other threads and this idea came up briefly and Mark's concern about doing this was related to re-dating pages due to unexpected breaks. Since the item, at least in this case, doesn't have an external due-date (it doesn't matter on which specific date I take the coat to the drycleaners - PICKING UP the coat afterwards, yes, but then it'll go on the calendar), re-dating pages won't negatively impact this kind of task. Tasks that do have external due-dates may not work as well having DWM as the tickler, I don't know.

To be fair, I haven't had any unexpected breaks that needed pages to be re-dated or items dismissed just because of a break, so I may be missing something because of no first-hand experience.

So... DWM as tickler, yay or nay? (or more likely... try it and report back LOL)

In my Outlook implementation of DWM, I'm doing exactly what Lillian describes.

I don't want my DWM list to be cluttered with things that CAN'T be done yet. I don't want any items to show up on the list until they CAN be done. I filter the tasks in Outlook so I only see the coming month.

(1) I can put it in a tickler of some kind (electronic of 43-folders style or whatever), and then when it shows up in the tickler, I can then copy it to the 30-day page in DWM.

2010-02-25 4:13
DWM dates, in the absence of renumbering, are LAST POSSIBLE START dates. So put an appointment on its exact date, and it will work out.

DWM dates also imply FIRST POSSIBLE START dates. A task can only start when its date is one month (or less) in the future. You can put "due items" on their due date, but that implies you won't start until a month before.

So figure out the earliest date you COULD start, and write the task 1 month after that date. Failing that, figure when you MUST start, and write the task on that date if it's more than a month out.
