Yes Avrum

I now call it "Major Review". Even with AF it is easy for lots of things to slip through the cracks and for me to lose a sense of priorities in my life. Hence I just put "Major Review" as a task in AF and do it when it stands out.

One of the reasons why I went cold on GTD is that I could very rarely get around the the Weekly Review and I would usually skimp it through lack of time. Now, I do it when I feel ready (maybe about once every ten days) and it is a great way of taking stock and renewing my sense that everything is in order.

Reply by Mike

2009-05-30 11:26
My reviews have not changed in years. I do them weekly, monthly (coincident with the weekly), and yearly (coincident with the weekly but usually taking much longer.

Reviews to me mean aligning goals, projects and actions. Personally, I don't think of AF as having anything to say about goals. So every week I review where I stand with respect to my goals and assure that I have projects running to advance them, and actions on my AF list to move the projects forward. (Sometimes I have the project listed on AF ... it all depends.)

As to resistance to reviews, I've never had a problem with that. I suppose because I did not make it some horrendous thing as described in GtD. I simply go over my goals for the period and assure myself that I'm advancing ... and take the appropriate steps to move forward during the next week. It is all very casual. Just me and my list and a pencil (or sometimes a computer file.) I've read so many people complaining about the GtD review and wondered why they just don't give it up and review their goals instead.

David - I think you're right that GTD itself doesn't make the weekly review a horrendous task, but people build up resistance precisely because of its being 'weekly'. Say your weekly review should be done on a Monday, but for whatever reason it can't be. Then suddenly it's a task you're feeling guilty about because you *should* have done it the day before. That makes it loom large and resistance sets in. That's why having it on the AF list is such a better approach for these 'quasi-regular' tasks
