Leon:

The idea of AF is that it sifts what you put in it and concentrates on what is important or right for you at the time. So you should be able to open up any number of projects and some will die and some will stand still and some will progress.

What you must be careful of is committing yourself to something in such a way that you can't get out of it. This is normally when the commitment is made to other people. In this case I would advise using Rainer's suggestion. Or simply saying that your time is fully committed at the moment.

It might help to track how your AF list is expanding (or contracting!) over time. I put today's date next to each item as I cross it off. Then at the end of the day I draw a line under the last item, and write down three numbers:

First, the number of items I added today, found by counting the items between this line and the previous one.
Second, the number of items I completed today, found by scanning all the pages.
Third, the number of active items, found by (previous active count + items added - items completed)

Now I can easily flip back through my list and see how its size has changed over time - and if it ever grew a lot for several days in a row, I'd know that I was taking on more than I was doing.

I think the important thing to remember is that just because it is on the list does not mean that it "must" be done - but could be a "possible" as Mark calls it. The other thing to remember is that the list can capture "everything". Therefore it provides a real sense of security, in that, once you get used to entering everything on your list, you always know where to find things.

Johnners point about expanding lists is an important one - some days my items will increase drastically, usually if I have a really creative day when a lot of thoughts spring to mind - or I am working on a project which generates a lot of new items. Although I maintain a seperate project list, my early thoughts will go into AF, may then migrate to my project list to be sorted, and ultimately come back to my AF list.
