I'm quite astonished how well AF4 keeps the number of pages down. I converted from RAF (AF3) and had about 10-15 active pages. Now I never seem to get beyond 3 or 4. The forced reviews are what makes this happen (i.e. not wanting to do something from the closed list, but rather return to the open list). I find that I'm doing reviews every couple of days or so.

In order not to keep highlighting many items and copy them over and over again, I've been thinking hard about why some tasks are not getting cleared. Often, it's because I really don't intend to work on these in the next few days. So instead of dismissing outright, or mindlessly copying back to the end of the open list, I've been using my tickler files (see

http://members.optusnet.com.au/~charles57/GTD/tickler.html

if you don't understand the 43-folders "tickler" system) more, to bring tasks back into the open AF list at just the right time. I use one AF4 notebook for both home and work, but have separate tickler files at each location.

The benefits of placing longer-lead items into the tickler are that the AF4 list stays even more compact, and only a few tasks get highlighted for review.
