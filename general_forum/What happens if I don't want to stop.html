Margherita:

Sounds like you've got the opposite problem to most people!

I don't know enough about you to be able to answer this question myself, but is the fact that you are leaping ahead with your art projects telling you something? How were you doing before with your art - did you have the same problem before or was your art languishing a bit? Maybe this is the crest of a wave that you should be riding for a bit.

However if you feel you do need to limit it, then you can simply set yourself an upper limit, e.g. " I will work on this for not more than an hour", then re-enter the task at the end of the list.

Another way is to take the art projects off the list altogether and have a set period each day during which you work on your art and nothing else. Out of that time, you can do anything except work on your art.

Very interesting post/s. For some months I've been going to the studio for two days a week - the other artists are usually absent or quiet, and there is no internet, and treat it as a 9 to 5 job, stopping only for lunch in the cafe across the road.

In fact I just finished reading Steven Pressfield's "The War of Art", about overcoming resistance, when Mark unleashed AF - this clever integration and refinement of his earlier ideas. So now, it appears, I must give in to resistance after all. But of course not while I am in the Studio!

(As an aside perhaps I can recommend the above book to fellow artists here, about resistance and many forms it can take.)

But I am facing the issue that has cropped up in a few threads already about different lists. In my case they are MLO outlines. I have one for the Studio, one for Home, and one for Office. The Office is at home, but I find it useful to set boundaries around what I do, and when. But some Studio related things can only be done at home. E.g. my binding machine and internet are at home/office, where I do my printing and sometimes internet research on recommended page layouts (I have my first performance coming up in a few months), while my composing and nearly all the creative work is done at the Studio miles away. I can do some internet research at the library near the Studio (if I get to it early enough!), but occasionally websites I need are blocked (they all need to be approved by some mysterious library authority).

Likewise my home/office tasks are mostly separate but sometimes could be on either list, if the computer is being recalcitrant (not uncommon), I might prefer not to wait till Monday to process "Fix computer". And of course, I want to "Check AF forum" in both Home and Office lists.

So this Venn diagram situation of partly interlocking lists is a spur to improving my set up. I'm only about half way through reading these posts (which seem to be expanding as fast as I can read them), but if this issue has been solved yet I'd love to know.

Generally I am getting different things done than usual, and - I think - being more productive.

And I really appreciate the creation of this forum which is, it seems to me, quite a work of art in itself.

At this point I am just letting those lists evolve naturally and will see where they go.

I think we will all find that we naturally evolve the number of location lists that are right for us as we go along and get more attuned to the system. My gut feeling is that the number of lists I use may well change. I would say just go with what feels right - if it needs tweaking for you later then you can easily do so.

My projects tend to vary, and I am also finding that some larger tasks I am treating as projects, as that enables me gain control over them.

In some cases I will just include the steps for a large task/project as they come to mind, eg "review project x", "set up system for project x", "liaise with Y re marketing plan for project x" etc. In other cases I will just have "project z" and have separate files, lists etc for that project in their own location. Projects naturally generate a lot of "stuff", be it files, boxes, notes, digital media etc and having a list of tasks to complete for that project helps keep me on track. What I am finding is that I am now treating those lists in an AF way rather than as standard To Do lists.

As a general rule I am finding that those projects which need to be progressed as urgent and important (e.g. do tax return) tend to gravitate towards the first example and have the steps listed on my main AF list. Other less urgent projects (e.g. update my Family Tree) are more likely to end up on my list as just Family Tree (or Project Z),

Hope that clarifies a bit more. You might find the following thread on projects to be of help also
