Zane, I really do think that fast-5 is very similar to the No S diet. For me it's the restricting of the snacking. I know some people do fast-5 as a "window" and eat for 5 hours. I don't, I eat one meal around 2 p.m. and one around 6-7 p.m. That's it. I honestly don't get hungry until maybe 11 a.m., then it goes away and doesn't come back until mid afternoon. The key for me is that the 2 p.m. meal has to a) have > 25g of protein & b) be ~ 750 calories. That ensures that it'll tide me over so that I am not actually hungry when I eat the second and last meal of the day. (And not hungry when I do a run through the grocery store either!)

Fortunately, I don't have sugar cravings and don't eat huge portions. My poison is high salt foods and (used to be) wobbly pops. I quit that though since AF gives me another hobby. :-)

Hi Jessica, thanks!

Re. the cleaning, sometimes it stood out with AF1 pretty naturally on its own. If I was seeing that I wouldn't even touch cleaning using AF1 that day, I was switching to AF2 and writing what needed to be done and the time I would take (used my microwave timer). So I was (at the time) sort of doing RAF in the reverse way, doing AF1 and switching to AF2 at the end of the day. If you give yourself 3 minutes to clean the living room, you can move super fast and get it 50% done - which is good enough for me! At some point, I'd like to be able to do that, and get it 95% done, I never want to be 100% (unless I'm selling a house).

So, I would take the major rooms and write at the end of the list:

- kitchen - 3 min.
- bedroom - 3 min.
- bathroom - 3 min.
- LR/DR - 3 min.
- library - 3 min.

and do whatever "stood out" (sometimes literally!) in that room. I'd rotate a bit and put in hallways, kids room, basement, laundry room, etc. occasionally. (I have a very large house though.) I didn't count the garage, laundry (daily every a.m.) or dishes in the cleaning time. The laundry and dishes are part of my *new*! morning routine.

I think one of the things that's really helping is that I'm about 25% decluttered and am getting more ruthless with purging as I go along. I eventually want to get back to the minimalism of my 20's and downsize to a little house (I just bought this house to flip). Stuff doesn't make me happy, people and experiences do. :-)
