«Aha, misunderstood the animated demo then.»

The demo consists of a single day. The open list is not closed at the beginning of the day, it is closed somewhere in the middle when Mark completes a first pass of the closed list without taking action, dismissing all tasks in the closed list. If you point out confusing parts in the demo, I can correct them.

«What would you do with urgent tasks if you can't work on anything outside of closed list then?»

There is never a situation in which you can't work on something outside the closed list. You can move out of the closed list at any time. The only difference - if you didn't do a single task on the closed list beforehand, you have to dismiss the tasks in the closed list. If you did even the tiniest little thing for any task on the closed list, you can move into the open list at any time without dismissing anything.

«Or maybe with not-really-urgent, but still requiring faster reply than you would provide after finishing the long closed list?»

Same as above, just move out of the closed list into the open list and do it. You don't have to finish the closed list before doing stuff from the open list. Watch the demo again and note how often Mark actions tasks on his open list.
