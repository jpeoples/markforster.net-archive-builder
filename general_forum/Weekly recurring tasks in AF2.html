I can see how Autofocus handles daily or irregularly recurring tasks such as exercise, but what about weekly ones, such as 'do weekly review'? Say I do the review, cross it off and rewrite it at the end of the list. Then I won't want to do it again for another seven days, but still want to be reminded of it.

Several days later I look for the first block of unactioned tasks, including my weekly review. I draw the line underneath, so putting them all 'on notice'. The next day, I'm still not at the end of the week, I don't want to do the review, so the review would get dismissed, according to the rules.

The simplest answer, I guess, it to make it a calendar item? Or is there a better solution? Or am I missing something obvious (again)?

I'm using an electronic approach and, for this, I'm just filtering the list for tasks that I can start now. For a weekly review task, I would schedule it recurring so it can only start in a week from now and therefore not show up in my list until then.

For a manual approach, I think the equivalent would be to use a tickler file which you check daily.

Putting these items on your calendar will quickly swamp it, I think.

Linda:

The way I handle this type of recurring task (weekly or monthly etc) is to put a reminder in Outlook Tasks. When the task appears I dismiss the task for a week or month or whatever the appropriate period is and write the task at the end of my AF list.

So the reminder is not to do the task, but to put it back on the list.

Elsewhere I described my "every" lists. I have one page which contians the following lists (in very terse format:

- Things I do EVERY DAY (both by hour and not assigned a particular time)
- Things I do EVERY WEEK (by day of week)
- Things I do EVERY MONTH (by day of month)
- Things I do EVERY YEAR (by date)

The last includes things which are not due EVERY year but on the same date every so many years. (Like "renew passport" which comes up every 10 years ... next in October of 2013 ... and "renew driver's license" which comes up every forth year, and "renew car registration" which comes up every other year, etc.)

At a glance I can see what is on the horizon while not having to leaf through a calendar to notice them. I might ALSO put them on the calendar so I'll see blocked time, for example, and not schedule something to conflict.

If you are smaller format based (as I am, mostly) you could make up an index card for each category and need only four of them ... and they also come in pretty florescent colors ;-)
