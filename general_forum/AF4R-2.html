Merry Christmas to you too, Jupiter!

I am actually trialing a version of AF4 + 3TS that incorporates almost all that I have learned over the past year in various versions and tweaks of DWM and AF, and so far it achieves ALL of your requirements...and then some! ^___^

In summary, it has

1) AF4R's distinction of new/old, recurring, and unfinished tasks, but instead of putting them in distinct columns like AF4R, I am keeping them in the list with different symbols like in my practice in DWM2:

> New task
o Finished recurrent task
● Unfinished task

2) Tweaked 3TS as I detailed here: http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1346124#item1346300

3) Maintenance checklists: http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1253799

4) Project files as described by Alan: http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1319077

5) The "Filtering/Pre-Filtering" AF4 tweak: http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1262814#item1265058

6) How I process the open and closed lists is now as Mark detailed it in AF4R: work on the closed list until you are done for now, then process the open list until you are done, then rinse; at least one task (hopefully more) must be done in the closed list upon transferring there, otherwise all remaining old tasks are dismissed. This is to facilitate the processing of Unfinished Tasks.

The system so far has worked very well. My observations:

1) It is fast, slim and nimble, yet can still accept and process anything you can throw at it because of the filtering/pre-filtering tweak. It never bloats up with useless tasks, but always presents you with relatively few but relevant tasks.

2) Recurring tasks are either placed into maintenance checklists or placed on review by filtering/pre-filtering until they are needed to be done; the AF thus remains uncluttered.

3) The provision of one possible urgent task into the 3TS makes the whole system able to process urgent tasks very powerfully and flexibly (I have not properly tried SAF, but I would be surprised if it processes urgent tasks better).

4) The 3TS has exceeded the "separate piece of paper" tweak that I had trialed previously in http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1305619#item1319853 in promise. It glues the whole thing together; it efficiently incorporates AF4, projects and checklists into one -almost- seamless system; re-writing is minimal.

5) The 3TS also achieves the "pull" effect described in http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1335203 : by drawing a line in between days of use of the 3TS paper, one can see the rough volume of work done in a day, and that is very fulfilling and motivating.

6) The whole system still processes unfinished tasks powerfully...more powerfully, in fact, than with my previous DWM2 version, which is quite a surprise.

I will try this system more before I post the more-or-less finalized instructions, but there should be enough details here for anyone to try making something like it or incorporate some of the ideas into his/her system.

God bless, Jupiter, and good luck with your tweak! I'll try to help if I can.
