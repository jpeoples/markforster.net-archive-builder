Yeah, I ran into the same issue. So now I write both personal and work items on the same page.

In column 1, here is what I do: I write work tasks starting from the top of the page, going down. I write personal tasks starting from the bottom of the page, going up. When they meet, I draw a very heavy line to separate them.

For column 2, I just mix them both in together -- though you could use the same top-down, bottom-up approach for column 2 if you wanted. I just find it adds needless complexity.

What happens if I am "working" and I come to a page with only personal items in Column 1? (OR vice-versa.) Usually I can take some small incidental action and it serves as a nice short break. But if I can't take any action, then I follow the rules and dismiss the lot of them. Dismiss doesn't mean they disappear -- it just means I need to figure out why they are getting dismissed. Sometimes it means I need to take a day off from work to deal with the backlog of personal items (or vice-versa, I need to work some overtime). Sometimes it means I need to audit my commitments to see why this is happening. What's nice is the "review dismissed items" process takes care of that already -- I don't need a new special process.

What happens if I am working and come to a personal item in Column 2? Again, if I can take a short break to take some action on that task, all is well. But if I really can't do it right now because I need to focus on work, then I just move it forward. This is in accordance with Mark's tip, if you physically can't do something in Column 2, then move it forward.

I've been doing this for about a week and it's been working pretty well.
