Cruisader,

Just to be clear, I'm not on a crusade to eliminate your system. . . I'm just hashing it out with you, because I assume that is what you are looking for on this thread.

You said: <>

Here's the thing. As I understand it in FM of RAF the greatest length of time it would take for a page to be entirely put on review would be equal to or less than half the total number of lines on a page. So for a 30 line page, if every other item had been done, and you placed the line after the first continuous block every day ---> it would take 15 days for all these items to be put on review.

But, that is the worst case scenario. For that to happen you would have had to action every other item on the page and then never action another one. Unlikely, no? For two reasons:

1) at least a few contiguous blocks likely have MORE than one item, and for each 2 additional item in a block that is one less day it would take for all the items to be put on review. So if your first block has 6 items in it, it would take only (15-6/2)=12 days for the whole page to be put on review. You can see how quickly this would mount up, given a few multi-item contiguous blocks. And;

2) more importantly with a *closed list* there is an engine which propels you to action (even a little! not even to completion!) at least *one* item on this closed page. If you don't you leave the page and go back into RM. So, either you chip away at this page, or you never get past it in FM, *or* you realize that the items (or some of them) on this page are *never* going to get done for some reason and then you just outright delete them.

So, again, worst case scenario with a 30 line page is that item #30 of page 1 would be reached in 15 days or less, and you would be forced to deal with it. In contrast, in your system, the worst case scenario is that item #30 of page 1 might not be on the radar for 30 days.

The other problem is losing the "autofocus" element. I take the autofocusing aspect of AF to be that *items* are put in focus, and not just ends of list are put in focus (a'la the dualfocus system in your other thread). If the first page is closed I *have* to deal with it somehow. Either I chip away at it "little and often", or I realize they will never get done and delete them. The tension is that FM wants me to go forward, but won't let me until I do something. *That* is the engine.

In your system you can ignore all the items on page one for 30 days, and work on page 2 items, or even page 3 or 4 items! You could ignore 3 pages of items for 90 days and blissfully work on page 4. Not so in RAF. Those first 3 pages will be dealt with either by a)your desire to get to the next page, or b) the inexorable line of doom which eats up multiple items quickly, or c) common sense which tells you to just delete items to get to the next page.

Or perhaps my maths are off, and I'm misunderstanding everything. In that case, please tell me to go jump in the lake! : )

--Joseph

It's always difficult to judge the effectiveness of a system just from reading it, but with that proviso the impression I got from reading through your description was that you were badly weakening "forward mode".

What you have in the two main modes is two powerful tools for dealing with tasks. Reverse mode is best for urgent and less weighty items and forward mode is best for less urgent and more weighty items - though they are not mutually exclusive.

One of the great advantages of the Revised AF system is that both tools are available to be used when appropriate. If one is weakened then the other has to take up the slack by doing the job it isn't best at.
