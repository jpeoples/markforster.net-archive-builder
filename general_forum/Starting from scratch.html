Donna,

I think the choice of system is based upon a persons analysis of their personality and work style. All of the systems I have designed have been to combat specific issues I have had and problems I have needed to solve to be more efficient. Donna, this is an excellent opportunity to start as you mean to go on, and I'm sure you will get plenty of advice here.

I love Marks book 'Do It Tomorrow'. I have a problem with using this system in it's purest form because many of my current customers are very used to saying 'jump' and me immediately saying 'how high'. If you are working for an organisation you can start by setting expectations on when you get back to them on issues, emails and so on. I would highly recommend his book on the DIT system.

Having said that, you could click on the autofocus system link at the top of the page and read up on this system - in my view the simplest option.

I never got on with the DWM system - too much spread over too many pages for my liking. Mark has hinted that he has a new system he is testing with the best bits from all his previous systems, and I am eagerly awaiting further details on that. In the mean time I am using AF. One tweak that works for me as I have alot of projects is that I have a sheet for each project with a list of tasks for that project - and it is the project name that goes on AF and not the idividual tasks. This helps me get a better overview of my work, as well as having the detail when I need it.

Hi Donna,

Whatever system you decide to use, I would suggest trying to keep your incoming work as upto date as possible & try to keep clear of building up a backlog of work. This feels better / less stressful (to me, anyway) to turn up for work every day.

My personal view is that any system where long lists can feature (GTD, Autofocus) tend to make it easier to defer tasks whilst allowing many other tasks to creep in. And this can feel overwhelming.

To that end, At the moment (I change my mind a lot) I tend to favour some of the concepts from Do It Tommorow, particularly with regard to a short daily 'will do' checklist for dealing with incoming work. I use time boxing for other tasks.

Good Luck!

If it must be done immediately, do it immediately. Otherwise just record it immediately. In AF4 for example it just goes on your list at the end. You can think later about what exactly is expected of you, when you get to it. Stuff done regularly, simply put it on your list with a date saying when it must be finished. When one month's work is done, either write in the next month's work item right away, or else wait until something else (calender entry; tickler file) triggers you to write it in.

I'm sure your job can keep you busy, but I see no reason you can't be very successful following a system like this.
