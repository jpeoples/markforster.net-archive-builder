Hi All,

I bought my lovely wife a Kindle 2 which arrived in the post yesterday. I want to share some of my initial impressions with you ... with more to follow as I gain experience.

1) WOW! The packaging is better than a lot of products bought elsewhere! No kidding, very cool.

2) It is small and light... about the size of a large paperback but very thin and light. I got the cover and a reading light with it for a total price of just under $350.

3) I wish the contrast were a little bit greater, but it is not bad. It is not back lighted so you need a reading light for night. The small one I got has two speeds ... LED lights which run on 3xAAA batteries. Clips nicely on top of the Kindle.

4) Buying books is a snap. You do get them in under a minute. Jeff Bezos (father of Amazon and the Kindle) claims "under 60 seconds) but privately says it takes about 12 seconds if you have a good connection. The books are a bit more expensive than the dead tree versions ... despite what Jeff claims. He compares $9.99 for most best sellers to the hard backed versions. But if you buy the paperback, they cost sometimes 1/2 that much. But you do get the convenience. You don't have to gas up the buggy (which costs way more than the book these days ;-), drive to the store, and buy more than you wanted ;-) Click right from the Kiindle and you have it instantly. If your time is worth anything this is the way to go.

5) Books are downloaded over Amazon's private Spring Network ... Amazon pays the tab for the network. They are keeping it a bit quiet but you can actually use the Kindle over that 3G EVDO network to do other things. If it becomes too widely advertised Amazon might start charging for it. ;-(

6) You can copy some of your files to the Kindle by plugging it into your computer's USB port so it looks like a disk drive. It will read Word, .TXT, and MOBI, as well as a couple of other things ... but not PDF. The new, larger DX reads .PDF. Of course, you can get free software to convert anything to anything these days so that is not a show stopper.

7) You can actually access the internet over the damn thing ... but it is slow and klunky. You can send email using your Gmail account, access Wikipedia, Google Search, etc.

8) While reading you can get a dictionary lookup for any word you put the cursor on. Way cool.

9) It has a rudimentary text-to-speech facility so it will read the book to you over its small stereo speakers or a plugged in headset. It is not fantastic and the words sometimes run together and can be puzzling. But I suppose you get used to it. The idea is that you can "read" on the treadmill.

10) There are a gazillion free ebooks out there ... all of those in the public domain which amounts to all of the classical literature ever written. JB's vision is to have every book ever published in digital format. He may just do it. Oh, and if your family has multiple Kindles, you can share the books ... up to six Kindles on a single account. AND, it will, if you select the option, synchronize the books over their network so that when you close the book on one Kindle and open it on another, it remembers where you left off reading.

11) Jeff's vision for the kindle was to make it like a book. When he asked himself what was the distinguishing feature of a "book" it was that it "disappeared". IOW, once you started reading you forgot you were holding a book. He has actually achieved that with the Kindle, IMO. You only need to press one button (next page) with either thumb and that is like turning pages. Nothing else to mess with while reading.

Bottom line ... I'm very impressed with this gizmo. I was not going to get one for myself but I'm now reconsidering. I might spring for the DX ($500) because of the PDF ability and higher resolution. And I don't mind the almost double weight. And, of course, my eyes are getting older and I can use the bigger screen. I really think that this thing is going to revolutionize reading. So what if Steve Jobs says it is a non-stater because no one reads anymore. Harumph, for those of us who do, it is ground breaking,

After I catch up on some sleep (I've been fooling with the damn thing since we got it yesterday) I'm going to convert some of my PDFs into Kindle format and see how that goes. Oh, and find out why my background music won't play on it (I think it is because I have it in a sub directory). Did I mention that? It plays music as well! Oh yeah, and shows pictures. And has a slideshow mode to turn the pages for you and .... on and on and on.

Please feel free to ask questions. They will stimulate my investigation ;-)
