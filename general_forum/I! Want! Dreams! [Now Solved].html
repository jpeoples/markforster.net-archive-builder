(Sorry for the headline, but I wanted to generate some attention.)

It's about Mark's second book "How to Make Your Dreams Come True". As everybody knows, this book is sold out, there will be no reprint and Mark is planning to release it as a (free?) ebook.

At the moment, the ebook is not yet available, however, and even if it were, I'd still prefer an old-fashioned paperback.

What makes me write yet another (I already did one) post about this is that I had some weird experiences with Amazon.co.uk's "Marketplace". (Important information: I live in Germany.)

So far, I've tried to buy the "Dreams" book numerous times (in addition to what I already wrote on another occasion). At one point in time, it suddenly reappeared as available by Amazon.co.uk, but an order I placed went unanswered (unlike the earlier attempt where at least I got a negative one), and it was listed as "out of stock" again. Then, I tried to buy a "new" or at least "used - good" copy from a third-party vendor via Amazon UK's "Marketplace". (BTW, the book never appears on Amazon .com or .de "Marketplace", only in the UK.) I've bought books from both Amazon's UK and US sites and their "Marketplace" sections before to be shipped to me in Germany, and never had any problems. But this time, it was very different. Every time I tried to order the book (I tried this every time I found a copy offered for less than £ 15 plus shipping, which would be my upper limit), I got to some point in the ordering procedure where I was suddenly told that the item cannot be dispatched to the address I entered. There was no indication before that it would only be shipped inside the UK. I don't know if this is a change in Amazon UK's policies, or if it is some kind of database error. The weirdest part of it all is that those offers always disappeared totally after my mishaps.
A question to Amazon's support about this phenomenon went unanswered. I can't say that I was surprised.
It looks like they decided to neither sell anything nor even talk to non-Brits anymore.

To make a long story short: Is there a nice soul in the UK willing to risk some quid (£ 15 plus shipping max), buy it for me, let me send over the money and re-ship it to my address in Germany?
