Web browsing easily gets out of control The problem is the new thing always seems important. The solution is Autofocus: record that shiny new page to look at later, and stick to the current task. You could write them on your list, but it's cumbersome writing the address out and later typing it back in. Bookmarks could work, but it's too much having to manage bookmarks.

A better solution: I got the "Read It Later" plugin for Firefox. Read It Later makes a good AF style list of pages to visit later. And my master AF list only has "Read web" for idle reading.

Now if I see a good link, I click on it, click "Read it Later", and stick to the previous page. Then in Firefox I click "Read Something", see a list of things to read (oldest to newest), and process that list in AF fashion. Pick a link, read it, "Mark as Read", and if I want to read it again, click "Read it Later", and the page goes to the end of the list!

(If you don't have or want that "Read It Later", my best suggestion for you is to Ctrl-Click on links. That will make a new tab that you can get to another time.)

What I find when using it is just how non-essential a lot of that saved to-read stuff is. By clicking the page to be read later, I satisfy that pesky novelty-circuit in my brain (I've seen something new), and I "promise" that part that I'll read it later. But I hardly ever get to it (actually, never, so far). Read It Later does its job by sweeping those distracting pages out of my way and parking them somewhere so I can go back "just in case." Even if "the case" never arrives, it somehow quiets that compulsing part of me, so I'm very grateful for that extension.
