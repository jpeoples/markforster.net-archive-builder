Hi Rick

No you don't work on the page until you have done everything in one sitting. You work on the first items on the page "that stands out" i.e. that you feel like doing, then move on to the next items that "stands out". Once no items stand out on that page (i.e. from an "I feel like doing this now" perspective as opposed to an "I should do this" prespective) then you move on to the next page. Obviously from a common sense perpective if something need to be done now then do it now, but there are usually a lot less tasks with that real urgency than we think.

There have been a number of posts on this issue, as you seem to be new to this my best advice is to spend a little time reading through the forum. I won't direct you to a specific post as you may well find that the forum answres questions you don't even know you have yet! Good Luck!

Mike, I would have to disagree with you regarding the last item on a page. I think you may be missing two important points about AF, firstly, that it works on a series of closed lists and is not one long list. It does not matter where the item is on a page, whether it is the 1st, 5th 20th or last. The page is a closed list and the item just a part of that "unit". If you do not cross off an item that has actioned, albeit partially, then you are losing the ability to complete all doable items on that page which enables the system to reject any (and all!) remaining items. Secondly AF works with our intuitive minds rather than our rational minds; yes common sense is vital but the system is set up in such a way that we are learning to do what "feels" right rather than what our previous rational approach has told us we "should" do. It is that very act of learning to recognise how we feel about something that eventually enables us to actually recognise the value of it's place in our lives (or not!!) Just try crossing that item off - I promise you it won't "feel" as bad as you think :-)

Rick and others:

Actually the instructions are quite clear about this. You treat the last page (i.e. the one which you are still writing tasks on) like any other page, except that you don't dismiss any items on it. This is because the last page might only contain a few items, all of which were recent re-entries, and you wouldn't want to start on any of them again almost immediately.

Once you've done every item you want to on the last page, you start again on the first active page.

In spite of what Jim says, my practice is to cross off and re-enter an item, even if it is on the next line. This is because crossing off an item is an important psychological reward, and re-writing the item gives a feeling of progress.
