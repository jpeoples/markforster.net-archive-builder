Every now and then, someone complains that they can't figure out how to navigate this forum and find recent posts they haven't already read.

Here is a method that works for me

(1) When I want to read the AF forum, I open the main forum page. I look at the date and time of the most recent post. I write that down on a sheet of paper (I use a large sticky on the last page of my AF book).

(2) I look for all the discussions whose date-stamp is newer than the date/time when I last visited the forum. I use Firefox as my browser, and control-click to open each of those newly-updated discussions quickly in a new tab. Then, when I look at each discussion, I scroll down to the end, then page back up till I hit the new posts. Since I know the date/time when I last visited the forum, it's easy to find the posts I haven't read yet.


Anyone else have a better method?

It took me a while to get SitDelta optimally set up. One thing important for watching the forum is to define the region that is being watched. You can do this by right-clicking on the delta icon the bottom right and select Regions -> +Watch Region, then use the mouse to select the region in which all the posts are. So you won't get any false positives from microscopic changes in the surrounding page. I also saved these settings as a preset for http://markforster.net/forum*, so it will be applied to any subpages of the forum I add.

Now open every thread you are interested in and click the Delta icon once to add that page to site delta. No you'll always get new posts in that discussions highlighted, and the forum overview can be used to see if there are new threads that might interest you. You then add these to SiteDelta, too.

So now you can set a timer interval for SiteDelta to look for changes, or you can do so manually by right-clicking the delta icon and selecting Pages -> Check all for Changes. If there are changed pages you get a red pages icon beside the delta icon. Right click this and select "open all in tabs". On each page now open click the delta icon once to have the new posts highlighted and scrolled to.
