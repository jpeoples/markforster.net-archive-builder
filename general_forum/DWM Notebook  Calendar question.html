I really don't have any axes to grind. I used the dated system for a full day. I didn't like the fact that my diary forced me to write in tiny, cramped, handwriting.

So, I switched to the dateless, numbered pages. Note, my main problem with the diary was not the dates, but the height (or lack thereof) of the page lines.

Given that I was custom-building my diary, I decided to build it, as much as possible to my own needs and my own specifications.

So, if you want to cast stones, please cast them at me, not at Alan Baljeu. I apologize for designating my implementation as Alan's. He was just providing a very nice conceptualization of DWM. I liked his conceptualization so much, that I "tweaked" DWM, if you want to call it a tweak. I don't. I think that I AM doing DWM.

I know that I have a vacation planned the week of February 15. So the numbered pages allow me to deal with that vacation seamlessly.

My system uses "clock arithmetic." If you are using a 24-hour clock, 2 hours after 23:00 is 1:00. My system has a 20-day monthly cycle. (I typically go to the office Monday-Friday.)

If today is day x, and I have to re-enter an item, I re-enter it on page x + 5. If I have a new item to enter I enter it on page x + 20. If today is day 17, a week from today is day 2. A month from today is day 17.

If I had a diary that I liked, in terms of size, I would have stuck with it. But I didn't, so I didn't.

To me, the difference between dated pages and numbered pages is about as significant as using black ink or blue ink. OK. That's a bit of an exaggeration. A better analogy would be ink versus pencil.

By the way, I am still very enthusiastic about DWM. This week I have had more discretionary time than is typical for me. So that helped. Nonethelss, the "feel" of DWM strikes me as suitable, even for days when I have much less discretionary time. It keeps my highly focused on those items which are closest to expiration. And it allows me to get a "win" every time I move my nearest active page further into the future.

Moises,

Why do you have to adjust your numbering for your days off? One of the really strong points of the numbered (vs. dated) approach is that the numbers can be interpreted as "days on which I am working the system". So if I am not using it for Saturday and Sunday, I can simply ignore the system and pick it up on Monday. Here is an illustration:

1 Thr
2 Fri

Take Sat and Sun off

3 Mon (resume using system)
4 Tue
5 Wed

Leave on vacation for a month

6 Mon (first day back, resume using the system)
7 Tue
8 Wed
9 Thr
10 Fri

Take weekend off

11 Mon

Etc. IOW, you never have to change numbering or alter the pages in any way. That was why the idea seemed brilliant to me. New items always go on today's number plus 30 and reentered items go on today's number plus 7.
