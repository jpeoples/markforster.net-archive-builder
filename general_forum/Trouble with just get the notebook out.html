Hi Jaqueline,

I would recommend time boxing. Plan out your discretionary time in advance, schedule x hours for the work notebook, y hours for the home notebook and perhaps z hours for flexible notebook use to accommodate for fluctuating balance between them. The point is, if you are in a time period you scheduled for home notebook use, you are not allowed to open the other notebook.

I use that approach since I currently work at home, so I have to draw sharp boundaries so I don't avoid the af list with the less appealing items.

Last weekend I switched to using a very small pocket notebook for home tasks (about 3" x 2"). I treat each 2-page "spread" as a page in the system, and fit around 17 items on a spread. It's small enough to keep in my pocket at all times, and I keep a pen in my pocket too.

This made a MASSIVE difference to me this weekend. Every time I found myself pausing and wondering what to do next, I would whip out my notebook and pick a task. I had a very productive Saturday, and it never felt like hard labour at all.

The physical task of getting my book out, and sitting down to leaf through it put me in a lethargic frame of mind. As I was sitting down to read the notebook, it was too tempting to stay sitting down and surf the net or read a novel.

One thing that really helps with AF is the freedom to give up a task when you're tired of it. This is especially important with house care, because you want your home to serve YOU and not the other way around.

If you have the notebook on you all the time then if you feel the temptation to waste time (and you know in your heart that you will regret the waste), then write the temptation down as an item: "read a bit of that trashy novel", "surf the net". Often the act of writing it down is all you need to move on from the temptation. And you know that you'll have the option to do it once you reach that page.

Hi David,
You always give such good advice! My book is 5x8.5. I like the idea of a small book that can be carried with me constantly, but I don't think clothing manufacturer's think of practicality like that when it comes to women. Men have places for pens and papers and wallets - women just get a purse - and the ability to carry what won't fit in the man's pockets.

I've been finding that I have to take the book out of my purse as soon as I come home and on to the kitchen counter works. It has to be done the minute I get home and I'm getting into a routine with that. I start doing things while supper is cooking - the only unfortunate thing is that I occasionally overcook things because I'm absorbed in the task - I'll have to learn to start using my timer more diligently. :-) Then I do a quick check-in with my son and see what he's done while I've been gone at work - then I do a scan through the pages to see what I can cross off my list that he's done. Then I go back to my active page. The book lives on my kitchen counter on weekends as I'm in and out of there most of the time - right by the coffee pot - maybe that's why my weekend mornings are so productive!
On weekend afternoons, I go through the list, pulling off the things I need to go buy and transferring them to a small piece of paper - yes, I'm doing that out of order, but it wouldn't be as efficient as running out to buy something every time it appears on the list. I live in a bedroom community, so running out to pick something up isn't very convenient.
