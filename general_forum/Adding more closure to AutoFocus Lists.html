Mark, this is my first post in this forum and before I do so, let me thank you and everybody else on this forum to provide such a breath of insight related to the subject of time management. Your insights have been invaluable to me.

I've been using AF for a month now and wanted to comment on a slight change to the AF List that has provided a larger sense of close list to the AF system

I use AutoFocus for unscheduled tasks and daily tasks as instructed by Mark but added an additional element: I will add the starting date of each page in the top section and add the “last date” to the bottom of the page (the date the last item was added to the page). I will then decide how many days it should take me to complete the page (based on the pace of my job, I’ve found that adding 5 days from the “last date” keeps the pace of my work highly competitive. I will write that date in the top right corner of the page and it will become my commitment date. I don’t allow pages to “mature” on the same dates. I will use AF to manage my daily tasks but I will also commit to complete each page at a certain date. I am still allow to dismiss tasks or use the “little and often” principle to move forward projects.

I added the additional element to Mark’s AutoFocus to add more “closure” to each individual AF page. I was concern about missing some of the discipline DIT provided with the idea of getting a day's workload done in one day. I realize this is not 100% AutFocus but it adds that good feeling of setting goals to my workload and achieving it, something I will always be grateful for Mark’s DIT model.

Two specific thoughts come to mind on this point. Firstly, if I list everything, without grading, I am finding that my subconscious instinctively sorts the "real" urgency and importance within the amazing degree of flexibility arising from doing what I want when I want. If I have say 50 items which will take 10 hours to do and impose some sort of order on them so specifying which are completed first, then at the end of 10 hours those 50 items will be completed. If I use "pure" AF at the end of 10 hours those 50 items will still be completed. However the second process will have been a lot more enjoyable than the first (and the likelihood is that there would have been increased resistance with the first and perhaps only 49 items are completed as a result!)

The second thought will sound rather strange ........ on more occasions than I would have expected, an item that I would have prioritised but have not actioned has turned out to have different requirements by the time I reach it. There is no way my subconscious could have been aware of the factors in advance so why (and how!) did AF "get it it right"? Strange ........

I personally believe that premonitions are entirely possible.

But I think most of them are just random events. I am thinking especially about all the premonitions we have that turn out to be false alarms.
