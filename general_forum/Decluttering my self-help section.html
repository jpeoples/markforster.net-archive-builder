I am (or was - I've toned it down since AF) a voracious reader and built up an extensive collection of self-help books over the years in trying to solve my procrastination tendencies. Thanks to AF, I finally feel ready to let a lot of these books go.
Bye bye to "Your Own Worst Enemy - Breaking the Habit of Adult Under-Achievement", Maximum Achievement", David Allen's "Ready for Anything", Tony Robbins ... and lots of others.

One book that I really like though is Overcoming Procrastination by Albert Ellis. He has a very good chapter in there on behavioral methods to combat procrastination. He also has a good discussion in there on how people talk to themselves because of their procrastination. I'm still giving the book away though.

I feel kind of sad though, in looking for so many years at all the *why's*, I realize I have wasted so much time because I just didn't have the right *how*.

Isaiah Berlin wrote about the hedgehog and the fox. The fox knows many things, but the hedgehog knows one big thing.

Mark Forster's a fox. He keeps creating new systems and writing new books about them. David Allen is a hedgehog. He wrote the same book three times, but gave it three different titles.

Albert Ellis probably wrote 150 books. Just read one and you've pretty much read the other 149. But it was a VERY good book.

I view AF as a very Ellisian (?) method. He might not have mentioned it in _Overcoming Procrastination_ but it fits right in with his methodology.
