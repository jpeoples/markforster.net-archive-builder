Surfing the internet and being productive are not mutually exclusive. I use it all the time for work, education, and help with productivity systems (e.g. this site!). If you're feeling guilty about surfing, you're admitting that the sites you're visiting are not work-related. So perhaps it would be better to track blocks of solid work time. Two methods for doing this are the Pomodoro technique - http://www.pomodorotechnique.com - and the Unschedule - http://www.neilfiore.com/nowhabit-schedule.shtml. Both of these involve tracking timed work blocks - 25 to 30 minutes - and rewarding yourself with shorter breaks. You can use your reward times for non-work surfing, and not feel guilty about it!

I canceled my home internet service for that reason. Of course I still have an Phone..., and work...

"Get Everything Done" does not and cannot possibly mean "Read everything on the internet". I used to worry about missing out on stuff, but now there's more stuff than I could possibly ever see to worry about missing it. Everything out there on the web is interesting, but so what? The Read It Later app is helpful for this. If I see something interesting, I click the Read it Later button, and stop the surfing. Later, I have a list of things to read.

I have a group of bookmarks called Daily. The idea is I open the group once per day, and only once per day. This includes low-priority email addresses.
