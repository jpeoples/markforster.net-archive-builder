B) Only Stuff I might do soon
C) Only Stuff I expect to do soon

A. But with a qualification. If there is something I want to do but I know I won't get to it for a long time, then it goes further through the notebook to turn up when my pages fill to that far. For instance, I need to book an appointment to get my eyes checked, but I can't do that till I have a little money saved (ditto the dentist), so booking that appointment is on a page I will hopefully reach once another of my current 'tasks' - paying down some debt - will be achieved.

Reply by Trementilla

2011-03-9 8:47
I use it as a list of "wish for", "maybe", "don't forget". Yes, I know that's not the way it's supposed to be used.

Usually I have not many projects going on at once, but they are really big and I work on them for many months and only do just them (e.g. writing academic books which need lots of research before and lots of time spent writing at daily). My really big projects hardly ever go on the lists. It tires me having to keep rewriting them on and on for months.

But I imagine my case is quite unusual. I have one very clear, very fixed priority at a time, all the rest goes on the list, therefore it doesn't really matter much whether what's on the list gets done or not in the end. I keep it mostly for not-work related stuff.

Reply by malisa

2011-03-9 13:46
A) with jens' qualifications. Everything that I think of to do/buy/think more about/research etc. that I will do at some other time than 'right now'.

I realized yesterday morning that I've always tended to think in lists. I find myself 'chanting' them in my head and deleting items as I finish. I think this becomes more important as I get older. ;) I also do best at remembering things when I associate like items.
Going to the kitchen: refill coffee, fill dogs' water bowl, take vitamin.
Going to the other half of the house: grab hat/jacket, put on makeup, brush teeth, put on jewelry.

I realized yesterday that these are little, internal, mini-C2s that get cycled through (hopefully) almost automatically.
