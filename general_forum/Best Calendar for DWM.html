Mark:

Do as Lillian recommends and date the pages yourself.

It's only a hassle the first time as you can stop dating one month, or 28 days from now as I do. First thing each day you just enter the next date, the rest of the notebook can stay blank or used fpr other purposes.

Another advantage of this is that if you miss some days due to travel, illness whatever, and don't manage to enter or process DWM for a few days, you can just start the next page by dating it one month or 28 days from your restart date..

2010-05-9 21:09
Since my work and home books are separate, I put Fri, Sat, and Sunday's dates on the same page in my book for the office. It works since the Week tab will never fall on a Sat or Sun and it cuts down on the number of pages. It also means that I am closing out one page per day, everyday, and not 3 every time a weekend comes up.

If there is a Monday holiday, I try to remember to put it on the same weekend page just to keep those days all together. It also helps to put into perspective that there is a short week coming up.
