a/ Do work as it shows up (without putting it on the list, but put follow-ups on the list)
b/ process predefinded stuff (from your list)
c/ process incoming stuff (inbox - putting on it on your list or do it immediately)

You can go either way. I'm not sure you explained yourself well enough to give a great answer, but I'm sure an answer can be found with SuperFocus. Try this:

Have some dedicated times when you don't follow the list, and times (more than now) when you DO follow it. Get the habit of choosing among the options presented. This still allows freedom to choose what's suits you for now,and control over how long you work each item.
If you want to do something other than what is listed, aim to turn the page (following thre rules) or write it in as urgent. Only for these "urgent" items, try working a while, rewrite as unfinshed, do a bit of other stuff, turn the page, and resume your chosen activity.

Doing this balances what you want to do now with all the stuff you should get to eventually. You retain creative control, same as a painter who chooses to use only pastels is still in artistic control.

I use the list for anything I consider NOT play or food. That includes work, school (when I was in it), personal tasks/errands (put keyboard on ebay, etc.).

So for yours, I would put homework, projects, housework on the list. Maybe hobbies.

Hobbies is your call, if you love your hobbies and use that as a way to relax from your other work, then no need to have it on the list (again, my opinion). But if there is any psychological resistance to certain hobby tasks, or some tasks get stuck undone for a while, then you can use the list to get them done. I put most of my hobbies on the list as they often have little tasks that I somehow resist for some reason and am thus frustrated that the big picture is not moving forward.
