It's very interesting. Mark's systems are designed to have minimal rules: as simple as possible. This is a very good thing because it makes them work extremely smoothly once you get the hang of it.

However, the rules are generally missing necessary background commentary. People get confused if they don't have a grasp on some meta concepts, such as:
* when is it a good idea to work on something?
* when is it better to move on?
* how long should I work on a thing?
* why are we writing things on today+30 and today+7?
* how do I stay on top of my urgent items?

There's more. The above questions are not answerable by rules. But understanding them is key to understanding how and why the system works, and in some cases making the system work in practice.

I only need DWM's 4 rules to operate the system. But the system relies on my intution to make decisions. And my intuition works better if I understand the principles (answers to the above) behind the system. Which is to say, if DWM becomes an addition to the DIT book, the rules fit on page 1, but DWM deserves a full chapter to explain the rules.

Roger J, you asked if a real due/deadline date is after expiration date, is there a danger that a task becomes written off before the task is due and before it gets done. Of course there is that danger: if the task is due 2 months from now, and is entered at the +1-month date and so expires in one month, you might be tempted not to take action too soon, because the rules of re-entry at +7 would mean if you didn't take more action in the next 7 days it would expire, and you might prefer to wait and not take any action till just before the expiry date and then re-enter it at the +7 days, which would still be well before the actual deadline.

However, that danger can work to your advantage: if you work on a task "little and often" (or "large and often"), and keep re-entering it at the +7 expiry date, you're well ahead of the game, instead of running at the last minute to meet a deadline.

Roger J:

You might be overlooking the benefits of the tickler/43 folders and the project page to complement DWM. Here's what I do.

If I have a task with a due date more than a month in the future I do one of two things depending on how much thinking/work is required or how intensive it is:
1. Simple - put a reminder to put it on my list in my tickler/43 folder system so that it comes out one month ahead of its due date. That way I don't run the risk of a task expiring on DWM before its due date.
2. Intensive - break it down so that the interim steps get done without putting the final deliverable in danger of early expiry

Here's a worked example for a simple task:
Today is 23rd Februrary
New task: deliver report by 23rd April (If I put it onto my list now it gets entered on 23rd March which means that it might expire before it is due. As I know this is a simple report which will only take me an hour or so e.g. just a matter of filling in a simple one page form. I can stick to a single task entry.)
Put note in tickler: Enter 'deliver report (23rd Apr)' on list, file in 23rd March folder
Time passes...
Today is 23rd March
Get everything out of 23rd March tickler folder, see note and
Put task on list: 'deliver report (23rd Apr', entered one month out on 23rd April

Here's a worked example for a more intensive task:
Today is 23rd Februrary
New task: deliver report by 23rd April (I already know that this is going to need several 'hits' e.g. outline, three or four drafts, peer review, final edit so I break it down in this way. I have a project with seven interim steps/deliverables and eight weeks before the final due date. I'll give each a week which will leave one week contingency at the end.)
Create project: I write the interim steps on a new project page with all the due dates and give the project a name 'Report A'
Put task on list: 'Work on Report A (2nd March)', entered one month out on 23rd March
Today is 27th February
Complete 'Work on Report A (2nd March)' and cross it out
Check project page for next due date
Put task on list: 'Work on Report A (9th March)', entered one month out on 27th March
and so on...

There's a good chance I'll complete the interim steps ahead of time. If I leave each step until its due date I'll get it all done on time. If I ignore a step until it approaches expiry I know I'll feel a lot of pressure so I've given myself the best chance of getting it done.

Hope this helps.
