2009-09-6 13:02
The way AF4 deals better with urgent stuff than AF1 is that in AF1, you would have to do something on EVERY page to prevent the entire page from being dismissed, before you could reach any urgent items near the end of the list. With AF4, you only need to action ONE item from the closed list to prevent everything being dismissed, before moving on to the open list where your latest urgent items are. Reading through the entire closed and open lists before reaching any urgent items near the end of the list helps me decide if there's really nothing hidden in either list that has become more urgent than the newer urgent tasks at the end.

I could imagine that if your work requires you to react to emergencies right away (like in NOW instead of within a few minutes) AF4 wouldn't work as well as AF2 or RAF. For my work, I've noticed that AF2/RAF allows me to react to semi-emergencies much too quick, at the expense of more important stuff. So for me, I think AF4 is going to work very well. The only resistance I have in leaving AF2/RAF is that it just feels good to respond to emergencies, even when at the expense of more important (and often a bit more boring) older tasks.

Oh, and the other concern I have is the same as yours, Walter, dealing with Home and Work stuff. I might go back to the dual mode list (work stuff from the top, home stuff from the bottom) that I used in AF1, but I'll start with one integrated list first, to see how that works.
