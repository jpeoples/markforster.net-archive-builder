Autofocus without the List?

Author: Mark Forster | Created: 2009-02-12 10:58 | Last Activity: 2009-02-14 19:22

Original Post

2009-02-12 10:58
Having achieved a record 5,322 individual visitors on this site yesterday (and even more so far today), I should be happy.

But no!

I have been stung by a couple of people commenting that at the end of the day, Autofocus is a to do list. To my mind that's a bit like saying that a Rolls-Royce is an engine.

It's certainly true that a Rolls wouldn't go very far without it's engine. But is Autofocus dependant on its list?

My hypothesis is "No, it's not". So I am now going to experiment with running AF without using the list. At all. I'm not even going to look at it.

Having been using AF for a couple of months now, I feel that it's had a very definite training effect on my mind. I now want to see what that training can achieve on its own.

I strongly counsel you not to try this at home (and especially not at work!). Therefore I'm not going to share the exact method I'm going to use - at least not until I've proved to myself that it works.

Whether it will work or not I have not the faintest idea. But you will probably be able to tell by the quantity and quality of work you see emanating from me on this website, discussion forum and blog.

Mark

I'm finding that some items sort of wander off my lists on their own but then periodically return. I found this initially with routine tasks, that whereas I had started by listing them, once I had gotten into a pattern, or AF had prompted me to change the way in which I did them for some reason, it just seemed unnecessary or inappropriate to return them to my lists. However, every so often it feels the right things to do to put them back for a while.

I am now starting to find that a lot of my project tasks are "wandering off" as well. Initially I would do something on a project and readd it to the end of my list, but I have been so prompted to overhaul my project management system that virtually all of my projects have temporarily placed themselves within my project management system awaiting review. I have regular tasks to review my system, which is picking up anything that has any degree of urgency, and any project related individual tasks are still within my lists. I have no doubt that those projects will return to the lists in due course when the time is right, and I am totally confident that nothing is being missed, but at the moment they are not in my list as such, although are indirectly through my "review project management" task.

AF is becoming in a way the glue that holds everything together - but without it - no way! To me my list IS the point of focus!
