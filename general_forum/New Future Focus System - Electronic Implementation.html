Ok - I'll bite. At first I read it and thought - ick! Then I decided I'd try it. I don't have any planners on hand since I use gcal on my iPhone, so I decided I'd test with an electronic tool for a few days.

I picked Toodledo since I already had an account and the iPhone app. I suppose any decent task manager with dates would work. I took my current paper list of 47 remaining tasks and entered them into toodledo and post-dated them by 30 days from the date I had listed in my paper version (which worked perfect since my oldest task was only 3 weeks old).

Here's what I like so far...

1. I get an instant closed list for the current day - stuff that HAS to be done today so I know exactly where to start. :)

2. I get a sort of prioritized list...items currently in progress are at the top of my list when sorted by due date.

3. Finally a way to implement tasks that repeat on a specific day...and with toodledo I add a "start date" so these tasks are hidden until they day they are due which keeps the list uncluttered. This feature saves me the worry of wondering if I re-entered the item or not. :D

And the bonus of adding this to my phone is kind of a big deal for me. I'm on the go so much right now that having a portable list is really helpful. I like being able to quickly add stuff, pre-date it and move on. I'm getting a lot done and feeling like I get the important things done for the day. I do like the idea of feeling like you're clearing future lists. That feels good. Feels less like I am behind on life and more like I'm in control.

Just my two cents.
