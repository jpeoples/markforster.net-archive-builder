2009-01-31 15:15
I have been struggling with a backlog at work combined with major home improvements and paper piles everywhere (not to mention dust and disarray - no washine machine, cooker or sink for a week) I was growing more anxious and depressed about my growing AF list. With little discretionary time I was getting more and more overwhelmed about what I should be focusing on.

Yesterday I got back my copy of DIT which I had loaned to a colleague at work months ago! I decided to give it a quick re-read last night to remind myself of how DIT was different to AF. I realised that I really liked the idea of the task diary, especially to schedule tasks to a specific date in the future if they cannot be actioned right now. I also like the certainty of writing out each day my tasks for tomorrow, which provides the structure I seem to need to get going each day.

But I also love the way that I can throw anything into Autofocus, large or small; quick notes to self that would otherwise be forgotten till too late, or missed altogether.

Anyway, having an unexpected day to myself, I have been tackling tasks in both methods. I have crossed off tasks from AF if they were best actioned (or could only be done) at a future date and written them in my task diary (an unused paper calendar) I had knocking around.... At the same time, I have been working on tasks that need to be done today (on an urgent/same day basis). I've also taken all routine tasks out of AF and scheduled them, as I found they were just were not getting done under AF.

My AF list is heavily reduced as is my anxiety level. Psychologically, I feel better knowing that my tasks are spread out over a couple of weeks, and that I don't have to decide or intuit whether 'doing the laundry' is standing out for me today. So now I can't work out whether to stick with DIT, Aotufocus or try and work some combined version of the two!

2009-01-31 23:07
Linda:

I note that you said in your first post above that you have little discretionary time. The fact is that no task management system is going to work if you have no discretionary time to do the tasks. DIT requires discretionary time just like AF does.

I would advise you to see if you can increase the amount of discretionary time you have by looking carefully at the non-discretionary work you have. Is it all really necessary?

It would also be a good idea to see if you really need as many tasks requiring discretionary time.

However you manage it, you have got to match the tasks needing discretionary time with the discretionary time you have available. If you don't, you will always have backlogs regardless of what system you use.

2009-02-3 22:21
My 2-cents on DIT vs AF: Basically I always thought that DIT was a great idea in principle, but totally impractical for managing the kind of work I have. Basically I disagreed with the main premise that 'one days incoming work must equal one days outgoing work' (or however it is stated). Yes, it must be true in the long-run, but as Keynes said: 'in the long run we're all dead' (of overwork!). Basically in my kind of job (and I don't think I'm a special case or anything) there is no reason why I wouldn't have 10 days worth of work land on my desk on one day, none at all on the next, and basically no discretionary time to do anything about it on day 3, etc. So although DIT could cope with this, it never felt like I was doing it 'properly', and I had a semi-permanent backlog.

AF seems to be about the best way to deal with such a widely varying workload. Yes, there is not the same sense of 'completion' at the end of the day, but then I never got that anyway with DIT. I do get a sense of completion when I finish the day knowing I've worked efficiently throughout.

How do I know when I've finished the day with AF? - I finish when I feel like it!

Mark's comment above - 'you have got to match the tasks needing discretionary time with the discretionary time you have available' got me thinking. I'm now operating on a similar principle with my DIT/AF hybrid. I use a Palm PDA with the Datebk6 calendar. The calendar lists my committments - meetings, classes, appointments as well as day-specific tasks (identified from my AF list).

In the Tasks app, I have a checklist of the recurring daily and weekly maintenance-type tasks that keep life ticking over, such as cleaning, email, deal with post etc. (this is the same as DIT's daily will-do closed list ). Those three - calendar events, day-specific tasks and daily checklist are my basic minimum essentials and sit outside my AF system.

Now I feel I can use AF in a more planned way. My AF list holds everything else that comes along in life's random way. I can throw all tasks, projects, reminders at it. knowing that I will get to work on them when I have the time to do so. Thanks, Mark!
