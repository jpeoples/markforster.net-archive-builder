Just two FVP lists.

My personal list is on a tablet running Cloud Outliner. I take it everywhere. Add, move, select and delete are all onscreen buttons so it's very smooth. One item in my list is a tickler outline that I reference daily to see if anything else needs to be pulled up to the main.

My work list is in OneNote, following my old 1 tab per task scheme. It's easy because all tabs are always visible, Ctrl-q highlights, Ctrl-n makes new, Alt-m-enter to move to end, Ctrl-shift-a-del to delete, Ctrl-pageUp/Down to scan. I also keep a work log page where i type notes as i work.

- I use Outlook for work-related, time-specific items. I didn't choose it, but it's adequate.
- Daily planner (paper) for personal, time-specific items.
- Occasional undated Planner Pad pages for particularly complicated weeks.
- Large (38 lines per page) spiral notebook for work items. It's a cheap notebook because it doesn't have to travel. I don't need to write down most of my tasks, so I'm still on the first page after two weeks. I process it like AF4, or maybe more like DIT: new items are usually for Tomorrow.
- Smaller, sturdier notebook (22 lines per page) for personal tasks. I waver between AF and FVP, depending on whether or not it's possible to act on a particular page at the moment. (I've got an issue with a particular category of context-sensitive tasks, but the issue will get worse if I spin those tasks off to a third folder.)
- Nascent "17 Folders" tickler system: one folder for each month, and five more for various other dated, semi-dated, and dateless items. My previous "system" was to leave these things in a messy heap on top of my desk where I could be reminded of them all the time. That was more effective than it sounds, but it interfered with the proper use of my desk.
- "Project plans" in various formats, most of them very simple (less than one page), slowly finding their way into my 17 Folders.
